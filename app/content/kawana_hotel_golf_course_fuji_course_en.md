Kawana Hotel’s **Fuji Course** in Ito, Shizuoka, is a historic oceanside layout often searched with “Kawana hotel golf” and “Kawana hotel review.” It is a prestige Izu Peninsula day—clifftop views, classic strategy, and hotel-centric stays—not a cheap municipal loop.

## Search intent note

Many queries mix **hotel review** and **golf booking**. If you only need lodging, prioritize hotel inventory. If you need a tee time, confirm whether the rate is:

1. Hotel guest golf package
2. Outside day visitor product
3. Member / introduction only for that date

Do not assume every GORA-style date is open to walk-up visitors.

## How the Fuji Course plays

Expect elevation changes, ocean wind, and greens that reward patience. Visual intimidation off elevated tees is part of the test—club for the wind you feel on the tee, not the postcard. Misses toward cliffs and deep bunkers are expensive; center-of-green is the default miss.

## Green fees & booking

Prestige Izu packages sit above average resort pricing. Day rates and stay-and-play bundles differ a lot; plan for **premium ¥20,000s+ territory** on busy dates when visitor slots exist. Start with [booking search](/booking/kawana_hotel_golf_course_fuji_course_en) and also check Kawana Hotel stay packages if golf-only inventory is thin.

## Access from Tokyo

- Train to **Ito** + taxi / hotel transfer, or drive the peninsula (traffic on weekends)
- From central Tokyo, door-to-door is often **2.5–3.5+ hours**—do not cut it fine for an early tee
- Overnight at Kawana (or Ito) is the stress-free plan

## Dress & tips

- Upscale resort dress standards; soft spikes; no denim
- Camera OK for views—keep pace of play
- Related: [Mt. Fuji view golf](/guide/mt-fuji-view-golf) · [Onsen after golf](/guide/onsen-after-golf)

## Bottom line

Kawana Fuji is for travelers who want **Izu prestige golf + hotel**, not the cheapest green fee near Tokyo. Align your booking path (hotel package vs day rate) before you chase a bare tee sheet—and leave generous travel time from Tokyo.
