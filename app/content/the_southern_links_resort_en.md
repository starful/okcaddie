## Course Overview

The Southern Links Resort, opened in 1988, stands as a premier golf destination on Okinawa's southern coast, famously designed by Pete Dye, Jr. This 18-hole, par-72 layout stretches approximately 6,800 to 7,050 yards from the back tees, offering a significant challenge characterized by its dramatic oceanfront setting. The course integrates strategic bunkering and severe elevation changes into its design, typical of Dye's innovative approach. Fairways and tees are generally covered in Zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface, while the greens are typically Bentgrass, maintained for brisk speeds and subtle undulations. The overall character blends traditional links elements with a tropical resort ambiance, providing both scenic beauty and rigorous golf.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating The Southern Links requires careful planning, with several holes demanding precise execution. The 8th hole, a par-3 ranging from 160 to 200 yards, is an iconic ocean-carry that plays entirely over the Pacific to a green perched on a cliff edge, requiring unwavering commitment and accurate club selection to avoid the unforgiving waves below. Following this, the 9th, a challenging par-4 measuring 420-460 yards, features a tee shot that must thread a narrow landing area bordered by thick rough on one side and a steep drop-off to the ocean on the other, demanding a fade for right-handers to find the ideal position before an uphill approach to a multi-tiered green. Later in the round, the 14th hole, a strategic par-5 stretching 530-570 yards, offers a risk-reward opportunity with a dogleg left around a significant water hazard, tempting longer hitters to cut the corner while demanding a precise lay-up for those playing it safe, culminating in an approach to a green well-guarded by bunkers. The finishing 18th hole, a stout par-4 of 440-470 yards, requires a precise tee shot to a fairway that narrows before ascending to an elevated green, making a long iron or hybrid approach shot crucial to finish strong and navigate the prevailing crosswinds that often sweep across this exposed hole.

## Conditions & Seasonality

The best times to experience The Southern Links are typically during the milder months of spring (March to May) and autumn (October to November), when temperatures are comfortable and rainfall is less frequent. Okinawa's seaside location means wind is a constant factor, often influencing club selection significantly, and sudden rain squalls can occur even outside the rainy season (May-June) or typhoon season (July-September). Pace of play is generally well-managed, particularly with caddies, but expect rounds to extend to four and a half hours during peak times. Weekday mornings usually offer the least crowded conditions, while weekends and public holidays see a considerable increase in resort guests and local golfers, leading to slightly slower play.

## Green Fees & Booking

Green fees at The Southern Links reflect its premium resort status, generally ranging from ¥18,000 to ¥25,000 on weekdays, and increasing to ¥25,000 to ¥35,000 on weekends and holidays. These fees typically include a shared golf cart, which is mandatory for all players. While visitors are welcome year-round, securing a caddie (highly recommended for first-time players) usually incurs an additional fee of ¥10,000 to ¥15,000 per bag. Booking well in advance is advisable, especially during peak seasons, and can be conveniently done through major Japanese golf booking portals like Rakuten GORA or GDO, or directly via the resort's official website. These platforms often provide various package deals that may include lunch or specific time slots.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

The Southern Links maintains a relatively standard yet respectful golf dress code consistent with most premium Japanese clubs. Collared shirts or mock necks are required, paired with golf-specific trousers or tailored shorts; denim jeans, t-shirts, and tracksuits are not permitted on the course or in the clubhouse. Soft spikes are mandatory for golf shoes. On the course, mobile phone use is generally discouraged for conversations, though silent use for GPS or photography is tolerated in moderation. Maintaining an appropriate pace of play is a key expectation; groups are encouraged to keep up with the players ahead to ensure a smooth flow, with caddies playing a vital role in upholding this standard.

## Access

The Southern Links Resort is situated on the southern tip of Okinawa Island, making Naha Airport (OKA) the primary point of entry for visitors. From Naha Airport, the drive to the resort in Yaese typically takes approximately 30 to 45 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. Given Okinawa's island geography, direct drive times from mainland cities like Osaka, Kobe, Hiroshima, or Tokyo are not applicable. Car rental is the most practical and recommended method of transportation around Okinawa for golfers, offering flexibility to explore beyond the course. Ample, complimentary parking is available on-site at The Southern Links Resort, accommodating both rental vehicles and tour buses.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at The Southern Links offers a comfortable and well-appointed experience, featuring spacious locker rooms equipped with individual changing areas and full amenities. A large, impeccably clean public bath (daiyokujo) provides a relaxing post-round soak, complete with showering facilities and toiletries. The resort's main restaurant provides panoramic views of the ocean and the golf course, creating an ideal setting for a meal. The menu showcases a blend of traditional Okinawan specialties, such as Goya Champuru and Okinawa Soba, alongside classic Japanese dishes and some Western options. It's an excellent spot to reflect on your round while savoring local flavors, often featuring fresh seafood sourced from the surrounding waters.

## Caddie Tips

Playing The Southern Links with a caddie is a significant advantage, particularly for first-time visitors navigating the course's unique challenges. A common mistake golfers make here is misjudging the strength and direction of the ocean wind, leading to incorrect club selection and shots that drift significantly off target. Local caddies provide crucial insight into these unpredictable gusts, advising on appropriate carries over hazards and offering precise reads on the subtle breaks of the Bentgrass greens. This course genuinely suits mid-to-low handicap players who appreciate strategic design, are confident in their ball-striking, and can adapt to varying wind conditions. Higher handicappers will find the dramatic scenery alone worthwhile, but should manage expectations regarding score and be prepared for a rigorous test of golf.
