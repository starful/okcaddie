## Who it's for

- Travelers based in **Fukuoka / Hakata** who want coast golf without a long drive to southern Kyushu
- Groups that need **bookable tee times** (not member-intro only)
- Mid-handicappers who enjoy elevation and wind more than flat resort fairways

If you only want a pure links walking test, look north (e.g. Tohoku/Hokkaido links). Keya is scenic coastal golf with Japanese cart-day structure.

## Course overview

Expect tree-lined corridors opening to **Genkai Sea** views on several holes. Wind off the water changes club selection; play to the middle of greens when the breeze is up. Fairway bunkers and greenside sand punish greedy lines more than sheer length. Bent greens are usually smoother than pure Korai courses—pace control matters.

Typical Japanese pace: cart play, halfway house or clubhouse lunch, baths after the round. Confirm caddie vs self-play options when you book; they vary by product and day.

## Green fees & booking

Fees move with weekday/weekend, season, and whether lunch or caddie is included. As a **planning range** for visitors:

- **Weekday packages**: often roughly mid-¥10,000s to low-¥20,000s per person
- **Weekend / holiday**: higher; peak cherry-blossom and Golden Week dates fill early

## Access

- **Airport**: Fukuoka (FUK) → roughly **45–70 minutes** by car to Itoshima depending on traffic
- **Best option**: rental car or pre-booked transfer; golf bags on crowded city buses are awkward
- **From Hakata**: west along the coast / Route 202 corridor—build buffer for morning tees

Combine with Itoshima beaches or seafood lunch only if your tee time allows; don’t stack a dawn round after a late FUK arrival.

## Dress code & tips

- Soft spikes only
- Soft shell or wind layer even in shoulder seasons—coast breeze cools fast
