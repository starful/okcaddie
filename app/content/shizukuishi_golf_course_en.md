**Introduction:**

Shizukuishi Golf Course is more than just a place to play golf; it's an immersive experience steeped in the traditions of Japanese hospitality and a deep respect for the natural environment. Located in the picturesque Iwate Prefecture, known for its stunning landscapes and serene atmosphere, Shizukuishi offers a compelling alternative to the bustling urban courses of Japan. The course has earned a reputation for its challenging yet fair layout, its impeccable maintenance, and its dedication to providing a premium experience at a reasonable price point. Golfers who seek a genuine connection with nature, combined with a rewarding test of their skills, will find Shizukuishi an exceptional destination. The club strives to provide a welcoming atmosphere, with a focus on customer satisfaction.

**Price & Booking:**

Green fees at Shizukuishi Golf Course are moderately priced, making it an attractive option for both local residents and visiting golfers. Weekday rates typically range from ¥8,000 to ¥12,000 JPY, while weekend and holiday rates can climb to ¥12,000 to ¥18,000 JPY. These prices usually include green fees, caddie fees (mandatory in most cases), and consumption tax. Cart fees are typically extra, costing around ¥3,000 to ¥4,000 JPY per player. Seasonal discounts and packages are often available, particularly during the shoulder seasons (spring and autumn). It's advisable to check the official website or contact the course directly for the most up-to-date pricing information.

Booking a tee time at Shizukuishi can be done through several channels. The official website allows for direct online booking, although the site is often only available in Japanese. Major Japanese golf booking platforms, such as GDO (Golf Digest Online) and Rakuten GORA, also offer tee times and packages. For international visitors, it's recommended to use a concierge service or a Japanese-speaking friend to assist with the booking process, as English support may be limited. Booking well in advance, especially for weekend play, is highly recommended, particularly during peak seasons.

**Course Accessibility:**

Shizukuishi Golf Course operates as a semi-private facility. While membership options are available, the course is also open to the public, allowing non-members to enjoy the facilities. However, access for non-members may be restricted during peak times or special events, so it's always best to check availability in advance. The semi-private nature of the course ensures a high level of maintenance and service, while still providing opportunities for casual golfers to experience its unique charm. Securing a tee time through a member is occasionally possible, which can often provide a more favorable rate, however this is not generally possible unless there is a prior relationship.

**Course Highlights:**

The course at Shizukuishi is a meticulously designed 18-hole layout that blends seamlessly with the surrounding landscape. While the specific architect may be less widely known than some of the famous names in golf course design, the course itself speaks volumes about the skill and vision that went into its creation. The course is characterized by its strategic bunkering, undulating fairways, and well-protected greens. Water hazards come into play on several holes, adding to the challenge and the visual appeal of the course.

One of the signature holes is the par-3 17th, which requires a precise tee shot over a picturesque pond to a well-guarded green. The wind can often be a factor on this hole, making club selection crucial. Another memorable hole is the par-5 9th, a sweeping dogleg left that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. A well-placed drive is essential to set up an opportunity to reach the green in two, but golfers must be wary of the strategically placed bunkers that guard the approach.

The course is known for its immaculate condition, with perfectly manicured fairways and greens that run true and fast. The greens are generally small to medium in size, demanding accuracy with approach shots. The bunkering is both aesthetically pleasing and strategically challenging, requiring golfers to think carefully about their shot selection and course management.

**Facilities:**

The clubhouse at Shizukuishi Golf Course is a haven of comfort and relaxation, designed to provide golfers with a seamless and enjoyable experience. The pro shop offers a wide selection of golf equipment, apparel, and accessories from leading brands. Knowledgeable staff are on hand to provide assistance and advice.

The restaurant at Shizukuishi serves a variety of Japanese and Western dishes, with a focus on fresh, local ingredients. The menu changes seasonally to reflect the availability of the freshest produce. The restaurant offers stunning views of the course and the surrounding mountains, providing a perfect setting for a post-round meal or a casual drink.

However, the highlight of the facilities at Shizukuishi is undoubtedly the onsen (hot spring bath). After a challenging round of golf, there's nothing quite like soaking in the soothing waters of the onsen. The onsen features separate baths for men and women, both of which are meticulously maintained and offer a range of therapeutic benefits. The water is sourced from a natural hot spring and is rich in minerals that are said to be beneficial for the skin and muscles. The onsen is a truly authentic Japanese experience and a must-do for any visitor to Shizukuishi. The bathing facilities are incredibly clean and well-maintained, rivaling those of any high-end resort.

**Scenic Beauty:**

Shizukuishi Golf Course is blessed with a stunning natural setting. The course is surrounded by rolling hills, lush forests, and towering mountains, creating a sense of tranquility and serenity. The best time to visit Shizukuishi is during the spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November), when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its most vibrant. In spring, the cherry blossoms add a touch of magic to the landscape, while in autumn, the leaves turn a brilliant array of red, orange, and gold. Even during the summer months, the course remains relatively cool and comfortable due to its elevation.

The views from the course are simply breathtaking. On a clear day, you can see the majestic Mount Iwate, the highest peak in Iwate Prefecture, dominating the skyline. The course is also home to a variety of wildlife, including birds, deer, and rabbits, adding to the natural charm of the area. Shizukuishi offers a truly immersive experience in nature, allowing golfers to escape the stresses of everyday life and reconnect with the beauty of the natural world. The air is clean and crisp, and the atmosphere is one of peace and tranquility.

**Access:**

Shizukuishi Golf Course is conveniently located within easy reach of major transportation hubs. The nearest major city is Morioka, the capital of Iwate Prefecture. From Morioka Station, you can take a local train to Shizukuishi Station, which is approximately a 15-minute taxi ride from the golf course. Alternatively, you can rent a car at Morioka Station and drive to the course in about 30 minutes. The drive is scenic and well-signposted.

For international visitors, the best way to reach Morioka is by taking the JR Tohoku Shinkansen (bullet train) from Tokyo Station. The journey takes approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes. Morioka Airport also offers domestic flights from major Japanese cities, but the train is generally the more convenient option. Shizukuishi Golf Course also offers shuttle service upon request; however, it is advisable to book in advance to secure one.
