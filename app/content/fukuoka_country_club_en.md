**Course Highlights:**

The Fukuoka Country Club boasts two distinct courses: the Wajiro Course and the Rakuen Course. The Wajiro Course is generally considered the more challenging of the two, featuring strategically placed bunkers, undulating greens, and water hazards that demand precision and careful course management. The Rakuen Course, while slightly shorter, offers its own set of challenges, with tighter fairways and more emphasis on accuracy.

While the architect is less prominently celebrated than some of the more internationally renowned course designers, the layouts showcase a deep understanding of the natural terrain and a commitment to creating a strategic and enjoyable golfing experience. The fairways are typically well-maintained and the greens are fast and true. The bunkering is particularly noteworthy, with strategically placed hazards that punish errant shots and reward thoughtful club selection.

Signature holes include a par-3 on the Wajiro Course that plays over a water hazard to a well-guarded green, demanding a precise tee shot. Also, a par-4 on the Rakuen Course that requires a well-placed drive to avoid fairway bunkers, followed by an accurate approach shot to a small, undulating green. The club regularly hosts local amateur tournaments, adding a competitive edge to the golfing experience.
