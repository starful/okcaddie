**Price & Booking:**

Playing Oak Village Golf Club comes with a price tag that reflects its premium status. Weekday green fees typically range from ¥35,000 to ¥50,000 (approximately $250-$350 USD), while weekend and holiday fees can climb to ¥50,000 to ¥70,000 (approximately $350-$500 USD) or more, depending on the season and any special promotions. These fees generally include green fees, caddie fees (highly recommended and often required), and consumption tax. Cart fees are usually an additional charge.

Booking a tee time requires careful planning. Given its popularity and exclusivity, securing a reservation can be challenging, especially during peak seasons like spring (cherry blossom season) and autumn (foliage season). Advance booking, often several weeks or even months ahead, is strongly recommended. The most reliable method is through the official Oak Village Golf Club website, although it may require Japanese language proficiency or the use of a translation tool. Alternatively, luxury golf concierge services, high-end travel agencies specializing in golf tours, or connections through members are viable options.

Seasonal tips: Spring and autumn offer the most pleasant weather and stunning scenery, but expect higher demand and premium pricing. Summer can be hot and humid, but offers lower rates and fewer crowds. Winter can be cold, but the course is often still playable and provides a unique golfing experience. Check for special packages or stay-and-play deals, which can offer better value.

**Course Accessibility:**

Oak Village Golf Club operates as a **Semi-Private** club. While membership provides priority access and exclusive benefits, the club also welcomes non-member guests, albeit with certain restrictions and higher fees. Tee times for non-members are generally more limited and subject to availability. Access is typically granted through introductions from existing members, partnerships with affiliated hotels or resorts, or booking through specialized golf tour operators. While technically accessible to the public, securing a tee time without a connection requires significant effort and potentially a willingness to pay a premium. Be prepared to provide information about your golfing experience and handicap when making inquiries.

**Course Highlights:**

The strategic layout of Oak Village is its defining characteristic, a testament to the architect's vision and expertise in seamlessly integrating the course with the natural terrain. While the architect is not widely publicized (adding to the air of exclusivity), the design philosophy emphasizes risk-reward opportunities, demanding thoughtful shot selection and precise execution. The course plays to a challenging par of 72, with multiple tee boxes to accommodate golfers of all skill levels.

Several holes stand out as signature challenges. The par-3 7th hole, often played over a picturesque ravine, requires accurate club selection and nerves of steel. The par-5 14th, a demanding dogleg that winds through towering pines, presents a strategic test of length and accuracy. And the closing par-4 18th, with its undulating fairway and well-guarded green, demands a strong finish to secure a satisfying round.

Expect undulating fairways, strategically placed bunkers, and challenging water hazards that add to the strategic complexity of the course. The greens are meticulously maintained, known for their smooth surfaces and true rolls. The overall design philosophy prioritizes strategic play over brute force, rewarding golfers who can think their way around the course. Each hole offers a unique challenge and aesthetic appeal, making for a memorable and engaging golfing experience.

**Access:**

Reaching Oak Village Golf Club requires some planning due to its secluded location. The most convenient option is to rent a car, providing flexibility and ease of access. From central Tokyo, the drive typically takes around 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on traffic conditions. Take the Aqua Line Expressway followed by local roads. The club provides ample parking for guests.

Alternatively, you can take a train to JR Kazusa-Ichinomiya Station, the nearest major train station. From there, you will need to take a taxi to the golf club, which takes approximately 20-30 minutes. Pre-booking a taxi is recommended, especially during peak seasons. The club may also offer a shuttle service from the station, but it's best to confirm availability in advance.

Detailed driving directions and train schedules can be found on the Oak Village Golf Club website or through online navigation services. Consider traffic conditions when planning your journey, especially during rush hour.
