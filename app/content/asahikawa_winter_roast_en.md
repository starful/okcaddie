## Course Overview

Asahikawa Winter Roast, nestled in the heart of Hokkaido's second-largest city, opened its fairways in 1993, designed by local architect Shinji Ikeda. The course was conceived to offer a robust golf experience capable of extending the playing season into the shoulder months, embracing Hokkaido's distinct climate rather than shying away from it. It features a challenging 18-hole, Par 72 layout, spanning approximately 6,650 to 6,900 yards from the championship tees, with various tee boxes accommodating all skill levels down to a comfortable 6,000 yards. The course boasts well-maintained bentgrass greens that provide a true roll, complemented by manicured cool-season turf fairways and strategic rough. Overall, the course presents a classic parkland design, characterized by mature tree lines, subtle elevation changes, and thoughtfully placed bunkers, offering a strategic test of golf amidst Hokkaido's natural beauty.

## Layout & Strategy

The strategic demands of Asahikawa Winter Roast are evident across its varied holes, requiring careful planning and execution for a good score.

Hole 3, a Par 4 playing between 380 and 410 yards, features a distinct dogleg left that demands precision off the tee. A dense tree line guards the left side of the fairway, while a large bunker awaits any overcooked drive to the right, urging golfers to play a slight fade or a conservative iron to the elbow of the dogleg. The approach shot often calls for a mid-iron to a subtly sloped green that generally runs from back to front, making pin positions on the front-half particularly challenging to hold.

The picturesque 7th hole is a demanding Par 3, stretching between 170 and 190 yards, often played directly into or across the prevailing wind. Water hazards protect the front and left sides of the green, while a deep, cavernous bunker collects any shots pushed right. Club selection is paramount here; aiming for the center of the green is typically the safest play, letting the natural contours and the elements dictate the ball's final resting place, often leading to a challenging two-putt due to the green's gentle undulations.

Hole 12, a Par 5 measuring from 520 to 550 yards, presents an uphill tee shot followed by a gradual downhill approach to the green. The tee shot must carry a slight rise, with fairway bunkers strategically positioned to catch drives of approximately 250-280 yards, compelling players to either lay up short or challenge the bunkers for a better angle. Aggressive players might attempt to reach the green in two, but a well-placed bunker guarding the front-right of the green and out-of-bounds left makes a lay-up to a comfortable wedge distance a more prudent strategy for most.

The 16th hole is a formidable Par 4, stretching 420 to 450 yards, notable for its narrow driving chute and elevated green. The tee shot requires accuracy to thread between dense trees on both sides, with cross-bunkers positioned around 280 yards from the tee, demanding either a powerful, straight drive or a careful lay-up with a 3-wood or hybrid. The approach shot is often a mid-to-long iron to a small, elevated green that presents a deceptive read, often breaking more than it appears, making par a very satisfying result on this challenging penultimate hole.

## Conditions & Seasonality

Asahikawa Winter Roast offers its most agreeable playing conditions from late May through early October, avoiding the unpredictable spring thaws and the onset of winter snows. While July and August bring the warmest temperatures, the Hokkaido summer is generally mild and pleasant, rarely experiencing the oppressive humidity found in more southern regions of Japan. Wind can be a factor, particularly in spring and fall, adding an extra layer of challenge to an already strategic course. Rain showers are possible throughout the season, so bringing appropriate waterproof gear is advisable.

Pace of play here is generally excellent, reflecting Japanese golf culture where groups are meticulously scheduled, and efficient play is encouraged. Weekday rounds typically move along briskly, averaging four to four-and-a-half hours for 18 holes. Weekends, as expected, see a higher volume of golfers, but the course staff are adept at managing traffic, ensuring play remains within reasonable bounds. Early morning tee times, especially on weekends, are popular among local golfers seeking a quick round before the day heats up or gets too crowded.

## Green Fees & Booking

Green fees at Asahikawa Winter Roast offer competitive value for a well-maintained Hokkaido course. Weekday rates generally range from ¥12,000 to ¥18,000, which typically includes a cart fee. On weekends and holidays, these rates increase, ranging from ¥18,000 to ¥25,000. While the course is fully accessible to visitors, members enjoy preferential tee times and often discounted rates.

Booking a tee time is straightforward and highly recommended, especially for weekend play or during peak season. The most efficient method for international visitors is often through major Japanese golf booking portals such as Rakuten GORA or GDO, which provide English-language interfaces and real-time availability. Alternatively, bookings can be made directly via the course's official website, typically found through the general booking link provided on this platform. Caddies are usually an optional extra, costing approximately ¥4,000-¥6,000 per person when shared among a foursome, and carts are typically mandatory and included in the base fee.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Adherence to the dress code at Asahikawa Winter Roast is expected, reflecting the respectful nature of golf in Japan. Golfers are required to wear collared shirts tucked into trousers or tailored shorts. Denim jeans, t-shirts, and tracksuits are not permitted. Spikeless golf shoes are mandatory to preserve the greens and ensure safety.

On-course etiquette emphasizes maintaining a steady pace of play and respecting the course. Mobile phones should be kept on silent mode and used discreetly, away from playing areas. Players are expected to be ready to play when it's their turn, repair all pitch marks on the greens, and rake bunkers thoroughly after use. These guidelines contribute to a smooth and enjoyable experience for all golfers on the course.

## Access

Asahikawa Winter Roast is conveniently located for those traveling within Hokkaido, rather than being a short hop from major Honshu cities. The nearest significant transportation hub is Asahikawa Airport (AKJ), which is approximately a 30 to 40-minute drive from the course. For those arriving by train, JR Asahikawa Station in the city center is about a 20-minute drive away, offering connections to other parts of Hokkaido.

Travelers from outside Hokkaido, particularly from distant cities like Osaka, Kobe, Hiroshima, or Tokyo, would need to fly into either Asahikawa Airport or New Chitose Airport (CTS) near Sapporo. From New Chitose Airport, the drive to the course is considerably longer, typically taking around 2.5 to 3 hours, making a rental car the most practical option for travel throughout the region. Ample free parking is available on-site, accommodating both personal vehicles and larger tour buses.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Asahikawa Winter Roast truly lives up to its "Warm interior" designation, providing a welcoming and comfortable retreat regardless of the weather outside. The locker rooms are impeccably clean and spacious, equipped with all necessary amenities for pre- and post-round preparations. A highlight of the facility is the traditional Japanese bathhouse, or *furo*, which often features hot spring water. This communal bathing experience is a perfect way to relax and soothe tired muscles after a round, embodying the course's commitment to comfort and local hospitality, especially appealing during cooler seasons.

The clubhouse restaurant offers a delightful culinary experience, emphasizing fresh, local Hokkaido ingredients. The menu presents a balanced selection of both traditional Japanese dishes and Western-style options. Popular choices often include local seafood delicacies, hearty ramen, and a Hokkaido specialty like "Jingisukan" (grilled lamb). The dining area provides pleasant views of the course, creating an ideal setting to recount the day's rounds over a delicious meal and a local beverage.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Asahikawa Winter Roast with a caddie provides a significant advantage, particularly for first-time visitors. Common mistakes often include misjudging the subtle breaks on the bentgrass greens, which frequently have more movement than they initially appear. Caddies possess invaluable local knowledge regarding these greens, helping with accurate reads and suggesting optimal approach shot landing areas to set up easier putts. They also excel at advising on club selection, especially when dealing with the often-present Hokkaido winds and the course's various elevation changes.

Caddies are instrumental in guiding golfers through the strategic nuances of the course, such as identifying hidden hazards, advising on the best lines off the tee to avoid tree lines or bunkers, and suggesting sensible lay-up positions on the longer holes. For instance, on the challenging 16th, a caddie's advice on tee shot placement can be the difference between a clear approach and a blocked shot. This course primarily suits mid-to-low handicap golfers who appreciate strategic design and enjoy thinking their way around a golf course, rather than simply overpowering it. Higher handicappers will still enjoy the beautiful setting and excellent conditioning but may find the strategic demands a tougher challenge without a caddie's guidance.
