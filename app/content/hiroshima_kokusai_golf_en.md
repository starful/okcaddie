## Course Overview

Hiroshima Kokusai Golf is an 18-hole, par 72 course, presenting a challenging mountain-style layout in Higashihiroshima. It typically plays around 6,600-6,900 yards from the back tees, offering a good test of accuracy over raw distance. Fairways commonly feature Zoysia grass, while the greens are usually Bentgrass, providing consistent playing surfaces year-round. This course is known for its scenic views across the Hiroshima mountains and offers significant value for its green fees.

## Layout & Strategy

The course demands strategic thinking, particularly due to its mountain terrain, which features significant elevation changes and tree-lined corridors. For instance, the **6th hole, a par 4**, often presents an elevated tee shot looking down onto a fairway that narrows into a dogleg left. A precise tee shot with a 3-wood or hybrid is recommended to avoid dense forest on the left, leaving a mid-iron approach to a two-tiered green.

The **12th hole, a par 3**, can play significantly shorter than its yardage due to a dramatic downhill tee shot. Club selection is critical here, accounting for the elevation drop and potential crosswinds, aiming for the center of a green protected by bunkers on either side. On the **15th hole, a par 5**, a well-placed tee shot avoids a fairway bunker on the right, setting up a risk-reward second shot; going for the green in two requires carrying a natural ravine, otherwise laying up to a comfortable distance ensures a straightforward wedge approach.

## Practical Info

Green fees at Hiroshima Kokusai Golf are competitive, aligning with its "Value for Money" tag. Expect weekday rates generally ranging from JPY 7,000 to JPY 12,000, while weekend and holiday rounds typically fall between JPY 10,000 and JPY 16,000. These prices usually include a mandatory shared cart.

The course is open to the public for reservations; booking well in advance, especially for weekend play, is advisable through online portals or a local agent. Standard golf attire is expected, meaning collared shirts and no denim, and soft spikes are universally required. Caddies are often an optional addition, available for an extra fee, and can be valuable for navigating the mountain terrain.

## Access

Hiroshima Kokusai Golf is conveniently located in Higashihiroshima, offering relatively straightforward access for visitors. The nearest major train station is Higashi-Hiroshima Station, served by the Sanyo Shinkansen line, providing regional connectivity. From central Hiroshima City, the course is approximately a 45 to 60-minute drive, typically accessed via the Sanyo Expressway. Ample parking is available on-site for those driving privately or utilizing rental cars.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse at Hiroshima Kokusai Golf provides the expected amenities for a Japanese golf experience, focusing on comfort and tradition. Spacious locker rooms are available for changing, and a communal bath (ofuro), often with onsen-quality water or similar relaxing facilities, is typical for post-round rejuvenation. The restaurant offers a selection of Japanese and Western dishes, frequently featuring local ingredients, providing a comfortable setting for lunch or a post-game meal.

## Tips

To maximize your experience, aim for rounds during the spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November), when temperatures are mild and the mountain scenery is at its best. Be aware that summer can bring high humidity and typhoon risks, while winter may see colder temperatures or even snow at this elevation. A common mistake here is misjudging distances due to significant elevation changes; trust your rangefinder and consider a club down for downhill shots. Factor in potential peak season pricing during these optimal months, and book tee times well in advance to secure your preferred slot.
