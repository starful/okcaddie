## Course Overview
Nago Bay Golf Course offers an 18-hole, par-72 challenge overlooking the East China Sea. Opened in 1989, the course was designed by Hisayuki Sasaki and supervised by Isao Aoki. Measuring approximately 6,300 to 6,700 yards from its main tees, the layout features Bermuda grass fairways and bent grass greens. Constant ocean vistas and significant elevation changes are signature characteristics here.

## Layout & Strategy
Strategic play is crucial across Nago Bay, demanding careful club selection and shot placement. The 2nd hole, a challenging par-5, requires a precise tee shot to a right-sloping landing area, often tempting a cut over dense foliage. A conservative lay-up short of the green's bunkers frequently proves wiser than an aggressive second shot, especially with crosswinds. The 7th hole, a scenic par-3, is exposed to the elements; prevailing winds off the bay necessitate careful club adjustment for the mid-iron approach to its well-guarded green. Finally, the 18th, a finishing par-4, presents an elevated tee shot over a valley to a narrowing fairway, leading to a complex, multi-tiered green.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Nago Bay typically range from ¥8,000 to ¥12,000 on weekdays, increasing to ¥11,000 to ¥16,000 on weekends and holidays; these rates often include a shared cart. Advance reservations are strongly recommended, especially for groups and peak seasons, and can be made online or via local booking services. Play is usually conducted with a compulsory two-seater or four-seater electric cart equipped with GPS. The dress code follows standard golf etiquette: collared shirts, tailored shorts or trousers, and soft-spike golf shoes are required. Caddies are available by request for an additional fee per group, providing local course insights.

## Access
Nago Bay Golf Course is located in Nago, approximately 70-80 kilometers north of Naha Airport. The drive typically takes about 1.5 hours via the Okinawa Expressway, depending on traffic. As Okinawa lacks a comprehensive train system, a rental car is the most practical transport option for reaching the course. Ample free parking is available directly at the clubhouse.

## Clubhouse Notes
The Nago Bay clubhouse offers practical amenities for golfers. Clean locker rooms provide changing facilities, and a traditional Japanese communal bath (ofuro) allows for post-round relaxation. The on-site restaurant serves a selection of local Okinawan dishes; Okinawa Soba is a popular choice, alongside fresh seafood preparations that reflect the island's culinary heritage.

## Tips
For the most enjoyable experience, plan your visit during Okinawa's spring (March-May) or fall (October-November), when the weather is mild and less humid. Be prepared for wind, which significantly influences shot distances and trajectories, especially on ocean-exposed holes. Misjudging subtle breaks on the greens, which often pull towards the bay, is a common error. Booking tee times well in advance is crucial, particularly if your trip coincides with Japanese public holidays or the peak winter escape season, as these periods also tend to see slightly higher green fees.
