## Course Overview

Miyazaki Lakeside Golf presents an 18-hole, par 72 layout, recognized for its appealing lake views and overall value. The course typically plays between 6,700 to 7,000 yards from the back tees, offering a balanced challenge across its mature landscape. Its fairways are often Zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface, while the greens are generally Bentgrass, known for their consistent roll.

The design leverages the natural topography and the expansive lake, integrating water hazards strategically into play on several holes. This established course emphasizes shot placement over brute force, rewarding thoughtful approaches and precise execution. The scenic environment, characterized by mature trees and water features, contributes significantly to the golfing experience.

## Layout & Strategy

The course design at Miyazaki Lakeside Golf demands careful consideration, particularly around its numerous water elements. Hole 5, a par 4 dogleg left, presents an early test where the lake flanks the entire left side of the fairway. A tee shot aimed at the right side, potentially clearing a well-placed bunker, sets up the best angle for an approach to an often-elevated green.

The signature par 3, Hole 12, can feel like an island green, with water surrounding its front and sides. Club selection here is paramount, as a mishit can easily find the hazard; aim for the center of the green and be prepared for subtle breaks on the putting surface. Further into the back nine, Hole 16, a lengthy par 5, sweeps gently right with the lake becoming a prominent factor on the second and third shots. Strategic layups are key to avoid challenging carry distances over water to a protected green.

## Practical Info

Green fees at Miyazaki Lakeside Golf are generally competitive, reflecting its "value for money" tag. Weekday rates typically range from ¥8,000 to ¥12,000, while weekend and holiday play usually falls between ¥12,000 and ¥18,000, often including cart fees. Advance booking is highly recommended, especially for weekend slots, as the course is publicly accessible and popular with locals.

Golf carts are standard and often mandatory, equipped with GPS systems to assist with yardages and course navigation. The dress code is consistent with most Japanese golf clubs: collared shirts and trousers or golf shorts are expected, with soft spikes or spikeless shoes preferred. Caddie services are available, and while not always mandatory, they can significantly enhance the round, particularly for first-time visitors navigating the course's nuances.

## Access

Miyazaki Lakeside Golf is conveniently situated within the Miyazaki Prefecture, making it accessible from the regional hub. The nearest major transport link is Miyazaki Station, located in Miyazaki City. From Miyazaki Station, the course is approximately a 30 to 45-minute drive, covering a distance of around 20 to 25 kilometers.

Miyazaki Airport is also a viable entry point, with a similar drive time and distance to the course. Ample free parking is available on-site, accommodating both private vehicles and tour buses. The drive itself typically offers straightforward navigation, passing through scenic local areas.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse at Miyazaki Lakeside Golf provides functional and well-maintained facilities for golfers. Locker rooms are clean and efficient, designed to accommodate the flow of daily players. A common feature, the large communal bath (daiyokujo), offers a relaxing post-round soak, which is a traditional element of the Japanese golf experience. The restaurant typically serves a range of local and Western-style dishes. Among the local specialties, their Chicken Nanban, a Miyazaki culinary staple, is a frequently ordered item and offers a savory taste of the region.

## Tips

To best enjoy Miyazaki Lakeside Golf, consider visiting during the spring (March to May) or autumn (October to November) months when the weather is mild and comfortable for golf. During these peak seasons, expect slightly higher green fees and ensure bookings are made well in advance. A common mistake for first-time players is underestimating the influence of the lake on shot selection and misjudging the subtle elevation changes on the greens.

Summer months bring humidity and the risk of typhoons, while winter can be cool, particularly in the mornings. Paying attention to pin positions, especially on holes near water, can save strokes. If utilizing a caddie, heed their local knowledge regarding club selection and green reads, as their insights can be invaluable on this strategic layout.
