## Course Overview
Okayama Royal Golf provides a compelling 18-hole, par-72 experience in the Kibi region of Okayama Prefecture. Opened in the early 1990s, the course spans approximately 6,800-7,100 yards from the back tees, featuring a design that skillfully integrates with the natural mountain topography. Fairways are typically Zoysia grass, known for its resilience and firm lies, while the greens are a consistent Bentgrass.

As a mountain-style layout, the course is defined by significant elevation changes, undulating fairways, and strategic bunkering that often comes into play. Its "Value for Money" reputation is well-earned, offering both a challenging round and scenic views without a premium price tag. The design emphasizes shot placement over raw power, rewarding thoughtful play.

## Layout & Strategy
Hole 3, a par-4, demands a precise tee shot to a landing area that doglegs sharply left and uphill. Avoid the dense trees on the left side by favoring the right, setting up a mid-iron approach to an elevated green that often hides its front bunkers. The putting line here often features subtle breaks, influenced by the overall slope of the mountain.

The par-3 7th hole is a notable downhill shot, frequently exposed to crosswinds, which makes club selection critical. While the green appears large, a front-left bunker can collect errant shots, so aiming for the center or slightly right of the pin is a prudent strategy. Take an extra club less than you think for this downhill approach, allowing the slope to assist your ball flight.

On the par-5 15th, players face a decision on the second shot, a true risk-reward opportunity. A narrow fairway bordered by a hazard on the right and dense woods on the left makes reaching the green in two difficult for most, favoring a safe layup to a specific yardage. For those attempting to go for it, a long, accurate fairway wood or hybrid is necessary to clear the trouble, leading to a green with nuanced slopes that can challenge even well-struck putts.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Okayama Royal Golf typically range from 8,000-12,000 JPY on weekdays and 12,000-18,000 JPY on weekends and holidays, inclusive of cart fees. The course is publicly accessible, but advance reservations are strongly recommended, especially for weekend play or during peak seasons.

Golf carts are mandatory and come equipped with GPS navigation, making course management straightforward. The dress code adheres to standard golf etiquette: collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft spikes are required, while denim is prohibited. Caddies are available upon request and incur an additional fee, often recommended for first-time visitors to navigate the mountain terrain efficiently.

## Access
Okayama Royal Golf is conveniently located from major transport hubs in the region. The nearest local train station is Kibitsu Station, a short taxi ride away. For those arriving from further afield, Okayama Station, a Shinkansen stop, is the closest major city hub.

Driving from Okayama Station generally takes approximately 30-45 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. Ample on-site parking is available for guests arriving by private car or rental vehicle.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse facilities at Okayama Royal Golf are designed for comfort and convenience, featuring well-maintained locker rooms for changing before and after your round. A spacious communal bath (ofuro), typical of Japanese golf clubs, provides an ideal setting for post-round relaxation, complete with showers and soaking tubs. The restaurant offers a selection of Japanese and Western dishes, often highlighting local Okayama specialties such as Kibi pork dishes or fresh seasonal produce from the region.

## Tips
For the best playing conditions and scenery, consider visiting Okayama Royal Golf in spring (April-May) when cherry blossoms bloom, or autumn (October-November) for vibrant fall foliage and comfortable temperatures. Be prepared for significant elevation changes throughout the round, which commonly lead to misjudged distances and incorrect club selection, particularly on approach shots.

Summer months (July-August) can be very hot and humid, while winter (December-March) may bring colder temperatures and occasional frost delays. Peak season play, especially on weekends and national holidays during spring and autumn, will see higher green fees and more crowded tee sheets, so book well in advance. Always factor in the actual carry distance for uphill and downhill shots rather than just the yardage marker.
