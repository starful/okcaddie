## Course Overview
Takarazuka Golf Club, established in 1926, is a historic private club featuring 36 holes across two distinct 18-hole courses: the East and West. Both courses were originally designed by J.E. Crane. The East Course typically plays around 6,800-7,000 yards from the back tees with a par of 72, while the West Course is slightly shorter at approximately 6,600-6,800 yards, also par 72. Fairways are predominantly Zoysia, transitioning to Bentgrass greens known for their subtle undulations. As a true forest course, it is characterized by mature trees framing narrow fairways and requiring precise ball striking.

## Layout & Strategy
The East Course presents several strategic challenges, demanding precision from the tee. One notable par-4 offers an elevated tee shot looking down a tree-lined fairway that doglegs sharply right around 230 yards out. A precise drive down the left side is essential to avoid dense woods and open up an approach to a green guarded by bunkers.
Another example is a scenic par-3 that demands a mid-iron carry over a small valley to a two-tiered green. The putting line often involves navigating a subtle ridge, making par a commendable score on this hole. The West Course also features a strategic par-5 where a successful tee shot needs to hug the right side of a sloping fairway to gain an advantage, but longer hitters must beware of a hidden creek that cuts across.

## Practical Info
As a private club, access to Takarazuka Golf Club is primarily reserved for members and their guests. Non-members typically require an introduction from a member for booking. Green fees for non-member play, often including a mandatory caddie and cart (if available), range from approximately ¥18,000 to ¥25,000 on weekdays, and ¥25,000 to ¥35,000 on weekends and holidays.
The club maintains a traditional dress code; collared shirts and tailored trousers are standard on the course, with a jacket often recommended for the main dining room. Play is typically walking, accompanied by a professional caddie who provides valuable course management assistance and reads greens.

## Access
Takarazuka Golf Club is conveniently located in Hyogo Prefecture, accessible from major Kansai cities. The nearest main train station is Takarazuka Station, served by both JR Takarazuka Line and Hankyu Takarazuka Line, approximately a 15-20 minute taxi ride from the club. By car, it is about a 40-50 minute drive from central Osaka, or roughly 30 minutes from Kobe. The club provides extensive on-site parking for members and guests.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse at Takarazuka Golf Club maintains a classic ambiance, reflecting its long history. Its well-appointed locker rooms are spacious and feature comprehensive amenities. Guests can enjoy a relaxing post-round soak in the large communal bath (ofuro), a common feature in Japanese golf clubs. The main dining room, offering views of the course, serves a menu of both Japanese and Western cuisine, with regional specialties such as Hyogo Wagyu steak or fresh seasonal tempura often highlighted.

## Tips
For optimal conditions, plan your visit during spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November), when temperatures are mild and the surrounding foliage is at its most vibrant. Avoid the summer months (July-August) due to high humidity and potential for typhoons, or the colder, sometimes snowy winter (December-February). A common mistake is misreading the subtle undulations on the greens; trust your caddie's advice, particularly on approach shots and putting lines. Be prepared for varied weather, as mountain courses can experience sudden changes, and note that peak season pricing typically applies during favorable weather windows and holidays.
