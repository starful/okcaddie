Aichi Country Club actually features two distinct 18-hole courses: the East Course and the West Course. Both courses are meticulously designed to challenge golfers of all skill levels.

*   **East Course:** This course is renowned for its strategic layout, demanding accuracy and precision. It features undulating fairways, strategically placed bunkers, and challenging water hazards. The greens are known for their subtle slopes and speed, requiring a deft touch and careful reading. The East Course is often regarded as the more challenging of the two.

*   **West Course:** The West Course offers a more forgiving layout, with wider fairways and fewer water hazards. However, it still presents a formidable test of golfing skill. The greens are equally challenging, and the course's strategic bunkering demands careful shot placement. The West Course is often favored for its scenic beauty, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

While the specific architect of the courses is difficult to ascertain with absolute certainty given the club's private nature and age, both courses have undoubtedly been shaped and refined by some of Japan's leading golf course architects over the years. The signature holes on both courses often involve strategically placed water features or demanding approach shots to elevated greens.
