## Course Overview
Okayama Country Club, established in 1960, presents a classic 18-hole, par 72 layout, measuring approximately 6,800-7,000 yards from the championship tees. Designed by S. Fukui, this historic course is characteristic of Japan's mid-century forest layouts, featuring tree-lined fairways carved into natural terrain. The greens are typically bent grass, maintained for a smooth, consistent roll, while the fairways consist of zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface.

## Layout & Strategy
The course demands thoughtful strategy over sheer power, with many holes requiring precise placement off the tee. Consider the 5th hole, a par 4 that doglegs sharply left and uphill; a well-placed long iron or hybrid to the corner sets up a clearer approach, avoiding the dense trees that guard the left side. The 13th hole, a scenic par 3, plays downhill to a green often guarded by water in front and deep bunkers behind; selecting one club less than usual can be a prudent choice here, focusing on hitting the center of the green. Concluding the round, the 18th hole, a par 5, offers a strategic finish with multiple landing zones narrowing as you approach the green; players often face a decision between a layup to a wide area or a more aggressive second shot risking a tight bottleneck of trees.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Okayama Country Club typically range from 18,000-28,000 JPY on weekdays and 28,000-38,000 JPY on weekends and holidays, inclusive of caddie and cart. As a private club, guest play is generally arranged through members or select golf travel packages; direct public booking can be challenging. Play is primarily by riding cart, often accompanied by a professional caddie who provides yardage and reads the greens. A smart casual dress code applies on the course, with collared shirts and soft spikes mandatory, and a jacket is often expected in the main dining areas of the clubhouse.

## Access
Okayama Country Club is conveniently located approximately 40 minutes by car from Okayama Station, the main shinkansen hub in Okayama City. From the station, the drive covers about 15-20 kilometers, heading northeast into the surrounding hills. Ample parking is available on-site for those traveling by private vehicle.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse offers a traditional Japanese golf experience, featuring spacious locker rooms and a communal bath (ofuro) for post-round relaxation, a common amenity at Japanese courses. Dining facilities typically serve a range of local Okayama specialties, often highlighting fresh Setouchi seafood or locally raised Kibi beef in their signature dishes. The atmosphere is generally quiet and refined, reflecting the club's history and private status.

## Tips
The optimal golfing seasons in Okayama are spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November), when the weather is mild and the course conditions are at their best. A common mistake for first-time players is underestimating the subtle elevation changes and the speed of the bent grass greens, which often feature local breaks towards the Seto Inland Sea. Be mindful of summer heat and humidity from July to September, and the occasional typhoon risk in late summer. Peak season pricing applies during spring, autumn, and national holidays, so plan accordingly for potential cost increases.
