## Course Overview

Kujyu Kogen Golf, established in 1996 by a local design team, offers an 18-hole, par 72 layout carved into the dramatic landscape of the Kujyu mountain range in Oita Prefecture. Stretching approximately 6,850 yards from the longest tees, the course is known for its significant elevation changes and integration with the natural volcanic terrain. Fairways are typically Zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface, while the greens are a smooth Bentgrass, often presenting subtle breaks. The overall character of the course is defined by its high-altitude setting, providing golfers with extended carry distances and constant panoramic views.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating Kujyu Kogen demands thoughtful club selection and precise shot placement, particularly due to the varied terrain and wind exposure.

**Hole 3 (Par 4, 390-420 yards):** This uphill par 4 requires a well-struck tee shot to carry a rise and find the narrow landing area. Overcooking your drive can lead to trouble in the rough or down a slope, while an under-hit shot leaves a long, blind approach. The green here typically slopes from back to front, making approach shots from above the hole challenging to stop. A mid-iron approach aimed at the front edge of the green often provides the best opportunity for a two-putt par.

**Hole 7 (Par 3, 160-190 yards):** A signature downhill par 3, the 7th offers stunning views but demands a precise tee shot. The ball will fly further due to the significant elevation drop, so club selection needs careful adjustment for both distance and wind conditions which can swirl unpredictably. Bunkers guard the front and sides of the green, and any shot landing short or wide can quickly find a deep penalty. Aim for the center of the green, allowing the natural slope to potentially feed the ball towards the pin.

**Hole 12 (Par 5, 520-560 yards):** This dogleg right par 5 presents an immediate strategic decision off the tee. A drive hugging the right side can shorten the hole significantly, but hazards including a creek and dense trees lurk for anything pushed or hooked. The second shot involves navigating a subtle uphill lie, with water coming into play on the left side for those trying to reach in two. Laying up short of the water and positioning for a wedge approach is often the prudent play, as the green is multi-tiered and protected by bunkers.

**Hole 16 (Par 4, 360-390 yards):** The tee shot on the 16th is from an elevated position, offering an expansive view of the winding fairway below. While tempting to unleash a driver, accuracy is paramount as the landing zone narrows considerably. A strategic lay-up with a long iron or hybrid, positioning your ball on the flatter section of the fairway, can leave a comfortable mid-iron approach. The green is subtly contoured and often plays firmer than it appears, requiring a precise carry to avoid rolling off the back or sides.

## Conditions & Seasonality

The best times to play Kujyu Kogen Golf are during the spring (April to early June) and autumn (late September to October). Spring offers pleasant temperatures and blooming greenery, while autumn provides vibrant foliage and crisp air. Summer months (July-August) can be warm, but the high altitude often provides some relief from the oppressive humidity found at sea level. Winter play, from December to March, is possible but subject to course closures due to snow or frost, which are not uncommon in the Kujyu highlands. Wind can be a significant factor year-round, particularly on exposed holes, requiring players to account for gusts and crosswinds. Pace of play is generally well-managed, with most rounds completing within four hours, especially on weekdays. Weekends and national holidays see higher traffic, but marshals typically ensure a steady flow.

## Green Fees & Booking

Green fees at Kujyu Kogen Golf are reflective of its quality and location within a popular resort area. Expect weekday rates to range from approximately ¥10,000 to ¥16,000 per person for a round, which typically includes a shared golf cart. Weekend and holiday rates are higher, usually between ¥15,000 and ¥22,000. Caddies are available but generally optional; if requested, a caddie fee typically adds ¥4,000-¥6,000 per player for a shared caddie. Visitors are warmly welcomed, and there is no strict member-only policy. Booking is most efficiently done online through major Japanese golf reservation portals such as Rakuten GORA or GDO (Golf Digest Online). These platforms often feature various play plans, including "one-round only" or "morning half" options, and sometimes offer slight discounts. Direct booking through the course's official Japanese website is also an option, and may require translation assistance.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Kujyu Kogen Golf maintains a standard dress code typical of Japanese private courses, emphasizing respect for the game and fellow players. Collared shirts are mandatory for both men and women, and tailored trousers or golf shorts are expected; denim jeans are not permitted on the course or in the clubhouse dining areas. Soft-spiked golf shoes are required. Mobile phone usage is generally restricted on the course; phones should be kept on silent mode, and calls should only be made in designated areas or the clubhouse to minimize disruption. Players are expected to adhere to a reasonable pace of play, typically targeting a 2-hour front nine and a 2-hour back nine. Replacing divots, repairing pitch marks, and raking bunkers are customary expectations for all golfers.

## Access

Kujyu Kogen Golf is located in Taketa, Oita Prefecture, placing it within reasonable driving distance of key regional transport hubs. The most practical approach for international and domestic travelers is to fly into Oita Airport (OIT). From Oita Airport, the drive to the course is approximately 1.5 hours by rental car or taxi. For those traveling by train, the nearest major station is Bungo-Taketa on the JR Hōhi Main Line, which is about a 30-40 minute drive from the course. Rental cars are highly recommended for the flexibility to explore the surrounding Aso-Kuju National Park area. From Fukuoka City, the largest city in Kyushu, the course is roughly a 2.5 to 3-hour drive via expressways. Driving from Oita City itself typically takes around 1 hour. Ample free parking is available on-site at the clubhouse. Long-distance drives from Honshu cities like Osaka, Kobe, Hiroshima, or Tokyo are generally not practical; flying into Oita or Fukuoka is the advised method of access.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Kujyu Kogen Golf provides a comfortable and functional base for golfers. Locker rooms are modern and well-maintained, offering sufficient space for changing and secure storage. A highlight for many players is the traditional Japanese bath facility, often featuring an onsen-style communal bath (gender-separated). This provides a relaxing soak after a round, a common and appreciated amenity at Japanese golf courses. The clubhouse restaurant offers a variety of Japanese dishes, often featuring local Oita specialties. Expect to find items such as Bungo beef (a regional wagyu), chicken tempura, and fresh local vegetables. The dining area often boasts large windows providing scenic views of the course and the surrounding Kujyu mountains, making it a pleasant spot for a post-round meal or a light lunch between nines.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Kujyu Kogen Golf with local knowledge can significantly enhance your experience. A common mistake for first-time visitors is misjudging distances due to the high altitude; your ball will typically fly further, so adjust your club selection downwards, especially on elevated tees. Conversely, approaches to uphill greens will often require an extra half-club. Another frequent error is under-reading the greens; while they appear smooth, the subtle slopes and underlying mountain contours can create deceptive breaks that a caddie can expertly point out. Pay attention to the wind, as it can swirl unpredictably between the mountain peaks.

Caddies can provide invaluable local knowledge regarding ideal landing zones, blind hazards, and the best angles for approach shots on the doglegs and undulating fairways. They often know specific pin positions and how the surrounding terrain influences putts. This course is particularly well-suited for golfers who appreciate a strategic challenge, enjoy dramatic elevation changes, and value panoramic scenic views over a purely score-focused round. Mid to low handicappers will find the most rewarding experience by embracing the course's unique character and strategic demands. For higher handicappers, the course offers a beautiful setting, but the significant elevation changes and tricky greens may prove a substantial test, making a caddie even more beneficial.
