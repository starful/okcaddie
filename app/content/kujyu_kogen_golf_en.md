## Course Overview
Kujyu Kogen Golf offers 18 holes of mountain-style golf, typically playing to a par of 72. The course measures around 6,400 to 6,800 yards from the regular tees, often playing shorter due to its high altitude setting. Fairways are generally Zoysia, with Bentgrass greens, a common combination in this region of Japan. This course is characterized by significant elevation changes, requiring precise shot selection and offering panoramic vistas across the Kuju mountain range.

## Layout & Strategy
Three holes at Kujyu Kogen exemplify its strategic demands. The 4th hole, a downhill par-4, presents a tee shot over a valley to a landing area that slants left; precision with a 3-wood or hybrid is key to avoid the dense rough on the right. Its green is often guarded by a bunker front-left, requiring an approach that favors the right side before breaking left towards the pin.

The 7th, a challenging uphill par-3, demands an extra club or two due to the significant elevation gain from tee to green. The tee shot provides a clear view of the flag, but depth perception can be deceiving; miss short and you're in a collection area, while going long is usually preferable. The green typically runs front-to-back, so putting requires aggressive lines on uphill putts.

Later, the 13th, a dogleg right par-5, calls for a strategic tee shot to a plateau. While aggressive players might attempt to cut the corner, a precise drive down the center-left is usually safer, leaving a good layup opportunity before a downhill approach to a green flanked by bunkers. Putting on this green often features subtle breaks influenced by the surrounding mountain slopes.

## Practical Info
Green fees generally range from 10,000-16,000 JPY on weekdays and 15,000-22,000 JPY on weekends, including cart fees. Public access is available, and booking several weeks in advance is advisable, especially for weekend play or during peak seasons. Electric carts are standard, often equipped with GPS, and most rounds are cart-based, with walking generally not an option due to the terrain. Standard golf attire is expected: collared shirts, golf slacks or tailored shorts, and soft-spike golf shoes are customary. Caddies are available, usually as an optional add-on, and can provide valuable course knowledge regarding lies and breaks; booking a caddie should be requested during reservation.

## Access
Kujyu Kogen Golf is situated in a rural area, requiring a car for convenient access. The closest major city is Oita City, approximately a 90-minute to 2-hour drive depending on traffic conditions. From Kumamoto City, expect a similar drive time, around 1.5 to 2 hours via scenic mountain roads. The nearest train station, Bungo-Taketa Station on the JR Hohi Main Line, is still a significant taxi or car ride away, around 40 minutes. Ample free parking is available on site for those driving themselves.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse provides a well-appointed space for pre- and post-round activities. Locker rooms are clean and functional, leading to a communal Japanese-style bath, often featuring natural spring water (onsen) given the region's volcanic activity. The restaurant offers a selection of local and Japanese cuisine; their Bungo Beef curry or a set meal featuring local Kuju pork are often recommended for a satisfying post-round meal.

## Tips
The best times to play Kujyu Kogen Golf are spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November), when the weather is mild and the mountain scenery is at its most vibrant. Golfers often misjudge distances on uphill approaches, so consider taking an extra club for elevation gain. Mountain weather can shift rapidly, with fog or wind sometimes rolling in quickly, so pack appropriate layers. Expect higher green fees and increased booking competition during weekends in these peak seasons.
