## Course Overview

Phoenix Seagaia Resort, specifically its renowned Phoenix Country Club course, carries a significant legacy in Japanese golf. Designed by the esteemed Robert Trent Jones Jr. and opened in 1971, this 27-hole facility is best known for its two championship nines—the Sumiyoshi and Takachiho courses—which combine to form the 18-hole layout used for the annual Dunlop Phoenix Tournament. This prestigious event has been a fixture since 1974, attracting golf's biggest names and solidifying the course's reputation for exacting play.

The course typically plays as a par 72, with championship tees stretching over 7,000 yards, while regular tees offer a more manageable but still challenging experience around 6,500 yards. Fairways are often lined by dense pine forests, requiring precision off the tee to avoid penalizing lies. The turf primarily features Bermuda grass for fairways and rough, transitioning to immaculate bentgrass greens that are known for their speed and subtle undulations. The overall character is that of a classic parkland layout with strategic bunkering and an underlying coastal influence, presenting a stern test of golf amidst natural beauty.

## Layout & Strategy

The Phoenix Country Club layout at Seagaia rewards strategic thinking and accurate ball-striking, with several holes demanding particular attention. Here are four examples that showcase its character and strategic depth.

**Hole 3 (Sumiyoshi Course) – Par 4, 420-440 yards:** This challenging dogleg right requires a precise tee shot that skirts a large pond guarding the inside corner. Players must decide between a conservative play to the left, leaving a longer approach, or a more aggressive line over the water for a shorter second shot. The green itself is protected by bunkers on both sides and features subtle slopes that demand a well-struck approach to hold the surface.

**Hole 7 (Sumiyoshi Course) – Par 3, 170-190 yards:** This scenic par 3 often plays slightly downhill to an elevated green, creating a visual trickery that can lead to misclubbing. Deep bunkers frame the green, catching any shots that are short or pulled slightly offline. Wind can be a significant factor on this exposed tee, requiring an extra club or two depending on its direction and strength.

**Hole 13 (Takachiho Course) – Par 5, 540-560 yards:** A reachable par 5 for longer hitters, but not without risk. The tee shot needs to carry a series of bunkers on the left, opening up the fairway for a potential run at the green. Those going for it in two must navigate a narrow approach guarded by water on the left and thick trees on the right, making a lay-up to a precise yardage a safer, often wiser, play.

**Hole 17 (Takachiho Course) – Par 4, 450-470 yards:** Often considered one of the signature holes, this long par 4 features a tight, tree-lined driving corridor that places a premium on accuracy. The second shot is typically a long iron or hybrid into a green that slopes from back to front, making it difficult to get spin and hold the putting surface. Par here feels like a birdie, especially under tournament conditions.

## Conditions & Seasonality

Miyazaki generally enjoys a mild climate, making Phoenix Country Club playable for much of the year, but optimal conditions are found during specific seasons. The best months for a golf trip are typically **autumn (October-November)**, when temperatures are comfortable, and the weather is generally stable with less humidity and lower chances of rain. **Spring (April-May)** also offers pleasant conditions with blossoming flora, though occasional spring showers can occur.

The summer months **(July-September)** can be hot and humid, and this period also coincides with Japan's typhoon season, which can bring heavy rain and strong winds. While the course drains well, typhoon-related closures are possible. Winter **(December-February)** is cool but rarely sees snow, making it still playable, but pack layers as temperatures can drop, especially with coastal winds. Wind is a consistent factor due to the course's proximity to the coast, often influencing club selection and shot trajectory. Pace of play is generally well-managed in Japan; expect rounds to complete in around 4 hours 30 minutes to 5 hours, with marshals gently encouraging groups to maintain their position. Weekdays are typically less crowded than weekends and Japanese public holidays, offering a more serene golf experience.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Phoenix Country Club upholds a traditional golf club dress code, reflecting its status and the etiquette prevalent at high-end Japanese courses. For men, a collared golf shirt (tucked in), tailored golf trousers or shorts, and appropriate golf shoes (soft spikes only) are expected. Denim, t-shirts, tracksuits, or cargo shorts are not permitted. Women should wear collared golf shirts, golf skirts, skorts, or tailored trousers/shorts.

On the course, adherence to general golf etiquette is paramount. This includes repairing divots, fixing pitch marks on greens, and raking bunkers thoroughly. Mobile phone use is permitted but should be discreet; keep phones on silent and avoid taking calls on the course except in emergencies. Pace of play is taken seriously; be ready to hit when it's your turn, and avoid unnecessary delays. Caddies are instrumental in maintaining pace and will guide you on these expectations. It is customary to arrive well in advance of your tee time to allow for check-in, changing, and a brief warm-up.

## Access

Phoenix Seagaia Resort, and by extension Phoenix Country Club, is conveniently located in Miyazaki Prefecture, offering good accessibility for travelers. The nearest major transportation hub is **Miyazaki Airport (KMI)**. From the airport, the resort is approximately a **30-45 minute drive** via taxi or shuttle service. For those arriving in Miyazaki city center, the drive to the resort typically takes **20-30 minutes**.

While driving from major cities like Tokyo, Osaka, or Hiroshima is technically possible, it is not recommended due to the vast distances. Instead, most visitors from these regions opt for domestic flights to Miyazaki Airport. Flights from **Tokyo (Haneda or Narita)** to Miyazaki usually take around **1.5 to 2 hours**. From **Osaka (Itami or Kansai)**, the flight duration is approximately **1 hour**. Fukuoka, a major Kyushu hub, offers flights of about **45 minutes to 1 hour**, or a scenic but longer drive of **3-4 hours** through the prefecture. The resort offers ample, well-maintained parking facilities for guests who choose to rent a car or drive themselves to the property.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Phoenix Country Club provides a fittingly luxurious experience, complementing the championship golf course. The **locker rooms** are spacious, impeccably clean, and well-appointed, featuring private changing areas, fresh towels, and a range of toiletries to ensure comfort before and after your round. Following the Japanese tradition, the facility includes a large, communal **bath (ofuro)** area, providing a relaxing soak for tired muscles; some luxury resorts like Seagaia may even incorporate natural onsen spring water.

Dining options are varied and cater to diverse palates. The main **clubhouse restaurant** typically offers a selection of both Japanese and Western cuisine, from traditional bento boxes and noodle dishes to hearty steaks and salads. Expect high-quality ingredients, often sourced locally from Miyazaki Prefecture, which is renowned for its fresh produce and wagyu beef. Many golfers enjoy the opportunity to sample Miyazaki specialties like *Chicken Nanban* (fried chicken with tartar sauce) or local *Miyazaki Beef*. There are often casual dining areas for a quick bite or refreshing drink, alongside more formal settings for a celebratory meal, providing guests with flexibility and choice throughout their visit.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Phoenix Country Club with a caddie is not just a luxury; it’s an invaluable asset for navigating this championship layout. A common mistake players make here is **underestimating the subtle undulations on the greens**. While they may appear relatively flat, the bentgrass surfaces are quick and feature breaks that are challenging to read without local knowledge. Pay close attention to your caddie's advice on putting lines, as they often have years of experience with the specific greens.

**Local knowledge** from your caddie extends beyond just green reading. They will guide you on optimal landing areas off the tee, especially crucial on the pine-lined fairways where stray shots are heavily penalized. Caddies often know the exact yardage to carry bunkers or position a lay-up shot on strategic par 5s. They can also advise on how the prevailing coastal winds will affect your ball flight and club selection, an element often misjudged by first-time players. Who is this course best suited for? Phoenix Country Club is ideal for **mid to low handicappers** who appreciate a challenging, strategic golf experience and a meticulously maintained course. Players who enjoy tree-lined parkland layouts with a coastal influence will find this course particularly rewarding. It demands precision, thoughtful course management, and a decent short game, making it less suitable for absolute beginners or those solely looking for an easy, resort-style round.
