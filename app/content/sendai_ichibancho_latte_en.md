## Course Overview

Sendai Ichibancho Latte presents a thoughtful 18-hole, Par 72 layout set within the undulating terrain just outside Sendai. The course typically plays between 6,600 to 7,000 yards from the championship tees, requiring a blend of accuracy and distance management. Opened in the late 1990s, the design emphasizes natural contours and strategic positioning, rather than sheer length.

Fairways are predominantly Zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface, while the Bentgrass greens offer consistent roll and varied breaks. The course's signature characteristics include several holes featuring elevation changes, mature tree-lined fairways, and well-placed bunkering that demand careful shot selection. Distant mountain vistas and glimpses of the Sendai city fringe punctuate the round, adding to the regional character.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating Sendai Ichibancho Latte rewards strategic thinking throughout the round. A few holes particularly stand out for their tactical demands.

The 5th hole, a mid-length Par 4 often playing around 390 yards, presents a challenging dogleg left. From an elevated tee, players face a decision: challenge a large bunker guarding the corner with a driver, or lay up with a 3-wood for a safer, longer approach. Aggressive lines can lead to out-of-bounds left, while bailing right may leave an awkward downhill lie for the second shot; the elevated green slopes subtly from back to front, breaking towards the city.

Later, the 14th hole is a memorable Par 3, typically stretching around 175 yards, demanding a precise shot over water to a well-bunkered green. The tee shot must carry a pond that fronts the green, with deep bunkers protecting both the left and right flanks. Selecting the right long iron or hybrid is crucial here, heavily influenced by daily pin position and any prevailing wind; the green itself features multiple tiers and generally slopes slightly towards the water hazard.

The finishing 18th hole, a Par 5 playing roughly 520 yards, offers a dramatic conclusion to the round. The tee shot is downhill to a seemingly wide fairway, which progressively tightens towards the second landing zone. A creek crosses the fairway at approximately 280 yards and again near the green, while out-of-bounds looms on the right. Length off the tee is advantageous but must be coupled with precision to avoid the initial creek; the large, gently sloping green often plays faster than it appears, particularly on its front edge.

## Practical Info

Green fees at Sendai Ichibancho Latte range from approximately ¥12,000 to ¥18,000 on weekdays, increasing to ¥18,000-¥25,000 on weekends and public holidays. The course is open to the public, though advance booking is highly recommended, especially for weekend slots. Online reservation through major golf booking platforms is generally available and encouraged.

Play is primarily cart-based, with self-drive electric carts typically included in the green fees. Walking with a caddie can be arranged with prior notice for an additional fee, though caddies are often mandatory for premium packages or during peak season. The dress code requires collared shirts, soft spikes only, and prohibits denim on the course. Jackets are often suggested for dining in the main clubhouse restaurant.

## Access

Sendai Ichibancho Latte is conveniently accessible from Sendai Station, the nearest major train hub. From Sendai Station, a drive of approximately 30-45 minutes will bring you to the course, covering a distance of about 15-20 kilometers. For those driving, ample free parking is available on-site, making it a practical option for visitors with rental cars.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse at Sendai Ichibancho Latte offers a comfortable and well-appointed experience. Locker rooms are modern and kept meticulously clean, providing a pleasant space for pre- and post-round preparations. A spacious communal Japanese bath (ofuro) is available for relaxation after your round, a common and welcome feature at many Japanese courses. The restaurant focuses on fresh local ingredients, with the Miyagi specialty 'gyu-tan' (grilled beef tongue) being a popular choice, alongside seasonal seafood options, all enjoyed in a setting that offers views of the course. The pro shop stocks a reasonable selection of golf apparel, equipment, and local souvenirs.

## Tips

For the most pleasant playing conditions, target your visit for spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November), when temperatures are mild and the surrounding scenery is at its most vibrant. Common golfer mistakes often include underestimating the subtle elevation changes and misjudging the speed of the Bentgrass greens; always consider an extra club on uphill approaches and pay close attention to break on putts. Summer can bring high humidity and the risk of typhoons, while winter may see colder temperatures and potential snow closures, so checking the forecast is essential. Weekends during peak spring and autumn foliage periods will command the highest green fees, so consider weekday play for better value and potentially less crowded conditions. Utilizing a caddie, particularly on a first visit, can significantly enhance your experience by providing invaluable course knowledge.
