**Introduction:**

Southern Links Golf Club stands as a jewel in Okinawa's golfing crown. More than just a golf course, it's an experience that blends challenging play with the serene beauty of the Okinawan coastline. The meticulously manicured fairways and greens, combined with the strategic layout designed to test even the most seasoned golfer, create a truly memorable round. From the moment you arrive, you'll be captivated by the breathtaking views and the impeccable service, making Southern Links a must-visit destination for any golf enthusiast traveling to Okinawa. The course evokes a sense of prestige and exclusivity while still maintaining a welcoming atmosphere, making it perfect for both serious golfers and those looking to enjoy a relaxing day on the links.

**Price & Booking:**

Green fees at Southern Links vary depending on the season, day of the week, and whether you're staying at the affiliated hotel. Expect weekday rounds to range from ¥25,000 to ¥35,000 JPY, while weekend and holiday rates can climb to ¥40,000 - ¥50,000 JPY. Twilight rates are sometimes available and offer a more affordable option.

Booking is highly recommended, especially during peak seasons (spring and autumn). You can book directly through the official Southern Links website, major online golf booking platforms (Rakuten GORA, GDO), or through your hotel concierge if you're staying at a nearby resort. Booking in advance ensures you secure your preferred tee time and avoids disappointment. It's worth noting that booking well in advance (several months) can sometimes yield early bird discounts. Consider golfing during the shoulder seasons (early spring or late autumn) for pleasant weather and potentially lower rates.

**Course Accessibility:**

Southern Links operates as a semi-private course. While membership is available, the course is also open to the public, especially those staying at the associated hotels or booking through established golf tourism agencies. Non-members may face some restrictions on tee time availability, particularly during peak periods. However, with advance booking, access is generally straightforward. Check directly with the club for the most up-to-date policies regarding non-member access. They frequently offer packages and deals for visitors.

**Course Highlights:**

The course at Southern Links is a masterpiece of design, skillfully integrated into the rugged coastal landscape. The architect (although often uncredited, local sources suggest involvement from a respected Japanese firm specializing in links-style courses) has created a strategic layout that demands accuracy and thoughtful club selection.

Several holes stand out as signature challenges. The par-3 3rd hole, perched dramatically on a cliff edge, requires a precise shot over the crashing waves to a small green guarded by bunkers. The prevailing winds make club selection crucial here. The par-4 9th, a dogleg left with a stunning ocean backdrop, demands a well-placed tee shot to avoid the strategically positioned fairway bunkers. The approach shot to the green is equally challenging, requiring accuracy and a good read of the wind. The back nine continues the theme of challenging coastal golf, culminating in the demanding par-5 18th, which requires three well-executed shots to reach the green in regulation. The undulating fairways and strategically placed bunkers throughout the course ensure that every round is a unique test of golfing skill. The greens are known for their speed and subtle breaks, adding another layer of complexity to the game.

**Facilities:**

Southern Links boasts exceptional facilities designed to enhance the overall golfing experience. The clubhouse is a luxurious haven, offering panoramic views of the course and the ocean. Inside, you'll find a well-stocked pro-shop featuring a wide range of golf equipment, apparel, and souvenirs. The restaurant serves a delectable array of Japanese and international cuisine, with a focus on fresh, local ingredients. Enjoying a meal on the terrace after your round, overlooking the breathtaking scenery, is an experience in itself.

However, the true highlight of the facilities is the traditional Japanese Onsen (hot spring bath). Soaking in the therapeutic waters after a round of golf is the perfect way to relax and rejuvenate tired muscles. The Onsen features both indoor and outdoor baths, offering stunning views of the ocean. The locker rooms are spacious and well-appointed, providing a comfortable and convenient space to change and freshen up. Overall, the facilities at Southern Links are second to none, ensuring a truly luxurious and memorable experience.

**Scenic Beauty:**

The location of Southern Links on the southern tip of Okinawa is truly spectacular. The course is surrounded by breathtaking views of the turquoise ocean, rugged cliffs, and vibrant coral reefs. The best time to visit is during the spring (March-May) or autumn (September-November) months when the weather is mild and pleasant. During these seasons, the skies are clear, and the ocean is a dazzling shade of blue. The natural beauty of the surrounding landscape provides a stunning backdrop for your round of golf.

The course itself is meticulously maintained, with lush green fairways and pristine greens. The contrast between the vibrant green of the course and the deep blue of the ocean creates a visually stunning experience. Keep an eye out for the local wildlife, including various species of birds and even the occasional sea turtle. The natural beauty of Okinawa is truly captivating, and Southern Links provides the perfect setting to appreciate it.

**Access:**

Southern Links is located in Yaese-cho, on the southern part of Okinawa Island. The most convenient way to reach the course is by car. From Naha Airport (OKA), the drive takes approximately 45-60 minutes, depending on traffic. Take the Okinawa Expressway (Route 58) south, then follow the signs to Yaese-cho. Ample parking is available at the course.

Alternatively, you can take a taxi from Naha Airport, which will cost approximately ¥6,000 - ¥8,000 JPY. Some hotels also offer shuttle services to the course. While public transportation options are available, they are less convenient and require multiple transfers. Renting a car is highly recommended for exploring Okinawa and accessing Southern Links.
