**Price & Booking:**

Green fees at Nasu Ogawa Golf Club typically range from ¥25,000 to ¥45,000 on weekdays and ¥35,000 to ¥60,000 on weekends and holidays. These prices are subject to change depending on the season, promotions, and tee time availability. It's highly recommended to book well in advance, especially during peak seasons such as spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November) when the foliage is at its most vibrant. Booking can be done primarily through phone (+81 287-96-2121). A Japanese speaker is required. Online booking is generally unavailable. Seasonal tips: Consider visiting during the shoulder seasons (late March or early December) for potentially lower rates and fewer crowds. Packages that include accommodation at nearby hotels or ryokans can offer excellent value. Also, inquire about twilight rates for discounted play in the late afternoon. Caddies are typically mandatory, adding to the overall cost.

**Course Accessibility:**

Nasu Ogawa Golf Club is a semi-private course. The course at Nasu Ogawa Golf Club is a masterpiece of design, masterfully crafted to challenge and reward golfers of all abilities. The strategic layout features undulating fairways, strategically placed bunkers, and challenging water hazards that demand accuracy and thoughtful club selection. While the specific architect's name is not widely publicized, the course's design philosophy clearly emphasizes a harmonious blend of natural beauty and strategic play.

Signature Holes:

*   **Hole 7 (Par 3):** A visually stunning par 3 that requires a precise tee shot over a ravine to a well-protected green. The elevation change and surrounding scenery make this a truly memorable hole.
*   **Hole 12 (Par 4):** A dogleg right par 4 that demands a well-placed tee shot to set up an approach to a challenging green guarded by bunkers and water. Strategy is key here.
*   **Hole 18 (Par 4):** The finishing hole is a picturesque par 4 that winds its way back towards the clubhouse. A strong drive is essential to avoid the fairway bunkers, leaving a manageable approach to a receptive green. The pressure is on to finish strong in front of the clubhouse patrons!

The course boasts Bentgrass greens known for their smooth, consistent putting surfaces. The fairways are also meticulously maintained, providing excellent lies. Overall, Nasu Ogawa provides a very fair yet demanding test of golf.
