## Course Overview

Kagoshima Takachiho presents an 18-hole, par-72 layout carved into the dramatic foothills of Kirishima. While the original designer's name isn't widely publicized, the course skillfully integrates the natural topography, offering a playing experience that demands strategic thought. Total yardage typically ranges around 6,700-7,100 yards from the championship tees.

The fairways are generally maintained with a resilient Zoysia grass, providing a firm surface, while the greens feature Bentgrass for a consistent putting experience. Signature characteristics include significant elevation changes, panoramic views of the Kirishima mountain range, and strategic bunkering that often comes into play on approaches.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating Kagoshima Takachiho requires careful club selection and an appreciation for its elevation shifts. The 4th hole, a par-4, demands a precise tee shot from an elevated position to a fairway that doglegs subtly left, with a hidden bunker guarding the left side of the landing area. The approach shot then crosses a natural depression to a two-tiered green, often requiring one more club than the yardage suggests.

The 12th hole, a scenic par-3, is played significantly downhill over a valley to a green framed by mature pines. Wind can be a significant factor here, often requiring players to take one less club and aim for the center of the green to account for the downward trajectory and potential gusts. Putting on this green often features subtle breaks towards the valley floor.

The 17th, a reachable par-5, begins with an uphill drive followed by a downhill second shot on a relatively narrow fairway. Mature forest flanks both sides, punishing errant shots, and a water hazard protects the front-left of the green. A precise tee shot is crucial for any attempt to reach in two, while a conservative layup should aim to stay short of the water, leaving a short pitch into a green that can be deceptively fast given the natural slopes.

## Practical Info

Green fees for Kagoshima Takachiho generally range from JPY 10,000 to JPY 15,000 on weekdays, and JPY 18,000 to JPY 25,000 on weekends and holidays, typically including a shared cart. The course is open to public play, but booking several weeks in advance is highly recommended, especially for weekend tee times, which can be secured through online platforms or a local travel agent.

Riding carts are standard and often equipped with GPS, aiding navigation across the varied terrain; walking with a caddie is an option but less common due to the significant elevation changes. A standard golf dress code is observed, requiring collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft spikes on the course. Caddies are available upon request and offer valuable insights into the course's nuances, particularly regarding the mountain-influenced breaks and hidden hazards.

## Access

Kagoshima Takachiho is conveniently situated near Kagoshima Airport (KOJ), which is the most practical entry point for most travelers. From the airport, the course is approximately a 20-30 minute drive. For those arriving by train, Kokubu Station on the JR Nippo Main Line is the closest major station, roughly a 40-minute drive away. Ample parking is available on-site for both personal vehicles and rental cars.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse at Kagoshima Takachiho offers a comfortable and functional space for pre- and post-round activities. Modern, well-maintained locker facilities are provided for changing and securing belongings. A particular highlight is the traditional Japanese bath (onsen), fed by local hot springs, offering a genuinely restorative experience after a round on the challenging terrain. The on-site restaurant features regional cuisine, with dishes often highlighting local Kagoshima Black Pork or showcasing the fresh mountain vegetables from the Kirishima area.

## Tips

The most pleasant playing conditions at Kagoshima Takachiho are typically found in Spring (April-May) and Autumn (October-November), when temperatures are mild and the scenery is particularly vibrant. A common mistake here is underestimating the effect of elevation changes on club selection for approach shots; always factor in uphill or downhill lies. While winters are generally mild, chilly winds can be present at higher elevations, and summer months bring significant heat and humidity, along with the occasional risk of typhoons. Expect green fees to be at their peak during the popular cherry blossom season and throughout the autumn foliage period.
