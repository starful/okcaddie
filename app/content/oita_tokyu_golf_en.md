## Course Overview

Oita Tokyu Golf, established by the Tokyu Group, is a well-regarded 18-hole championship course nestled in the natural beauty of Oita Prefecture. While the exact design date and architect are not widely publicized for this resort-style course, it exhibits the hallmarks of a classic Japanese layout from the late 20th century, focusing on harmony with the existing landscape. The course measures approximately 6,800 yards from the back tees, playing to a par of 72, with shorter options available for varying skill levels. Fairways are typically Zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface, while the greens are manicured Bentgrass, ensuring consistent and true rolls. Its overall character is defined by mature forests, gentle elevation changes, and thoughtfully placed hazards that demand strategic shot-making rather than brute power.

## Layout & Strategy

Oita Tokyu Golf presents a diverse set of challenges, requiring careful club selection and course management on many holes. The design makes excellent use of the natural terrain, with many holes framed by trees and subtle elevation changes.

**Hole 1 – Par 4, 385 yards:** Kicking off your round, the first hole is a moderate par 4 that doglegs gently to the right. The tee shot here should favor the left side of the fairway to avoid a cluster of trees guarding the corner on the right, which can block your approach. A fairway wood or long iron might be a prudent choice for accuracy, leaving a mid-iron into a relatively flat green protected by bunkers front-right and left. The green itself has subtle undulations, often breaking more than it appears towards the valley below.

**Hole 7 – Par 3, 175 yards:** This scenic par 3 demands a precise carry over a natural ravine or dry creek bed to a well-guarded green. The wind can often be a factor here, swirling through the surrounding trees, so pay close attention to the flag and any guidance from your caddie. Club selection could range from a 7-iron to a 5-iron depending on conditions, aiming for the center of the green to avoid penal bunkers short and left. The putting surface slopes gently from back to front, so keeping your ball below the hole offers the best chance for a birdie or a comfortable par.

**Hole 11 – Par 5, 530 yards:** A true three-shot par 5 for most players, this hole meanders through a valley with a series of tight landing areas. The tee shot requires accuracy to find a relatively narrow fairway, with trees flanking both sides that penalize errant drives. Your second shot is often a layup, needing to clear a small stream or ditch that crosses the fairway around 250 yards from the green. The approach to the green is uphill and protected by a bunker on the right, making a precise short iron or wedge crucial. The large green has multiple tiers, so knowing the pin position is essential for avoiding a challenging two-putt.

**Hole 16 – Par 4, 410 yards:** As you approach the closing stretch, this challenging par 4 demands both length and precision from the tee. The fairway slopes slightly from right to left, and a pond lurks down the left side, coming into play for drives pushed or hooked. A solid drive down the right-center of the fairway is ideal, opening up the angle to the green. The approach shot is typically a mid-to-long iron into an elevated green, making club selection critical. Bunkers guard both sides of the green, and any shot short or long can lead to a difficult up-and-down from a collection area.

## Conditions & Seasonality

The best times to experience Oita Tokyu Golf are during the spring months of April and May, and the autumn months of October and November. During these periods, temperatures are comfortable, humidity is low, and the course conditions are typically at their peak, showcasing vibrant greenery in spring or stunning foliage in autumn. Summer, from June through September, can be quite hot and humid, with the possibility of heavy rainfall and typhoons, though morning rounds can still be enjoyable. Winter golf (December to March) is generally playable, but expect cooler temperatures and some frost delays on occasion, particularly in the early morning. Pace of play is generally good in Japan, with most four-ball rounds completing within 4.5 to 5 hours, often assisted by course marshals. Weekday rounds offer a more relaxed atmosphere and often faster pace compared to the busier weekend and holiday schedules.

## Green Fees & Booking

As a "Value for Money" option, Oita Tokyu Golf offers accessible pricing, especially considering the quality of the resort experience. Weekday green fees for visitors typically range from 8,000 JPY to 14,000 JPY per person, which often includes a shared cart and occasionally lunch as part of a package. Weekend and holiday rates are higher, usually falling between 12,000 JPY and 20,000 JPY per person. These prices almost always include a shared electric cart, which is often mandatory. Caddie services are usually available as an optional extra, adding approximately 6,000 JPY to 12,000 JPY per player when shared among a group of three or four. While the course is open to visitors, booking is predominantly done through Japanese online golf reservation platforms like Rakuten GORA and GDO (Golf Digest Online). International visitors may find it easier to book through a golf tour operator specializing in Japan, who can navigate the Japanese-language websites and handle payment. Direct booking without Japanese language skills can be challenging.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Oita Tokyu Golf adheres to standard Japanese golf course etiquette regarding dress and on-course conduct. Players are expected to wear collared shirts (tucked in for men), golf-specific trousers or shorts, and spikeless golf shoes. Denim, t-shirts, and overly casual sportswear are generally not permitted on the course or in the clubhouse dining areas. Mobile phone usage on the course should be discreet and limited to emergencies, with phone calls strictly avoided on the greens or in areas close to other players. Players are encouraged to maintain a good pace of play, keep up with the group ahead, and practice "ready golf." It is also expected that golfers will repair divots, rake bunkers, and respect the course condition. Adherence to these rules ensures a pleasant experience for all golfers and maintains the course's reputation for orderliness.

## Access

Oita Tokyu Golf is conveniently located in Oita Prefecture, accessible primarily by car. The nearest major transportation hub is Oita Station, a key station on the JR Kyushu Nippo Main Line, which offers connections throughout Kyushu. From Oita Station, the course is approximately a 30 to 40-minute drive, depending on traffic. For those traveling from further afield, Oita Airport (OIT) serves as the closest air gateway, with domestic flights connecting from major cities like Tokyo (Haneda and Narita), Osaka (Itami and Kansai), and Nagoya. From Oita Airport, the drive to the golf course typically takes between 45 to 60 minutes. Direct travel from major Honshu cities like Osaka, Kobe, Hiroshima, or Tokyo would involve a domestic flight to Oita Airport followed by a drive, as direct driving is impractical due to distance and ferry requirements. The course provides ample parking facilities for guests arriving by private vehicle or rental car, which is highly recommended for exploring the region.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Oita Tokyu Golf offers a welcoming and comfortable environment, reflecting its resort designation. Inside, you'll find well-maintained locker rooms with sufficient space for your belongings and a clean, spacious shower area. As is customary in Japan, a communal bath (ofuro) is available, providing a relaxing soak after your round, often with views of the surrounding natural landscape. The main restaurant within the clubhouse serves a variety of Japanese and Western dishes, focusing on fresh, local ingredients. Expect regional Oita specialties such as Toriten (Oita's famed chicken tempura) or dishes featuring local Bungo beef, alongside classic golf course fare like curry rice, udon noodles, and tonkatsu. A range of beverages, including local sake and beers, are also available. The dining area often features large windows, offering pleasant views of the course or the surrounding mountains, making it an ideal spot to reflect on your round.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Oita Tokyu Golf with a caddie, especially for your first visit, is highly recommended to enhance your experience and navigate its nuances effectively. Common mistakes here include underestimating the subtle elevation changes, leading to misjudged carries or approaches, and misreading greens that often break more than they appear due to the underlying topography. The tree-lined fairways demand accurate tee shots, and caddies excel at guiding you to the optimal landing zones to avoid being blocked out. They possess invaluable local knowledge regarding hidden hazards, the ideal club for each approach, and precise green reads that can save you strokes. For instance, many greens will have a general slope towards the valley or a specific mountain range, and a caddie will point this out. This course is particularly well-suited for mid-to-low handicap players who enjoy a strategic game where precision is rewarded over sheer power. Higher handicap players will also find it enjoyable, especially with a caddie's guidance, as the course is fair but demands thoughtful shot placement. It's a great choice for golfers who appreciate a well-maintained forest course with natural beauty and a relaxing resort atmosphere, rather than those seeking wide-open links-style challenges.
