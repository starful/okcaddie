## Course Overview
Oita Tokyu Golf features 18 holes, playing to a par of 72. Total yardage typically ranges from 6,500 to 6,800 yards from the back tees, offering a balanced challenge for various skill levels. While specific designer and opening year details are not widely publicized, the course reflects classic Japanese golf design principles.

Fairways are generally zoysia grass, providing a firm base for approach shots, while the greens are typically bentgrass, known for their smooth and true roll. As a "Forest Course," its signature characteristics include tree-lined fairways, often requiring precision off the tee, and changes in elevation that present engaging shot-making opportunities. The "Resort" aspect is reflected in its well-maintained facilities and tranquil natural setting, contributing to its "Value for Money" appeal.

## Layout & Strategy
**Hole 5, Par 4:** This hole presents an elevated tee shot looking down a dogleg left fairway. While tempting to cut the corner over the trees, a hidden bunker awaits aggressive drives on the left side of the landing area. A safer play is a 220-240 yard fade with a hybrid or 3-wood, positioning you for an unobstructed approach to a two-tiered green.

**Hole 12, Par 3:** A picturesque mid-length par 3, often playing downhill to a green guarded by bunkers front-left and a subtle slope on the right. Club selection is crucial; consider taking one less club than usual due to the elevation drop. The putting surface features a gentle ridge through the middle, making accurate reads from the sides essential.

**Hole 16, Par 5:** A true test, this par 5 often plays uphill with a slight dogleg right. The tee shot demands accuracy to avoid dense forest on both sides, with the ideal landing zone favoring the left side of the fairway. The second shot is often a lay-up short of a ravine or deep bunker protecting the green's front, setting up a short iron approach.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Oita Tokyu Golf typically range from ¥8,000 to ¥12,000 on weekdays, and ¥12,000 to ¥18,000 on weekends and holidays, often including a shared power cart. Public reservations are generally available via online portals or direct phone booking, usually several months in advance. The standard mode of play is via a two-seater electric cart, often equipped with GPS navigation.

The dress code follows conventional golf etiquette: collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft-spike golf shoes are expected. Denim is generally discouraged. While most rounds are self-guided with a cart, caddies can sometimes be requested for an additional fee, subject to availability and advance booking.

## Access
Oita Tokyu Golf is located within Oita City. The nearest major train station is Oita Station, approximately a 30-40 minute drive from the course. For travelers arriving by air, Oita Airport (OIT) is about a 60-75 minute drive, offering connections to major Japanese cities.

From Fukuoka, a primary regional hub, driving to Oita Tokyu Golf typically takes around 2 to 2.5 hours, depending on traffic, via the Oita Expressway. Ample free parking is available on-site for personal vehicles.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse at Oita Tokyu Golf provides comprehensive facilities for golfers. Spacious locker rooms are available for changing, and a relaxing communal bath, characteristic of Japanese golf clubs, offers a soothing experience after your round. While not a full-fledged onsen, the bath provides a traditional Japanese unwinding opportunity. The restaurant serves a variety of Japanese and Western dishes; local specialties, such as *toriten* (Oita's tempura chicken) or *ryukyu* (sashimi marinated in soy sauce), are often featured on the menu, providing a taste of the region.

## Tips
The ideal months to play Oita Tokyu Golf are typically spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November) when temperatures are mild and the surrounding forest foliage is at its most vibrant. Common mistakes include underestimating the elevation changes and failing to respect the narrow tree-lined fairways, leading to lost balls or challenging recovery shots. Oita's climate can be humid, especially in summer (June-August), and afternoon showers are possible, so check forecasts. Be aware that peak season (spring/autumn and major holidays) will see the higher end of the green fee range and require earlier booking.
