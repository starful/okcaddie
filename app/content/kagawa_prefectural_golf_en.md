## Course Overview
Kagawa Prefectural Golf, located in Takamatsu, offers an 18-hole, par-72 layout set amidst the region's undulating hills. While the original designer is not widely known, the course opened to provide accessible golf to the public, focusing on integrating the natural mountain terrain. Fairways are typically Zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface, while the greens are likely Bent grass, common in the region for a consistent roll.

This mountain-style course is characterized by significant elevation changes, requiring strategic shot-making and often presenting engaging downhill or uphill approaches. From the regular white tees, the course typically plays around 6,400 to 6,800 yards, providing a fair test for a range of handicaps. Its key characteristic is the combination of thoughtful routing through valleys and ridges with often expansive views of the surrounding Kagawa landscape.

## Layout & Strategy
The par-4 5th hole, often playing longer than its stated yardage, demands an accurate uphill tee shot to a landing area that may hide a left-side bunker. A precise mid-iron is then required to a two-tiered green, where putting lines break more than they appear. Later, the par-3 12th presents a shorter but intimidating downhill shot that must carry a deep ravine to a well-guarded green, with swirling winds a frequent factor.

On the back nine, the par-5 16th is a dogleg-right hole that requires careful club selection off the tee. Playing a driver too far can result in a blocked second shot, making a strategic layup with a fairway wood or long iron crucial to set up a reachable third shot. Navigating these holes well can significantly impact your score.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Kagawa Prefectural Golf are notably budget-friendly, typically ranging from 7,000-12,000 JPY on weekdays and 10,000-18,000 JPY on weekends and public holidays. The course is fully public, allowing for easy online or phone reservations, though booking well in advance for peak times is advisable. All rounds include riding carts, often equipped with GPS, and walking is generally not an option due to the course's topography.

The dress code is standard for Japanese golf courses: collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and no denim. Soft spike golf shoes are mandatory. While caddies are an available option for an additional fee, most visitors play without one, utilizing the cart's GPS for yardages and course information.

## Access
Kagawa Prefectural Golf is conveniently located a moderate drive from Takamatsu city center. From Takamatsu Station, expect a drive of approximately 30-45 minutes, depending on traffic. If arriving by air, Takamatsu Airport is roughly 45-60 minutes away by car.

While there isn't a direct train station adjacent to the course, the journey by car or taxi from the city offers pleasant views of the rural Kagawa landscape. Ample on-site parking is available for guests driving themselves to the facility.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse provides functional and clean locker room facilities, ensuring a comfortable experience before and after your round. A highlight for many golfers is the traditional Japanese public bath (ofuro), often a relaxing soak in hot water, perfect for easing muscles after a round on the hilly course. The restaurant offers a selection of local and Japanese comfort foods, with Sanuki Udon — Kagawa's renowned thick wheat noodles — being a popular and satisfying choice.

## Tips
For the most comfortable weather, consider visiting in spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November), when temperatures are mild and the scenery is at its best. A common mistake here is misjudging yardages due to the significant elevation changes, so trust your rangefinder and club down on downhill shots. Summer brings heat and humidity, while winter can be chilly, although Kagawa generally experiences milder conditions compared to other parts of Japan. Peak season pricing applies during weekends, holidays, and the prime spring/autumn months.
