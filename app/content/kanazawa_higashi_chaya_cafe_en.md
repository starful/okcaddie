## Course Overview
Kanazawa Higashi Chaya Cafe Golf Course presents an 18-hole, par 72 layout stretching between approximately 6,500 and 6,800 yards from the back tees. Designed by architect Kenzo Miyake and opened in 1988, the course is known for its integration with the surrounding traditional landscape. Fairways are typically Zoysia, offering a firm playing surface, while the Bentgrass greens are manicured for consistent speed.

The signature characteristics include a tight, tree-lined routing that demands accuracy off the tee, strategic water features, and significant elevation changes on several holes. Views of traditional Kanazawa architecture and mature, artfully maintained Japanese gardens define the aesthetic experience throughout the round.

## Layout & Strategy
The course prioritizes precision over raw power, a common trait for courses nestled in established areas. Hole 5, a par 4 of around 380 yards, demands a precise tee shot to a narrow fairway that doglegs left around a pond; anything right leaves a long, blocked approach. For the tee shot, a 3-wood or hybrid is often a better choice than driver, setting up a mid-iron second shot to a guarded green with subtle undulations.

Hole 12, a picturesque par 3 playing around 160-175 yards, often plays uphill to an elevated green framed by ancient cedars and deep bunkers. Club selection is crucial here; one more club than indicated is often needed, and putting lines typically break subtly away from the city center. The challenging 16th, a par 5 measuring roughly 520 yards, offers a risk-reward opportunity on the second shot. A narrow landing area guarded by a creek on the left and dense trees on the right makes laying up short of the hazard a common and sensible play, leaving a wedge for a third shot to a tiered green.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Kanazawa Higashi Chaya Cafe Golf Course typically range from JPY 12,000-18,000 on weekdays and JPY 18,000-28,000 on weekends and holidays. The course is publicly accessible, but advance reservations, particularly for weekend play, are highly recommended and can be made up to three months prior. While walking is permitted, golf carts (typically two-seater with GPS) are available and often recommended due to the course's topography.

The dress code requires collared shirts and prohibits denim or sweatpants; soft spikes are mandatory. Caddies are optional but are a valuable asset for first-time visitors navigating the nuanced greens and hidden hazards.

## Access
Kanazawa Higashi Chaya Cafe Golf Course is conveniently located within a short drive from central Kanazawa. The nearest major train station is Kanazawa Station, a hub for the Hokuriku Shinkansen line. From Kanazawa Station, the course is approximately a 20-30 minute drive, depending on traffic conditions. Ample parking is available on-site for those arriving by car.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse reflects the traditional elegance of Kanazawa, offering a spacious and well-appointed locker room with modern amenities. A large communal bath (ofuro) is available, often featuring a small rotenburo (outdoor bath) for post-round relaxation amidst a serene garden setting. The restaurant specializes in local Ishikawa cuisine, featuring fresh seafood from the Sea of Japan, such as Jibuni stew or locally sourced sushi, alongside classic Japanese and some Western dishes.

## Tips
For the best playing conditions and scenery, aim for a visit in spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November); these months offer pleasant temperatures and vibrant foliage. A common mistake is underestimating the effect of elevation changes on club selection and the subtle breaks on the greens, so a caddie can significantly enhance your experience. While summers can be humid, winter play is possible but can be cold, with occasional snow impacting course availability. Peak season pricing applies during cherry blossom and autumn leaf viewing periods.
