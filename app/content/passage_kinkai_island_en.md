**Price & Booking:**

A round at Passage Kinkai Island is an investment in an extraordinary experience. Green fees typically range from ¥25,000 to ¥45,000 per person, depending on the day of the week and the season. Weekday rates are generally lower than weekend rates. During peak season (spring and autumn), expect higher prices and increased demand, so booking well in advance is essential. Twilight rounds, offered later in the afternoon, can provide a more affordable option while still allowing you to enjoy the course's beauty. Package deals, including accommodation and multiple rounds of golf, offer excellent value for those planning a longer stay. Booking can be conveniently done online through the Passage Kinkai Island official website or via major Japanese golf booking platforms. Pro-tip: Keep an eye out for seasonal promotions and special offers, which can significantly reduce the cost of your visit.

**Access:**

Getting to Passage Kinkai Island requires a bit of planning, but the journey is well worth the effort. The most convenient way to access the island is via Nagasaki Airport (NGS). From the airport, you can take a direct bus to Nagasaki Station (approximately 40 minutes). From Nagasaki Station, take the JR Nagasaki Line to Isahaya Station (approximately 20 minutes). At Isahaya Station, transfer to the JR Omura Line to Kinkai Station (approximately 45 minutes). From Kinkai Station, the resort offers a complimentary shuttle service to Passage Kinkai Island (approximately 10 minutes).

Alternatively, you can rent a car at Nagasaki Airport and drive to the Iojima Ferry Terminal (approximately 1 hour). From the ferry terminal, take the ferry to Iojima Island (approximately 30 minutes). Once on the island, Passage Kinkai Island is a short drive away. Driving offers more flexibility, but the ferry schedule should be checked in advance. The resort also offers private helicopter transfers from Nagasaki Airport for those seeking the ultimate in convenience and luxury. Detailed travel information and ferry schedules can be found on the Passage Kinkai Island official website.
