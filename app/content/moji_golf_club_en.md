## Course Overview
Moji Golf Club, established in 1934, is a prestigious 18-hole, par-72 layout designed by the renowned C.H. Alison. This historic course in Kitakyushu, Fukuoka, typically plays around 6,800 to 7,100 yards from the championship tees. Fairways are predominantly zoysia, offering firm lies, while the greens feature bentgrass for consistent putting surfaces. A defining characteristic is its classic strategic bunkering and undulating terrain, reflective of Alison's architectural philosophy.

## Layout & Strategy
The 5th hole, a par-4, demands precision from the tee with a gentle dogleg right; stray left and you'll find a series of Alison's signature deep bunkers guarding the turn. A controlled 3-wood or hybrid off the tee can set up a mid-iron approach to a green that slopes subtly from back to front. The picturesque 13th, a par-3, often plays into a prevailing coastal breeze, requiring a confident long iron to a well-guarded green with steep fall-offs. Pay close attention to your caddie's read on the breaks, which can be deceptive near the Kanmon Strait.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Moji Golf Club are typically in the range of JPY 28,000-45,000 on weekdays and JPY 40,000-60,000 on weekends and holidays, inclusive of caddie and cart where available. As a private club, guest play is generally restricted to introductions by members or through specific high-end travel packages. Play is predominantly walking with a mandatory caddie, enhancing the traditional golf experience. A smart casual dress code applies on the course, with collared shirts and trousers required, while a jacket may be expected in the clubhouse dining areas.

## Access
Moji Golf Club is located in Kitakyushu, Fukuoka, approximately a 20-30 minute drive from the city center. For visitors arriving from broader Kyushu, it's about an hour's drive north from Fukuoka's Hakata Station, primarily via the Kyushu Expressway. The closest major train station is Moji-ko Station, about 15-20 minutes away by taxi. Ample parking is available on-site for those driving.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse at Moji Golf Club offers a refined and traditional ambiance, commensurate with its historic status. Locker rooms are spacious and well-appointed, providing a comfortable space before and after your round. A large communal bath (o-furo) invites relaxation, a customary part of the Japanese golf experience. The restaurant typically serves a range of high-quality Japanese cuisine, often featuring local seafood specialties from the Kanmon Strait, alongside classic clubhouse dishes prepared with fresh, seasonal ingredients.

## Tips
For the most pleasant conditions, consider visiting Moji Golf Club during the spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November), when temperatures are mild and the course foliage is vibrant. Be mindful of the coastal winds, which can significantly impact club selection, particularly on exposed holes. Always defer to your caddie's advice on green reading and local course nuances, as their insights are invaluable on this strategic layout. Peak season and public holiday play will command the higher end of the green fee range.
