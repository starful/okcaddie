## Course Overview

Moji Golf Club holds a distinguished place in Japanese golf history, founded in 1929 by the visionary Osamu Ueda. As one of the earliest courses in the country, it was conceived during a period of significant industrial growth in the Kitakyushu region, offering a leisure amenity for local business leaders and foreign residents. The course features 18 holes, playing to a par of 72, with yardages typically ranging from 6,400 to 6,800 yards from the regular tees, stretching beyond 7,000 yards from the championship markers.

The fairways are predominantly Zoysia grass, known for its resilience and firm playing surface, while the greens are meticulously maintained Bentgrass, ensuring consistent and true rolls. Moji’s character is that of a classic parkland course, thoughtfully routed through rolling hills with mature trees lining many fairways. The design prioritizes strategic shot-making over brute force, demanding precision and course management, all while offering glimpses of the Seto Inland Sea from higher elevations.

## Layout & Strategy

Moji Golf Club's layout presents a nuanced challenge, where each hole requires careful consideration. Here are a few notable examples:

**Hole 3: Par 4, 380-410 yards.** This dogleg left hole demands an accurate tee shot to position players for an approach to an elevated green. Trees guard the left side of the fairway, threatening to block out any tee shot cut too severely, while bunkers lie in wait for a less-than-perfect approach. A well-placed drive, favoring the right-center, allows for a mid-iron second shot to a green that slopes subtly from back to front, requiring a precise read.

**Hole 7: Par 3, 160-180 yards.** A picturesque yet intimidating par three, often played from an elevated tee box to a green set significantly below. The main challenge here is club selection, with the downhill trajectory and potential for coastal winds to impact ball flight. The green is protected by deep bunkers on both sides, making par a good score if the tee shot finds the putting surface.

**Hole 12: Par 5, 500-530 yards.** This gentle dogleg right par five offers a genuine birdie opportunity for those who can navigate its hazards. The tee shot should favor the left side to open up the fairway around the bend, but longer hitters must beware of a series of bunkers on the right. Aggressive players might attempt to reach in two, but a pond short and right of the green, alongside more bunkers, makes a strategic layup a prudent choice for many.

**Hole 17: Par 4, 420-450 yards.** A formidable penultimate hole, this long par four typically plays into the prevailing wind, requiring two strong shots to reach the green. Fairway bunkers are strategically placed to catch drives that stray from the center, leaving a challenging long iron or hybrid approach. The green itself is often multi-tiered with subtle breaks, making par a commendable achievement on this demanding hole.

## Conditions & Seasonality

Moji Golf Club maintains its grounds to a high standard throughout the year, but the experience varies with the seasons. The best months for play are typically spring (April to May) when the weather is mild, cherry blossoms may adorn the landscape, and the course is in prime condition. Autumn (October to November) also offers pleasant temperatures and vibrant fall foliage, making for an aesthetically pleasing round.

The coastal location means that wind can be a significant factor, particularly on more exposed holes, adding an extra layer of challenge. While generally drier than other parts of Japan, the rainy season (June and early July) can make fairways softer and reduce roll. Pace of play is consistently efficient, with rounds typically completed in 4 to 4.5 hours with a caddie, reflecting the Japanese golf etiquette of maintaining speed. Weekdays tend to be less crowded than weekends and public holidays, which often see the course reserved for members or corporate events.

## Green Fees & Booking

As a historic private club, Moji Golf Club operates with a traditional membership structure, meaning visitor access is more restricted than at public resort courses. Green fees for visitors typically range from ¥18,000 to ¥28,000 on weekdays, and ¥25,000 to ¥38,000 on weekends and holidays. These rates generally include the mandatory caddie fee and shared electric cart (if applicable, though Moji is primarily a walking course with manual pull carts for caddies).

Visitor play is often contingent on a member introduction, or arrangements made through established golf travel agencies like OKCaddie, who can leverage their relationships to secure limited tee times. Direct online booking for non-members, in the style of Rakuten GORA, is generally not available for such exclusive clubs. Planning several months in advance is advisable to secure preferred dates, especially during peak seasons.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Moji Golf Club upholds traditional golf attire standards, reflecting its private club status. Players are expected to wear collared shirts (tucked in), golf trousers, or appropriate golf skirts/shorts (no denim, cargo shorts, or athletic tracksuits are permitted). Soft spikes are mandatory, and changing shoes in the parking lot is not allowed. In the clubhouse, a jacket is often preferred, particularly in the main dining areas.

On-course etiquette includes silence for mobile phones; calls should be taken discreetly away from play, and extensive use is discouraged. Maintaining pace of play is paramount; groups are expected to keep up with the flight ahead, and caddies will provide gentle reminders if pace begins to lag. Respect for the course and fellow golfers is a cornerstone of the Moji experience.

## Access

Moji Golf Club is conveniently located in Kitakyushu, Fukuoka Prefecture, making it accessible from various points in Kyushu and Honshu. The nearest major train stations are Mojiko Station (JR Kagoshima Line) or Kokura Station (for Shinkansen bullet train connections), both approximately a 20-30 minute taxi or local train ride to the club. Shin-Shimonoseki Station, just across the Kanmon Straits, is also a short drive away for those arriving from Honshu via Shinkansen.

Driving times are reasonable for those based in the region. From Hakata Station in Fukuoka City, the drive typically takes around 1 hour, depending on traffic. Travelers coming from Hiroshima can expect a drive of approximately 2.5 to 3 hours, crossing the Kanmon Bridge. While driving from Osaka or Kobe is possible, it is a significant journey (around 6-7 hours without breaks); most visitors from these areas would opt for the Shinkansen to Kokura or Hakata, followed by local transport. The club provides ample parking facilities for those arriving by car.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Moji Golf Club offers facilities that align with its historic and premium character. The locker rooms are spacious, immaculately clean, and equipped with individual lockers, providing a comfortable and private space for changing. After a round, the traditional Japanese public bath (ofuro) is a highlight, offering a serene experience for relaxation and rejuvenation. While not a natural hot spring (onsen), the large communal soaking tubs are perfect for easing tired muscles.

The clubhouse restaurant provides a diverse menu, balancing classic Japanese dishes with some Western options. For lunch, players can expect hearty set meals featuring staples like tonkatsu, tempura, curry rice, and local noodle dishes, all prepared with fresh, often regional ingredients. The dining area offers pleasant views of the course, creating an inviting atmosphere for post-round meals and refreshments. Expect attentive service that reflects the club's dedication to its members and guests.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Moji Golf Club with a caddie is not just a luxury; it is integral to the experience and often a requirement for visitors. One common mistake players make is misjudging the subtle elevation changes, particularly on approach shots, leading to clubs being short or long. The greens, while appearing relatively flat, possess nuanced breaks that are difficult for an unfamiliar eye to discern without local knowledge.

Caddies at Moji are a fount of local knowledge. They provide invaluable advice on ideal target lines from the tee, especially on doglegs where unseen hazards or preferred landing areas exist. Their understanding of the turf and prevailing wind conditions is crucial for accurate club selection. More importantly, they excel at reading the Bentgrass greens, often saving players strokes with precise guidance on speed and break. This course suits players who appreciate strategic golf and a walking experience. Mid to low handicappers will find the course a compelling test of skill, while higher handicappers can still enjoy the beauty and history, but should be prepared for a challenge that rewards thoughtful play over sheer power.
