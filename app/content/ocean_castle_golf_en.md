## Course Overview
Ocean Castle Golf is typically an 18-hole, par 72 layout. The course yardage generally spans around 6,500 to 6,800 yards from the regular tees, extending closer to 7,000 yards from the championship markers. While specific details on the original designer and opening year are not widely available, the course is recognized for its integration with Okinawa's natural landscape.

The playing surfaces typically feature Bermuda grass for the fairways and greens, a resilient choice well-suited for Okinawa's subtropical climate. Its defining characteristics, as indicated by its name, are its elevated position offering broad Pacific Ocean vistas and a commitment to providing a good golfing experience at a competitive price point. The course is known for leveraging the island's natural contours to create engaging holes.

## Layout & Strategy
The course presents a blend of strategic challenges and scenic backdrops, often featuring elevation changes and coastal breezes. Here are a few holes that illustrate its character:

**Hole 3 (Par 4, around 380 yards):** This dogleg right often features an elevated tee shot, giving a glimpse of the ocean beyond the fairway. A precise drive with a 3-wood or hybrid is recommended to position the ball before the dogleg, avoiding a cluster of bunkers on the right side. The approach shot typically plays to a green protected by subtle mounding and a gentle slope from back to front, requiring careful distance control.

**Hole 7 (Par 3, around 165 yards):** Often cited as one of the course's most picturesque, this par 3 can be deceptive. An elevated tee offers a clear view of the green with the ocean as a backdrop, but swirling winds can make club selection challenging. Aim for the center of the green, as it often has slight undulations and subtle run-offs that can penalize off-center strikes, especially if the wind pushes your ball towards the periphery.

**Hole 14 (Par 5, around 510 yards):** A medium-length par 5 that begins with an uphill tee shot to a generous landing area. The second shot offers choices: a lay-up short of a fairway bunker complex or an aggressive attempt to reach a wider section of the fairway, potentially setting up a wedge for the third shot. The green itself is often subtly contoured, demanding careful reading of putts, particularly for those approaching from the front.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Ocean Castle Golf typically range from JPY 8,000-12,000 on weekdays and JPY 12,000-18,000 on weekends and holidays. These prices can fluctuate based on season, time of day, and any ongoing promotions. The course is open to the public, though booking well in advance, particularly for weekend morning slots, is strongly advised to secure your preferred tee time.

Rounds are generally played using a riding cart, which often includes a GPS navigation system to aid with distances and course layout. While caddies may be available upon prior request and for an additional fee, self-play with a cart is the predominant mode. The dress code adheres to standard golf etiquette: collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft-spiked golf shoes are required; denim is typically not permitted on the course.

## Access
Ocean Castle Golf is conveniently located in Nakagusuku, Okinawa. As Okinawa lacks a comprehensive train network outside of Naha's monorail, personal or rented vehicle transportation is essential.

From Naha, the capital city and main airport hub (Naha Airport, OKA), the course is approximately a 35-45 minute drive, covering about 25-30 kilometers, depending on traffic conditions. The route typically involves driving north on major arteries such as Route 58 or the Okinawa Expressway. Ample free parking is available on-site for all visitors.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse at Ocean Castle Golf provides the expected amenities for a comfortable golf experience. Inside, you'll find well-maintained locker rooms for changing and securing personal belongings, ensuring a smooth transition from arrival to the first tee. After your round, a communal Japanese-style bath (ofuro) is available, offering a relaxing soak to soothe muscles. The on-site restaurant serves a selection of local Okinawan cuisine, with the "Okinawa Soba" often highlighted on the menu for its savory broth and tender pork, providing a taste of local flavor.

## Tips
For the most enjoyable experience, consider visiting Ocean Castle Golf during the shoulder seasons of April-May or October-November. These periods offer pleasant temperatures and a reduced risk of typhoons, which can significantly disrupt travel and play during summer. Golfers should be prepared for the omnipresent ocean winds; these can dramatically alter club selection, especially on elevated tees and exposed fairways.

A common mistake is misjudging the elevation changes on the course; some uphill shots will play longer, and downhill shots shorter than their measured distance. During summer months (June-September), expect high humidity and heat, along with the peak of typhoon season, so always check the local weather forecasts. Weekends and Japanese public holidays typically see the highest green fees and busiest tee sheets, making weekday play a more economical and less crowded option.
