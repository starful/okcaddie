Kotohira Golf Club in Manno, Kagawa is a **visitor-friendly Shikoku** option—close enough to pair with Kotohira-gū (Konpira-san) sightseeing without needing a private-club invitation.

## Who it's for

- Golfers touring **Shikoku** who need a reliable bookable round
- Groups combining temple stairs / old-town walks with one golf day
- Mid-handicappers who like elevation changes and decision holes

Skip if you only want ocean-link wind—this is inland hill golf with views, not a seaside links.

## Course overview

Hills create uneven lies and forced club-ups on uphill approaches. Fairway bunkers sit on the greedy line; laying up to a flat stance often beats hero carries. Korai fairways reward clean contact; bent greens ask for pace control more than huge reads.

Typical day: cart play, lunch package options, baths. Confirm which tees your group should use—back tees stretch mid-handicappers on uphill holes.

## Green fees & booking

Shikoku value clubs still spike on holidays. **Planning ranges**:

- **Weekday**: often roughly mid-¥10,000s
- **Weekend**: high-¥10,000s to low-¥20,000s depending on lunch/caddie

## Access

- **Rail**: Kotohira Station + taxi (bags); shrine town hotels work well for morning tees
- **Air**: Takamatsu (TAK) → about **45–70 minutes** by car to the Manno / Kotohira area
- From Takamatsu city, plan **50–80 minutes** drive buffer

Do the Konpira climb on a non-golf day if possible—legs + early tee is a rough combo.

## Dress code & tips

- Standard Japanese dress; soft spikes

Kotohira GC is **easy-booking Shikoku golf** with a sensible stay-and-play hook near the shrine town. Lock a portal quote, taxi from Kotohira, and play the uphill holes one club stronger.
