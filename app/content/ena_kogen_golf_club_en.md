**Introduction:**

Ena Kogen Golf Club, perched high in the breathtaking highlands of Gifu Prefecture, offers a golfing experience that transcends the ordinary. More than just a round of golf, it’s an immersion into the natural beauty of Japan, coupled with a meticulously designed course that rewards strategic play and provides a genuine challenge for golfers of all abilities. While not commanding the stratospheric green fees of some of Japan's most exclusive clubs, Ena Kogen boasts a charm and playability that makes it a favorite among discerning local players and adventurous international visitors. Its value-for-money proposition is undeniable, especially when considering the breathtaking scenery and on-site accommodation options. The air is clean, the views are spectacular, and the golf is consistently rewarding. This is a course that lingers in the memory long after the final putt has dropped.

**Price & Booking:**

Ena Kogen Golf Club offers remarkably reasonable green fees, especially considering the quality of the course and its stunning location.

*   **Weekday Green Fees:** Expect to pay between ¥8,000 and ¥12,000 per player. This often includes caddie fees and consumption tax.
*   **Weekend & Holiday Green Fees:** Prices typically range from ¥12,000 to ¥18,000 per player, including caddie fees and consumption tax.
*   **Seasonal Tips:** The best deals are often found during the shoulder seasons (spring and autumn). Booking during these times not only avoids the peak season crowds but also allows you to experience the most vibrant colours of the surrounding landscape. Winter play is possible, weather permitting, and often offers significant discounts.
*   **Booking Channels:** While direct booking is possible via phone, proficiency in Japanese is highly recommended. The easiest options for international visitors are via online platforms such as Golf Digest Online or Rakuten GORA. These platforms offer English translations and streamlined booking processes. Securing a tee time well in advance, especially on weekends and holidays, is crucial. Many packages include lunch, making it an even better value.

**Course Accessibility:**

Ena Kogen Golf Club operates as a **public course**. This accessibility makes it an excellent option for visiting golfers who may not have access to private member clubs. While tee times can be competitive, especially during peak seasons, the open nature of the club ensures that anyone can experience its unique charm and challenging layout. The club occasionally hosts public tournaments, providing an opportunity to test your skills against other players in a competitive yet friendly environment.

**Course Highlights:**

The course at Ena Kogen is a masterpiece of strategic design, thoughtfully integrated into the rolling highland terrain. While the architect is not particularly famous internationally, their expertise in Japanese golf course design shines through in every hole. The layout presents a variety of challenges, demanding precision off the tee and strategic approaches to the greens. Water hazards come into play on several holes, adding to the excitement and requiring careful club selection.

*   **Signature Hole (Example):** The par-3 17th hole is often cited as the signature hole. Played from an elevated tee, it offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. The green is well-protected by bunkers, and the swirling winds can make club selection tricky. Successfully navigating this hole requires both skill and a bit of luck.
*   **Strategic Layout:** The course is not overly long, but it demands accuracy and thoughtful course management. The fairways are generally well-maintained, and the greens are known for their smooth surfaces and subtle breaks. Expect undulating greens that require a keen eye and a confident putting stroke.
*   **Elevation Changes:** The highland location naturally lends itself to significant elevation changes, providing dramatic views and adding another layer of complexity to the game. Be prepared to adjust your club selection accordingly, as the elevation can significantly impact the distance of your shots.

**Facilities:**

Ena Kogen Golf Club boasts a range of facilities designed to enhance the overall golfing experience.

*   **Clubhouse:** The clubhouse offers a comfortable and welcoming atmosphere. It features spacious locker rooms, comfortable seating areas, and a well-stocked pro shop.
*   **Restaurant:** The on-site restaurant serves a delicious selection of Japanese cuisine, with an emphasis on local specialties. After a round of golf, there's nothing better than relaxing with a cold beer and enjoying a hearty meal while taking in the stunning views. Many set course meal options are available.
*   **Pro-Shop:** The pro shop carries a wide range of golfing equipment, apparel, and accessories. Whether you need a new set of clubs or simply a souvenir to remember your visit, you're sure to find something of interest.
*   **Onsen/Bath:** Perhaps the most appealing feature of Ena Kogen is its exceptional onsen (hot spring bath). After a challenging round, soaking in the naturally heated waters is the perfect way to soothe tired muscles and unwind. The onsen facilities are meticulously maintained and offer both indoor and outdoor bathing options. This is a quintessential Japanese experience that should not be missed. The hot spring water is sourced from deep underground and is believed to have therapeutic properties.
* **Practice Facilities:** The club provides a driving range and practice putting green to fine tune your game.

**Scenic Beauty:**

Ena Kogen Golf Club is truly a feast for the eyes. The course is surrounded by lush forests, rolling hills, and majestic mountains.

*   **Best Season to Visit:** While the course is open year-round, the best time to visit is during the spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November). In spring, the cherry blossoms create a stunning backdrop, while in autumn, the vibrant foliage transforms the landscape into a kaleidoscope of colors. Summer offers pleasant temperatures, but it can also be more crowded.
*   **Nature:** The course is home to a variety of wildlife, including birds, deer, and even monkeys. Keep an eye out for these creatures as you make your way around the course.
*   **Surrounding Views:** The panoramic views from the course are simply breathtaking. On a clear day, you can see for miles in every direction. The surrounding mountains provide a dramatic backdrop, creating a truly unforgettable golfing experience. The Japanese Alps loom large on the horizon.

**Access:**

Getting to Ena Kogen Golf Club requires a combination of train and taxi or rental car.

*   **From Nagoya:** Take the JR Chuo Line from Nagoya Station to Ena Station (approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes). From Ena Station, take a taxi to the golf club (approximately 30 minutes). Alternatively, renting a car at Nagoya Station is a convenient option, allowing you to explore the surrounding area at your own pace. The drive from Nagoya to Ena Kogen Golf Club takes approximately 1 hour and 45 minutes.
*   **Detailed Directions (Driving):** From Nagoya, take the Tomei Expressway to the Ena Interchange. From the Ena Interchange, follow Route 19 towards Iwamura. The golf club is located on the left-hand side of the road, approximately 15 minutes from the interchange. The route is well-signposted in Japanese and English. Consider using a GPS navigation system.
