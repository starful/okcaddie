**Introduction:**

Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course stands as a testament to the enduring appeal of Hokkaido golf. Less internationally known than some of its illustrious neighbors, Wattsu quietly delivers a golfing experience that is both challenging and immensely enjoyable, particularly for those seeking value without compromising on quality. This is not about ostentatious luxury; it’s about honest, well-designed golf in a naturally beautiful setting. The course seamlessly blends into the rolling hills of Kitahiroshima, offering a true escape from the hustle of city life, a characteristic that defines the very essence of Hokkaido golf. What makes Wattsu special is its accessibility and focus on providing a pleasant round for all skill levels, while still presenting ample strategic challenges to keep experienced golfers engaged. The spirit of 'omotenashi' – Japanese hospitality – is deeply ingrained in the staff's dedication to ensuring every golfer feels welcome and taken care of.

**Price & Booking:**

Green fees at Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course are remarkably reasonable, making it an excellent option for budget-conscious golfers or those seeking value for money.

*   **Weekday Green Fees:** Expect to pay between ¥8,000 and ¥12,000, depending on the season and any ongoing promotions.
*   **Weekend & Holiday Green Fees:** Weekend rates typically range from ¥12,000 to ¥18,000. It's always advisable to book in advance, especially during peak season.

**Seasonal Tips:**

*   **Spring (April-May):** The course opens in spring, usually around late April or early May, after the snow melts. The weather can be unpredictable, so be prepared for cooler temperatures and the possibility of rain. Green fees are often lower during this shoulder season.
*   **Summer (June-August):** This is the peak season, with warm temperatures and long daylight hours. The course is in its best condition, but expect higher green fees and more competition for tee times. Book well in advance.
*   **Autumn (September-October):** Autumn is arguably the best time to play, with pleasant temperatures, stunning fall foliage, and fewer crowds than in summer. Green fees may be slightly lower than in summer.
*   **Winter (November-March):** The course is closed during winter due to heavy snowfall.

**Booking:**

Booking a tee time is relatively straightforward. You can book directly through the Sapporo Golf Club website (Japanese language only, often needing a Japanese speaker to assist), or more conveniently, through popular golf booking services like Rakuten GORA or Golf Digest Online. These platforms usually have English language options and allow you to compare prices and availability. It's recommended to book at least a few weeks in advance, especially during peak season.

**Course Accessibility:**

Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course is a semi-private course. While it has members, it is open to the public and welcomes visitors. It is very easy to book tee times via popular booking sites. This accessibility makes it a popular choice for both local residents and tourists looking to experience golf in Hokkaido.

**Course Highlights:**

The Wattsu Course is a meticulously designed 18-hole course that presents a balanced challenge for golfers of all skill levels. While the architect is not as widely celebrated as some of the more internationally renowned names, the course's strategic layout and thoughtful design are a testament to their expertise.

*   **Strategic Layout:** The course features a strategic blend of long and short holes, demanding both power and precision. The fairways are generally generous, but strategically placed bunkers and water hazards require careful club selection and shot placement.
*   **Signature Holes:**
    *   **Hole 5 (Par 4):** This demanding par 4 requires an accurate tee shot to avoid the fairway bunkers on either side. The approach shot is played to a well-protected green, making par a rewarding score.
    *   **Hole 12 (Par 3):** A visually stunning par 3, featuring a tee shot over a pond to a slightly elevated green. Club selection is crucial, as the wind can play a significant factor.
    *   **Hole 18 (Par 5):** A classic finishing hole, this par 5 offers a risk-reward opportunity for long hitters. A well-placed tee shot can set up an attempt to reach the green in two, but beware of the strategically placed bunkers guarding the green.

**Facilities:**

Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course boasts a range of facilities designed to enhance the overall golfing experience.

*   **Clubhouse:** The clubhouse is comfortable and well-appointed, offering a welcoming atmosphere for golfers to relax before and after their round.
*   **Restaurant:** The restaurant serves a variety of Japanese and Western dishes, using fresh, local ingredients. Enjoy a delicious meal while overlooking the stunning views of the course.
*   **Pro-Shop:** The pro shop is well-stocked with a wide range of golf equipment, apparel, and accessories from leading brands.
*   **Practice Facilities:** The course offers a driving range and putting green, allowing golfers to warm up before their round.
*   **Onsen/Bath:** After a long day on the course, relax and rejuvenate in the course's onsen (hot spring bath). This is a quintessential Japanese experience and the onsen at Wattsu is excellent, with both indoor and outdoor bathing options. The onsen water is sourced from a natural hot spring and is believed to have therapeutic benefits.

**Scenic Beauty:**

The Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course is located in a picturesque setting, surrounded by the natural beauty of Hokkaido.

*   **Best Season to Visit:** Autumn is the most visually stunning time to visit, with the surrounding forests ablaze with vibrant fall colors. However, the course is beautiful throughout the spring and summer months as well.
*   **Nature:** The course is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and birds. Keep an eye out for these creatures as you play your round.
*   **Surrounding Views:** The course offers stunning views of the surrounding Hokkaido mountains and forests. The fresh air and tranquil atmosphere make for a truly relaxing and enjoyable golfing experience. The sunsets over the mountains are particularly spectacular.

**Access:**

Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course is located in Kitahiroshima, which is conveniently accessible from Sapporo.

*   **From Sapporo:**
    *   **By Car:** The easiest way to reach the course is by car. It takes approximately 45-60 minutes to drive from Sapporo, depending on traffic. Take the Hokkaido Expressway and exit at the Kitahiroshima IC.
    *   **By Train & Taxi:** Take the JR Chitose Line from Sapporo Station to Kitahiroshima Station (approx. 20 minutes). From Kitahiroshima Station, take a taxi to the golf course (approx. 15 minutes).
*   **From New Chitose Airport:**
    *   **By Car:** The drive from New Chitose Airport is approximately 30-45 minutes.
    *   **By Train & Taxi:** Take the JR Rapid Airport train from New Chitose Airport Station to Kitahiroshima Station (approx. 20 minutes). Then take a taxi to the course.

**Final Verdict:**

Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course is a hidden gem that offers an exceptional golfing experience at an affordable price. Its strategic layout, well-maintained conditions, and stunning scenery make it a must-play for any golfer visiting Hokkaido. The combination of a challenging course, excellent facilities, and breathtaking natural beauty makes Wattsu a true standout.
