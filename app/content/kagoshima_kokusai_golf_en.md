## Course Overview

Kagoshima Kokusai Golf, established in 1974, provides a quintessential example of Kyushu's accessible mountain golf. This 18-hole, par-72 layout stretches approximately 6,550 yards from the regular tees, offering a balanced challenge without being overly demanding. While the original designer's name is not widely publicized, the course reflects classic Japanese design principles, integrating seamlessly with the natural topography.

The course features Bentgrass greens, known for their smooth rolling surfaces, and Zoysia grass fairways, which provide a firm stance and resilient playing conditions throughout much of the year. Its character is defined by significant elevation changes, strategic doglegs, and panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, truly embodying its "Mountain Style" tag. Golfers can expect a round where accurate shot-making and thoughtful club selection are rewarded over sheer power.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating Kagoshima Kokusai requires a keen eye for elevation and an understanding of how the ball will react on mountain terrain. Each hole presents its own set of strategic considerations, making for an engaging round.

The 3rd hole, a par-4 measuring around 380 yards, demands an accurate tee shot to a landing area that slopes from right to left. A deep bunker guards the left side, catching anything pulled, while dense woods line the right, penalizing pushes. The second shot is often played slightly uphill to a green with subtle undulations, requiring a precise mid-iron and a read that accounts for the ascent.

Hole 7, a par-3 of approximately 165 yards, is a visually striking downhill shot over a valley. The challenge here is judging the yardage accurately; players often over-club, forgetting the significant drop in elevation. The green is protected by bunkers short and left, with a steep runoff behind, making a tee shot to the heart of the green the safest play.

The 11th, a par-5 stretching about 520 yards, begins with a tight, uphill tee shot, urging players to keep the ball straight to avoid the tree-lined rough. The second shot offers a decision: lay up short of a cross-bunker that bisects the fairway, or attempt to carry it to position for a wedge into the green. The green itself is multi-tiered, demanding an approach that lands on the correct level to avoid a challenging two-putt.

Finally, the 16th hole, a par-4 around 410 yards, features a pronounced dogleg left with a severe downhill slope from tee to green. The tee shot needs to hug the left side to shorten the hole, but an overzealous cut can find trouble in the dense woods. The approach shot is a classic downhill scenario, where selecting one less club than usual is often prudent to avoid flying the green, which is guarded by a collection area short and a bunker long-right.

## Conditions & Seasonality

Kagoshima Kokusai Golf generally maintains its playing surfaces to a good standard, reflecting its commitment to delivering value. The Zoysia fairways hold up well through various conditions, while the Bentgrass greens offer consistent speeds.

The ideal playing seasons are spring (April to early June) and autumn (October to November), when temperatures are mild and rainfall is less frequent. Summer months (July to September) can be quite humid and hot, typical of southern Kyushu, though early morning rounds can mitigate the heat. Winters are generally mild, allowing for year-round play, though occasional colder snaps can occur from December to March. Wind can be a factor on this mountain course, particularly in exposed areas, so checking local forecasts is advisable. Pace of play on weekdays is generally relaxed, with rounds often completed in four to four and a half hours. Weekends tend to be busier, with more local groups, which might slightly extend playing times, especially during peak seasons.

## Green Fees & Booking

As a "Value for Money" course, Kagoshima Kokusai Golf offers attractive green fee packages, particularly outside of peak holiday periods. Weekday green fees for visitors typically range from ¥8,000 to ¥14,000, including cart usage. Weekend and national holiday rates usually fall between ¥12,000 and ¥19,000. These prices are quite competitive for a scenic 18-hole course in Japan.

The course is generally open to visitors, though priority for prime tee times may sometimes be given to local members. Most rounds include a shared two-seater or four-seater electric cart as standard, often equipped with GPS for yardages. Caddies are typically not available for individual rounds at courses like Kagoshima Kokusai; the standard play is self-guided with the provided cart. Booking is most conveniently done through popular Japanese online golf reservation platforms such as Rakuten GORA or GDO (Golf Digest Online). These platforms allow you to compare various packages, including lunch options, and secure your preferred tee time well in advance. Direct booking through the course's official website, if available, is another option, though often in Japanese.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Adherence to standard Japanese golf course etiquette is expected at Kagoshima Kokusai Golf. A collared shirt is mandatory, typically tucked in, along with tailored trousers or shorts. Denim jeans, tracksuits, and T-shirts are not permitted on the course or in the clubhouse. While metal spikes are generally phased out across Japan, spikeless golf shoes are the preferred and often required footwear.

On the course, maintaining a brisk pace of play is important. Keep up with the group in front, and be prepared to wave through faster players if you fall behind. Mobile phone use is permitted, but it is expected that phones remain on silent mode and calls are kept brief and discrete, away from other golfers. Beyond calls, using phones for GPS apps or scoring is common. Respect for the course condition is also paramount; remember to replace divots, repair pitch marks on greens, and rake bunkers thoroughly after use. These courtesies ensure a positive experience for all players.

## Access

Kagoshima Kokusai Golf is situated in Aira, within Kagoshima Prefecture on the southern island of Kyushu. The most practical way for international visitors to reach Kagoshima is by flying into Kagoshima Airport (KOJ). From Kagoshima Airport, the course is a relatively short drive, approximately 30-40 minutes by rental car or taxi.

For those traveling from further afield within Japan, domestic flights connect Kagoshima Airport to major cities like Tokyo (Haneda/Narita), Osaka (Itami/Kansai), and Fukuoka. Driving from cities like Fukuoka on Kyushu's north side takes around 2.5 to 3 hours via the Kyushu Expressway. Attempting to drive from cities such as Osaka, Kobe, Hiroshima, or especially Tokyo, is not practical for a golf trip; these distances are substantial, requiring many hours of driving or multiple days of travel. Instead, flying to Kagoshima Airport and then renting a car is the recommended approach. The course provides ample free parking for visitors, making self-driving a convenient option once you are in the Kagoshima region. The nearest major train station is Kagoshima-Chuo Station, from which the course is about a 50-minute drive.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Kagoshima Kokusai Golf offers a comfortable and functional environment typical of many Japanese courses. Upon arrival, golfers are directed to well-maintained locker rooms where they can change and store their belongings securely. These facilities are clean and practical, providing a standard level of amenity for pre and post-round preparations.

A highlight of many Japanese golf clubs, and often found here, is the spacious communal bath, or _ofuro_. After a round, especially in cooler weather, soaking in the large bath is a refreshing way to relax muscles and unwind. While not always a natural hot spring _onsen_, these baths offer a similar experience. The clubhouse restaurant typically serves a range of Japanese and Western-style dishes. Expect traditional Japanese golf course fare such as Tonkatsu (fried pork cutlet), Curry Rice, and various noodle dishes like Soba or Udon. Many clubs in Kagoshima also feature local specialties, so look for dishes incorporating Kagoshima's famous Kurobuta pork or fresh seafood. Lunch is often included in certain booking packages, providing a convenient break between the front and back nine. The dining area usually offers pleasant views of the course, enhancing the overall experience.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Kagoshima Kokusai requires more than just hitting the ball straight; understanding its mountain characteristics is key. A common mistake here is misjudging elevation changes, often leading to over-clubbing on uphill shots and under-clubbing on downhill approaches. Always factor in at least one club more for significant uphill plays and one less for downhill shots.

Local knowledge suggests that many greens have subtle breaks that are influenced by the surrounding mountains, often making putts break towards lower ground or the nearest body of water, even if it doesn't appear so visually. Pay close attention to your playing partners' putts to gauge these unseen tendencies. The prevailing wind can also be tricky through the valleys, shifting direction unexpectedly, so observe flags and trees carefully before selecting your club. This course is particularly well-suited for mid-handicappers (generally 10-20) who appreciate a scenic and thoughtful round of golf without extreme length or penal hazards. Players who enjoy walking a varied terrain, using their irons and wedges precisely, and are looking for a good value experience will find Kagoshima Kokusai quite rewarding. It may be less ideal for absolute beginners seeking a flat, straightforward layout, or for scratch golfers looking for a true championship test.
