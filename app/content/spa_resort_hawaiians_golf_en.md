**Introduction:**

Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf, located in Iwaki, Fukushima, is more than just an afterthought to the famous Spa Resort Hawaiians. It's a genuinely enjoyable and surprisingly challenging golf course that offers exceptional value, particularly when considered as part of a stay-and-play package. Don't let the name fool you; while the resort embraces a Hawaiian theme, the golf course itself is distinctly Japanese, with meticulously manicured greens, strategic bunkering, and a thoughtful layout that will test golfers of all skill levels. It's a hidden gem, often overlooked but certainly worthy of attention for those seeking an affordable and relaxing golfing experience in a unique setting. The course offers a surprisingly tranquil escape amidst the vibrant energy of the resort.

**Price & Booking:**

Weekday green fees at Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf typically range from ¥8,000 to ¥12,000, while weekend fees are slightly higher, ranging from ¥10,000 to ¥15,000. These prices are estimates and fluctuate based on the season and time of day. Booking in advance is highly recommended, especially during peak seasons such as Golden Week (late April/early May) and the autumn foliage season (late October/early November). The best value can often be found by booking a stay-and-play package through the Spa Resort Hawaiians website or through major Japanese golf booking sites like Golf Digest Online or Rakuten GORA. Keep an eye out for seasonal promotions and early booking discounts. Weekday play is generally less crowded, offering a more relaxed pace. Playing early in the morning can also help you avoid the heat during the summer months.

**Course Accessibility:**

Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf is a **public** golf course, welcoming both resort guests and outside visitors. While there isn't a formal membership program, the course is often frequented by locals and those enjoying a getaway at the Spa Resort Hawaiians. Tee times can be booked online, by phone, or through the resort concierge. Securing your tee time in advance is always recommended, especially on weekends and holidays.

**Course Highlights:**

Designed with a strategic layout, Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf presents a fair but challenging test for golfers of all abilities. While the architect's name may not be widely known internationally, the course showcases a deep understanding of Japanese golf course design principles. The fairways are generally forgiving, but strategically placed bunkers and water hazards demand accuracy off the tee and on approach shots. The greens are known for being well-maintained and relatively fast, requiring a deft touch with the putter.

A signature hole is arguably the par-3 17th. This stunning hole requires a precise tee shot over a water hazard to a well-protected green. The backdrop of the surrounding hills adds to the hole's scenic beauty and makes it a memorable challenge. Another noteworthy hole is the par-5 9th, a dogleg right that rewards a well-placed drive with the opportunity to reach the green in two. However, strategically placed bunkers guard the approach, demanding a precise second shot. Throughout the course, elevation changes and subtle undulations add to the challenge and visual appeal.

**Facilities:**

The clubhouse at Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf is modern and well-appointed, offering a comfortable place to relax before and after your round. The restaurant serves a variety of Japanese and Western dishes, with local specialties often featured on the menu. The pro shop is well-stocked with golf equipment, apparel, and souvenirs. However, the real highlight of the facilities is the access to the Spa Resort Hawaiians' famed **onsen (hot springs)**. After a round of golf, there's nothing quite like soaking in the therapeutic waters of the onsen to soothe tired muscles and relax your mind. The quality of the onsen is exceptional, with a variety of different baths offering unique experiences. This post-round onsen experience truly sets Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf apart from other courses. Shower facilities are also top-notch, with amenities such as shampoo, conditioner, and body wash provided. Locker rooms are spacious and well-maintained.

**Scenic Beauty:**

The best time to visit Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf is during the spring (April-May) or autumn (late October-November). In the spring, the surrounding hills come alive with vibrant green foliage and cherry blossoms. In the autumn, the course is awash in a tapestry of reds, oranges, and yellows. The course itself is meticulously maintained, with lush green fairways and manicured greens. The views of the surrounding hills and forests are stunning, providing a peaceful and tranquil backdrop to your round. While not directly on the coast, the course benefits from the fresh air and mild climate of the Fukushima region. The sound of birdsong and the gentle breeze add to the overall sense of serenity.

**Access:**

The most convenient way to access Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf is by car. From Tokyo, take the Joban Expressway to the Iwaki-Yumoto Interchange (IC). From the IC, it's approximately a 15-minute drive to the golf course. Alternatively, you can take the JR Joban Line to Iwaki-Yumoto Station. From the station, a free shuttle bus is available to the Spa Resort Hawaiians, which is a short walk from the golf course. The journey from Tokyo by train takes approximately 2.5 to 3 hours. Parking is readily available at the golf course. The resort also offers transportation services for its guests. Driving directions are clearly marked on major roads and highways.

**Final Verdict:**

Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf offers a surprisingly enjoyable and affordable golfing experience in a unique resort setting. While it may not be a world-renowned championship course, it provides a solid test of golf and a relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. The combination of a well-maintained course, strategic layout, and access to the renowned Spa Resort Hawaiians' onsen makes it a truly memorable experience. It's particularly well-suited for travelers seeking a stay-and-play getaway that combines golf with relaxation and cultural immersion. If you're looking for a hidden gem in Fukushima Prefecture, Spa Resort Hawaiians Golf is definitely worth considering. For the value and the overall experience, I highly recommend this course, especially for those who appreciate a good onsen after a round of golf. It's a unique Japanese golf experience that won't break the bank.
