Carrying a full golf travel bag through Tokyo trains is miserable. Japan’s **takkyubin** (宅急便) courier network—Yamato (black cat), Sagawa, and similar—is the practical way to move clubs between **airport → hotel → golf course → next city**.

## Quick facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **What it is** | Door-to-door (or counter-to-door) parcel delivery for luggage |
| **Best use** | Golf travel bags, suitcases, spare shoes |
| **Typical lead time** | Often next-day between major cities if dropped early; same-day is limited |
| **Cost (planning)** | Roughly **¥2,000–¥5,000+** per golf bag depending on size zone and speed |
| **Where to ship** | Hotels, ryokan, golf clubs, airport delivery counters |
| **Language tip** | Staff are used to “golf bag” (ゴルフバッグ) shipments |

## Who this guide is for

- Visitors playing **two+ courses** or moving cities with clubs
- Anyone connecting **Narita/Haneda/KIX** by rail who does not want clubs on the train
- Golfers staying downtown who will only need clubs on course day

If you rent clubs every round, you may not need this—but delivery still helps for suitcases on multi-city trips.

## How the flow usually works

1. **Land with clubs** (or buy/rent later)
2. At the airport delivery counter—or that evening at your hotel front desk—fill a waybill to your **next hotel** or directly to a **golf club**
3. Keep valuables (passport, laptop, rangefinder if fragile/expensive) with you
4. On departure day, ship bags ahead to the airport counter or next city before you ride empty-handed

Many golf clubs accept inbound takkyubin if labeled with your **name + play date + tee time**. Call or have the hotel confirm the club’s receiving desk hours.

## Timing rules that matter

- **Next-day** between major metros is common when you drop off before the cutoff (often late afternoon—ask)
- **Rural / island / remote Kyushu–Hokkaido** legs can take longer—build **2 days** for peace of mind before an early tee
- **Never** assume Sunday night drop-off arrives for a Monday 7:00 tee without asking
- Peak travel weeks (GW, Obon, New Year) delay both couriers and hotel receiving

For tee logistics overall, pair this with [booking tips for Japan](/guide/booking-tips-japan).

## Packing for courier (not airline) reality

- Use a proper **golf travel cover**; soft bags need extra padding at the head end
- Soft spikes and shoes can ride inside if weight allows; check size rank on the waybill
- Remove lithium battery extras from pockets if shipping spare electronics
- Write the destination in **clear romaji + Japanese address** from your hotel confirmation

Hotels will usually help rewrite the address label—ask politely at the front desk.

## Cost & payment

Prices scale by **size category + distance zone + speed**. A single golf travel bag on a Tokyo↔Osaka next-day move often lands in a few-thousand-yen band; longer or oversized ranks cost more. Pay by cash or card depending on counter. Keep the receipt / tracking number.

## Common mistakes

- Shipping to a golf club that is **closed the day before** your round—bags sit or bounce
- Using only the course name without **phone number and play date**
- Cutting timing so tight that a weather delay to courier trucks ruins the tee
- Putting irreplaceable items in the shipped bag

## Bottom line

Takkyubin is the **default logistics tool** for Japan golf travel with your own clubs. Ship early, label with play date, confirm the receiving desk, and ride the shinkansen without a coffin-sized bag at your knees.
