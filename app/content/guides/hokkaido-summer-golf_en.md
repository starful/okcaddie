# Cool Summer Golf Escapes in Hokkaido: Your Guide to Japan's Northern Golf Paradise

Hokkaido, Japan's northernmost island, is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and, increasingly, its world-class golf courses. While much of Japan swelters in the summer heat and humidity, Hokkaido offers a cool and refreshing escape, making it an ideal destination for golfers seeking respite and exceptional playing conditions. This guide explores the best summer golf escapes Hokkaido has to offer, helping you plan the perfect golfing vacation.

## Why Choose Hokkaido for Summer Golf?

*   **Cooler Temperatures:** Hokkaido enjoys significantly lower temperatures and humidity levels compared to the rest of Japan during the summer months (June-August). This makes for a much more comfortable and enjoyable golfing experience, allowing you to focus on your game without battling oppressive heat. Average temperatures during this time typically range from the low 60s to mid 70s Fahrenheit (16-24 Celsius).

*   **Stunning Scenery:** Hokkaido's landscapes are diverse and spectacular, ranging from rolling hills and lush forests to dramatic coastlines and volcanic mountains. Many golf courses are designed to take full advantage of these scenic surroundings, offering breathtaking views from every tee box and fairway. Imagine teeing off with a snow-capped volcano as your backdrop!

*   **World-Class Golf Courses:** Hokkaido boasts a high concentration of top-rated golf courses designed by renowned architects. These courses offer a variety of challenges for golfers of all skill levels, from championship layouts to more relaxed resort courses.

*   **Delicious Food:** After a round of golf, indulge in Hokkaido's famous cuisine, known for its fresh seafood, dairy products, and locally grown produce. From succulent crab and uni (sea urchin) to creamy ice cream and flavorful ramen, Hokkaido offers a culinary experience that will tantalize your taste buds.

*   **Relaxation and Rejuvenation:** Hokkaido offers a wide range of activities beyond golf, including hiking, fishing, onsen (hot springs), and exploring national parks. This makes it the perfect destination for a relaxing and rejuvenating vacation that combines golf with other enriching experiences.

## Top Golf Courses and Resorts in Hokkaido:

Here's a selection of some of the best golf courses and resorts in Hokkaido, categorized by region for easier planning:

### **Sapporo and Central Hokkaido:**

*   **[Sapporo Golf Club Wattsu Course](/course/sapporo_golf_club_wattsu_course_en):** A classic championship course with a rich history, hosting numerous professional tournaments. Known for its challenging layout and well-maintained greens. Expect meticulously manicured fairways and challenging bunkering. The course offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It's consistently ranked among the top courses in Japan.

*   **[The North Country Golf Club](/course/the_north_country_golf_club_en):** A picturesque course set amidst rolling hills and forests. Designed to blend seamlessly with the natural landscape, offering a challenging but fair test of golf. Features strategic bunkering and undulating greens.

*   **Hokkaido Brooks Country Club:** Designed by Jack Nicklaus, this course features wide fairways and strategically placed hazards. A great option for golfers of all skill levels. Offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

*   **Rusutsu Resort Golf 72 (Tower Course & Izumikawa Course):** Part of the larger Rusutsu Resort, these courses offer stunning views of Mount Yotei, often referred to as "Ezo Fuji" (Hokkaido's Mount Fuji). The Tower Course is more challenging, while the Izumikawa Course is more forgiving. The resort offers excellent accommodation and other leisure activities.

### **Niseko and Western Hokkaido:**

*   **Niseko Village Golf Course:** Set against the backdrop of Mount Yotei, this course offers breathtaking views and a challenging layout. Designed by Arnold Palmer, it features strategically placed bunkers and water hazards. Niseko is also famous for its winter skiing, making it a year-round destination.

*   **Hanazono Golf:** Part of the Park Hyatt Niseko Hanazono, this course boasts stunning views and a challenging layout. Provides a luxurious golfing experience with top-notch amenities.

*   **Otaru Country Club:** A classic links-style course located near the port city of Otaru. Features undulating fairways, challenging greens, and stunning views of the Sea of Japan. The wind can be a significant factor on this course.

### **Eastern Hokkaido:**

*   **Akkeshi Kiritappu Country Club:** Located in a beautiful coastal area, this course offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. The course is known for its natural beauty and challenging layout.

*   **Lake Akan Golf Course:** Located near Lake Akan, this course offers scenic views and a relaxed atmosphere. The course is surrounded by forests and mountains.

### **Southern Hokkaido:**

*   **Hakodate Onuma Prince Hotel Golf Course:** Nestled amidst the scenic Onuma Quasi-National Park, this course offers stunning views of Mount Komagatake and Lake Onuma. It provides a challenging but enjoyable golfing experience in a beautiful natural setting. The hotel offers excellent amenities and services.

## Planning Your Hokkaido Golf Trip:

Here are some tips for planning your perfect Hokkaido golf getaway:

*   **Best Time to Visit:** The best time for summer golf in Hokkaido is from June to August, when temperatures are mild and the weather is generally sunny. However, be aware that this is also peak season, so booking in advance is essential. September can also be a good option, with fewer crowds and pleasant weather, but be prepared for the possibility of cooler temperatures and rain.

*   **Booking Tee Times:** Booking tee times in advance is highly recommended, especially during peak season. You can book tee times directly with the golf courses or through online booking services. Many golf courses offer packages that include green fees, cart rental, and other amenities.

*   **Accommodation:** Hokkaido offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels and resorts to traditional ryokans (Japanese inns) and guesthouses. Choose accommodation that is convenient to the golf courses you plan to play and that suits your budget and preferences. Consider staying at a golf resort for the ultimate convenience.

*   **Transportation:** The most convenient way to get around Hokkaido is by renting a car. This will allow you to explore the island at your own pace and visit multiple golf courses. Public transportation is also available, but it may be less convenient, especially for reaching remote golf courses. Japan Rail Pass can be used on JR lines. Consider hiring a private driver for a hassle-free experience.

*   **Golf Equipment:** You can bring your own golf equipment or rent it at most golf courses. Renting golf equipment can be a convenient option if you are traveling from overseas or if you do not want to carry your clubs. However, be sure to check the availability and quality of rental equipment in advance.

*   **Language:** While English is spoken at many hotels and tourist attractions, it is helpful to learn some basic Japanese phrases. This will make your trip more enjoyable and allow you to interact more easily with locals.

*   **Other Activities:** In addition to golf, Hokkaido offers a wide range of other activities, such as hiking, fishing, onsen (hot springs), and exploring national parks. Plan your itinerary to include some of these activities to make the most of your trip.

*   **What to Pack:** Pack light clothing for warm days, as well as layers for cooler evenings. A light jacket or sweater is recommended, even in the summer. Don't forget sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to protect yourself from the sun. Also, bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the island. Insect repellent is useful for dealing with mosquitoes, especially in wooded areas.

## Hokkaido Golf Etiquette:

It's essential to understand and respect Japanese golf etiquette. Here are a few key points:

*   **Dress Code:** Most golf courses in Japan have a strict dress code. Collared shirts, slacks or golf shorts (no jeans or athletic shorts), and golf shoes are typically required.
*   **Pace of Play:** Maintain a brisk pace of play and be ready to play when it is your turn.
*   **Repair Divots and Ball Marks:** Always repair divots and ball marks on the course.
*   **Cart Rules:** Follow the cart rules and avoid driving carts on tees or greens.
*   **Respect for Other Players:** Be respectful of other players and avoid talking or making noise during their swings.
*   **Bowing:** Bowing is a common form of greeting and showing respect in Japan.
*   **Tipping:** Tipping is not customary in Japan.
