# Golf and Shopping Trip to Gotemba Outlet: A Perfect Japanese Getaway

Gotemba, nestled at the foot of Mount Fuji, offers a unique blend of outdoor recreation and retail therapy. A golf and shopping trip to Gotemba is a popular itinerary for both locals and tourists, allowing you to enjoy the scenic beauty of Japan while indulging in your favorite hobbies. This deep-dive guide provides comprehensive information on planning the perfect trip, focusing on selecting the right golf courses, navigating the Gotemba Premium Outlets, and optimizing your experience.

## Part 1: Mastering the Golfing Experience Near Gotemba

The Gotemba area boasts a variety of world-class golf courses, catering to different skill levels and preferences. Choosing the right course is crucial for a fulfilling golfing experience. Consider these factors when making your selection:

**1.1. Skill Level and Course Difficulty:**

*   **Beginner-Friendly Courses:** Look for courses with wider fairways, fewer hazards, and shorter distances. Courses with gentle slopes and less demanding greens are ideal for beginners to build confidence and enjoy the game without excessive frustration. Check course ratings and reviews for indications of difficulty. Phrases like "beginner-friendly," "easy to play," or "suitable for high handicappers" are good indicators.
*   **Intermediate Courses:** These courses offer a mix of challenges, including tighter fairways, strategically placed bunkers, and more undulating greens. They provide a good balance between playability and difficulty, suitable for golfers with some experience looking to improve their skills.
*   **Advanced Courses:** Designed to test experienced golfers, these courses feature narrow fairways, challenging water hazards, demanding rough, and fast, sloping greens. They often require precise shot-making and strategic course management. Professional golfers and low-handicap players will appreciate the challenge.

**1.2. Course Layout and Scenery:**

*   **Mountain Courses:** Many golf courses in the Gotemba area are located in the foothills of Mount Fuji, offering stunning views and challenging terrain. These courses often feature significant elevation changes, requiring careful club selection and strategic shot placement. The breathtaking scenery adds to the overall golfing experience.
*   **Parkland Courses:** These courses are characterized by lush greenery, mature trees lining the fairways, and well-manicured greens. They offer a more traditional golfing experience with a focus on precision and accuracy.
*   **Links-Style Courses:** While less common in the Gotemba area, some courses may incorporate elements of links-style design, featuring undulating fairways, pot bunkers, and exposed greens. These courses typically require a different approach to the game, emphasizing creativity and adaptability.

**1.3. Course Facilities and Amenities:**

*   **Clubhouse:** A well-equipped clubhouse provides a comfortable and relaxing environment before and after your round. Look for facilities such as a pro shop, restaurant, bar, changing rooms, and showers.
*   **Practice Facilities:** Practice facilities, including a driving range, putting green, and chipping area, allow you to warm up and hone your skills before teeing off.
*   **Rental Equipment:** If you don't want to bring your own clubs, inquire about rental options. Ensure that the clubs are of good quality and suitable for your skill level.
*   **Caddies:** Consider hiring a caddie to assist with course management, club selection, and reading greens. Caddies can significantly enhance your golfing experience, especially on unfamiliar courses.
*   **Onsen (Hot Spring Baths):** Many golf courses in Japan feature onsen facilities, providing a relaxing and rejuvenating experience after your round.

**1.4. Recommended Golf Courses Near Gotemba:**

*   **Taiheiyo Club Gotemba Course:** This prestigious course, designed by Gary Player, is known for its challenging layout and stunning views of Mount Fuji. It hosts the annual Mitsui Sumitomo VISA Taiheiyo Masters tournament.
*   **Gotemba Golf Club:** A more accessible option with beautiful scenery and a variety of holes suitable for different skill levels.
*   **Fuji Classic:** This course features a classic parkland layout with well-manicured fairways and challenging greens.
*   **Kawana Hotel Golf Course (Oshima Course):** While a bit further from Gotemba, the Kawana Hotel Golf Course is a renowned destination offering spectacular ocean views and challenging play.

**1.5. Booking Tee Times:**

*   **Online Booking:** Many golf courses offer online booking through their websites or third-party platforms. This is a convenient way to secure your tee time in advance, especially during peak season.
*   **Phone Booking:** You can also book tee times by calling the golf course directly.
*   **Travel Agencies:** Travel agencies specializing in golf tours can assist with booking tee times, arranging transportation, and providing other services.
*   **Japanese Golf Websites:** Websites like Rakuten GORA or Value Golf are useful for booking and comparing prices. Google Translate is helpful for navigating these.

**1.6. Golf Etiquette and Customs in Japan:**

*   **Punctuality:** Arrive at the golf course well in advance of your tee time to allow time for check-in and warm-up.
*   **Dress Code:** Adhere to the golf course's dress code, which typically includes collared shirts, golf shorts or trousers, and golf shoes.
*   **Pace of Play:** Maintain a reasonable pace of play to avoid holding up other golfers.
*   **Repair Divots and Ball Marks:** Repair any divots or ball marks on the fairway or green.
*   **Quiet on the Green:** Refrain from talking or moving around while other golfers are putting.
*   **Respect for the Course:** Avoid damaging the course or littering.
*   **Bowing:** Bowing is a common greeting and gesture of respect in Japan. Bow to the staff and other golfers.
*   **Gratuities:** Tipping is not customary in Japan.

## Part 2: Navigating the Gotemba Premium Outlets for a Successful Shopping Spree

The Gotemba Premium Outlets is one of the largest and most popular outlet malls in Japan, offering a wide selection of international and domestic brands at discounted prices. Planning your visit strategically can help you maximize your time and savings.

**2.1. Pre-Trip Planning:**

*   **Check the Outlet's Website:** Visit the Gotemba Premium Outlets website to view a list of stores, check for ongoing promotions, and download a map of the mall.
*   **Identify Target Brands:** Make a list of the brands you are interested in before your visit. This will help you prioritize your shopping and avoid getting overwhelmed by the sheer number of stores.
*   **Budget:** Set a budget for your shopping trip to avoid overspending.
*   **Check for Discount Coupons:** Look for discount coupons online or at your hotel. Many hotels offer coupons specifically for the Gotemba Premium Outlets. Credit card companies often have promotional tie-ins.
*   **Travel Time:** Factor in travel time from your golf course to the outlet. Traffic can be heavy, especially on weekends and holidays.

**2.2. Getting There:**

*   **Car:** The most convenient way to reach the Gotemba Premium Outlets is by car. There is ample parking available, but it can fill up quickly during peak season. Be aware of expressway tolls.
*   **Train and Bus:** You can take a train to Gotemba Station and then take a free shuttle bus to the outlet mall. This is a more economical option, but it may take longer. Odakyu Highway Bus also runs direct services from Shinjuku and other major areas.
*   **Direct Bus:** Several bus companies operate direct services from major cities such as Tokyo, Shinjuku, and Yokohama.

**2.3. Shopping Strategies:**

*   **Arrive Early:** Arrive at the outlet mall early in the morning to avoid crowds.
*   **Prioritize Your Shopping:** Focus on the brands you are most interested in first.
*   **Check for Promotions:** Look for special promotions and discounts offered by individual stores.
*   **Compare Prices:** Compare prices between different stores to ensure you are getting the best deal.
*   **Shop During Off-Peak Hours:** If possible, avoid shopping during weekends and holidays, when the outlet mall is most crowded. Weekday afternoons are usually less busy.
*   **Utilize Tax-Free Shopping:** If you are a foreign tourist, you can take advantage of tax-free shopping on purchases over a certain amount. Bring your passport with you. Look for "Tax-Free" signs in stores.
*   **Wear Comfortable Shoes:** You will be doing a lot of walking, so wear comfortable shoes.
*   **Stay Hydrated:** Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, especially on hot days.
*   **Take Breaks:** Take breaks throughout the day to rest and recharge.

**2.4. Food and Drink Options:**

*   The Gotemba Premium Outlets offers a variety of food and drink options, ranging from casual cafes to restaurants.
*   Consider trying some local specialties, such as Gotemba's famous soba noodles.
*   Many stores also offer takeaway options, allowing you to enjoy your food while you shop.

**2.5. Luggage Storage:**

*   Coin lockers are available for storing luggage and shopping bags.
*   Some hotels near the outlet mall offer luggage storage services for guests.

**2.6. Post-Shopping Tips:**

*   **Review Your Purchases:** Before leaving the outlet mall, review your purchases to ensure you are satisfied with your selections.
*   **Keep Your Receipts:** Keep your receipts for warranty purposes and potential returns.
*   **Plan for Transportation:** Arrange for transportation back to your hotel or next destination.

## Part 4: Beyond Golf and Shopping: Exploring Gotemba and the Surrounding Area

While golf and shopping are the main attractions, the Gotemba area offers a variety of other activities and attractions:

*   **Mount Fuji:** Take a scenic drive to the Mount Fuji area and enjoy breathtaking views of the iconic mountain.
*   **Lake Ashi:** Enjoy a cruise on Lake Ashi, surrounded by stunning scenery.
*   **Hakone:** Explore the Hakone region, known for its hot springs, art museums, and natural beauty.
*   **Gotemba Peace Pagoda:** Visit the Gotemba Peace Pagoda, a Buddhist monument offering panoramic views of Mount Fuji.
*   **Gotemba Kogen Beer:** Sample locally brewed beer at Gotemba Kogen Beer, a popular brewery and restaurant.
*   **Soba Noodles:** Indulge in a bowl of Gotemba's famous soba noodles, made with locally grown buckwheat.

## Part 5: Essential Japanese Phrases for Your Trip

Knowing a few basic Japanese phrases can enhance your experience and show respect for the local culture:

*   **Hello:** Konnichiwa (こんにちは)
*   **Thank you:** Arigato (ありがとう)
*   **Excuse me:** Sumimasen (すみません)
*   **Yes:** Hai (はい)
*   **No:** Iie (いいえ)
*   **How much is it?:** Ikura desu ka? (いくらですか？)
*   **Do you speak English?:** Eigo o hanasemasu ka? (英語を話せますか？)
*   **Golf:** Gorufu (ゴルフ)
*   **Shopping:** Shoppingu (ショッピング)
*   **Delicious:** Oishii (おいしい)
