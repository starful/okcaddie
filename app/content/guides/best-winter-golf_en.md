# Top 5 Kyushu Winter Golf Destinations: Beat the Cold and Perfect Your Swing

Kyushu, Japan's southernmost major island, offers a welcome escape from the harsh winter weather experienced in other parts of the country. With its relatively mild climate, stunning natural beauty, and world-class golf courses, Kyushu is quickly becoming a premier destination for **winter golf**. This guide unveils the top 5 must-visit golf destinations in Kyushu for the ultimate **winter golf** getaway.

## Why Choose Kyushu for Winter Golf?

Before diving into the top destinations, let's explore why Kyushu is an ideal choice for **winter golf**:

*   **Mild Climate:** Kyushu boasts a significantly milder climate compared to northern Japan, making it comfortable to play golf even during the winter months. Average temperatures range from 8-15°C (46-59°F), perfect for enjoying a round without freezing conditions.
*   **Beautiful Scenery:** From volcanic landscapes and lush green hills to stunning coastlines, Kyushu offers diverse and breathtaking scenery that enhances the golfing experience.
*   **World-Class Golf Courses:** Kyushu is home to a wide variety of golf courses, ranging from championship layouts designed by renowned architects to more relaxed and accessible courses suitable for all skill levels.
*   **Onsen (Hot Springs) Culture:** After a day on the green, relax and rejuvenate in one of Kyushu's many onsen (hot springs). This is the perfect way to unwind and soothe your muscles after a challenging round of golf.
*   **Delicious Local Cuisine:** Indulge in Kyushu's diverse culinary scene, from fresh seafood and succulent Kagoshima black pork to spicy Motsunabe (offal hot pot) and local ramen variations.

## Top 5 Kyushu Winter Golf Destinations:

Here are our top 5 recommendations for **winter golf** destinations in Kyushu, offering a blend of challenging courses, comfortable accommodations, and unforgettable experiences:

**1. Miyazaki Prefecture: The Golfing Paradise**

Miyazaki Prefecture, located on the southeastern coast of Kyushu, is often referred to as the "Golfing Paradise" of Japan. Its subtropical climate and abundance of sunshine make it an ideal destination for **winter golf**.

*   **Key Features:**
    *   Warmest winter climate in Kyushu.
    *   Numerous championship golf courses designed by renowned architects.
    *   Luxurious resorts and hotels catering to golfers.
    *   Beautiful coastal scenery and pristine beaches.

*   **Recommended Golf Courses:**
    *   **Phoenix Country Club:** Host of the annual Dunlop Phoenix Tournament, this championship course is renowned for its challenging layout and stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. It's a must-play for serious golfers seeking a test of their skills.
    *   **Tom Watson Golf Course:** Designed by the legendary Tom Watson, this course offers a blend of Scottish links-style golf and Japanese aesthetics. It's known for its strategic bunkering and undulating greens.
    *   **Umehara Golf Club:** Offers stunning ocean views and a challenging layout suitable for experienced golfers.
    *   **Miyazaki Country Club:** A more relaxed and accessible course for golfers of all skill levels.

*   **Accommodation Options:**
    *   **Sheraton Grande Ocean Resort:** A luxurious resort with stunning ocean views, multiple restaurants, and a spa.
    *   **ANA Holiday Inn Resort Miyazaki:** A family-friendly resort with a range of activities and amenities.
    *   **Sun Hotel Phoenix:** A more budget-friendly option with comfortable accommodations and easy access to golf courses.

*   **Things to Do Beyond Golf:**
    *   Visit the Aoshima Shrine, a picturesque shrine located on a small island just off the coast.
    *   Explore the Takachiho Gorge, a stunning natural gorge with waterfalls and hiking trails.
    *   Relax on the beautiful beaches of Miyazaki, such as Aoshima Beach.
    *   Sample local delicacies like Miyazaki beef and mangoes.

**2. Kagoshima Prefecture: Volcanic Views and Hot Springs**

Kagoshima Prefecture, located on the southwestern tip of Kyushu, is known for its active volcano, Sakurajima, and its abundance of onsen (hot springs). The region offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural experiences, making it a compelling destination for **winter golf**.

*   **Key Features:**
    *   Dramatic volcanic scenery.
    *   Numerous onsen (hot springs) for relaxation after a round of golf.
    *   Delicious local cuisine, including Kagoshima black pork and sweet potatoes.
    *   A variety of golf courses ranging from challenging to relaxed.

*   **Recommended Golf Courses:**
    *   **Ibusuki Golf Club:** Features stunning views of the Kinko Bay and Sakurajima volcano. The course is well-maintained and offers a challenging but fair test of golf.
    *   **Kagoshima Kokusai Golf Club:** A popular course with a classic layout and challenging greens.
    *   **Kirishima Kogen National Park Golf Club:** Located in the Kirishima mountains, this course offers breathtaking views and a unique golfing experience.

*   **Accommodation Options:**
    *   **Ibusuki Hakusuikan:** A luxurious ryokan (traditional Japanese inn) with stunning ocean views and a renowned sand bath onsen.
    *   **Shiroyama Hotel Kagoshima:** Offers panoramic views of Sakurajima and Kagoshima city.
    *   **Remm Kagoshima:** A modern hotel with comfortable accommodations and a convenient location.

*   **Things to Do Beyond Golf:**
    *   Visit Sakurajima, an active volcano, and take a ferry across the Kinko Bay.
    *   Relax in one of Kagoshima's many onsen (hot springs), such as the Sunamushi Onsen (sand bath).
    *   Explore the Sengan-en Garden, a beautiful traditional Japanese garden with views of Sakurajima.
    *   Sample local delicacies like Kagoshima black pork and sweet potatoes.
    *   Visit the Chiran Peace Museum, a poignant museum dedicated to the kamikaze pilots of World War II.

**3. Kumamoto Prefecture: Active Volcano and Stunning Nature**

Kumamoto Prefecture, located in the center of Kyushu, is known for its active volcano, Mount Aso, and its stunning natural beauty. The region offers a diverse range of golfing experiences, from challenging championship courses to more relaxed and accessible options.

*   **Key Features:**
    *   Spectacular views of Mount Aso, one of the world's largest active volcanoes.
    *   Beautiful natural landscapes, including forests, waterfalls, and hot springs.
    *   Delicious local cuisine, including Kumamoto ramen and horse meat.
    *   A variety of golf courses suitable for all skill levels.

*   **Recommended Golf Courses:**
    *   **Kumamoto Kuko Country Club:** Boasts a beautiful layout with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
    *   **Aso Highland Golf Course:** Located in the Aso mountains, this course offers a unique golfing experience with breathtaking views of Mount Aso.
    *   **Kosa Country Club:** A well-maintained course with a challenging layout and excellent facilities.

*   **Accommodation Options:**
    *   **Hotel Nikko Kumamoto:** A luxurious hotel with a convenient location in Kumamoto city.
    *   **Aso Resort Grandvrio Hotel:** Offers stunning views of Mount Aso and a range of activities and amenities.
    *   **Dormy Inn Kumamoto Natural Hot Spring:** A comfortable and affordable hotel with a natural hot spring.

*   **Things to Do Beyond Golf:**
    *   Visit Mount Aso and explore the surrounding volcanic landscape.
    *   Explore the Kikuchi Valley, a beautiful valley with waterfalls and hiking trails.
    *   Visit Kumamoto Castle, a historic castle that was partially damaged in the 2016 Kumamoto earthquake.
    *   Relax in one of Kumamoto's many onsen (hot springs).
    *   Sample local delicacies like Kumamoto ramen and horse meat.

**4. Oita Prefecture: Onsen Paradise and Rugged Coastline**

Oita Prefecture, located on the northeastern coast of Kyushu, is renowned for its onsen (hot springs), especially Beppu, one of Japan's most famous onsen resorts. The region also offers a rugged coastline and beautiful natural scenery, making it a unique destination for **winter golf**.

*   **Key Features:**
    *   Abundant onsen (hot springs), especially in Beppu and Yufuin.
    *   Rugged coastline with dramatic cliffs and stunning views.
    *   Delicious local cuisine, including toriten (tempura chicken) and reimen (cold noodles).
    *   A variety of golf courses, some with ocean views.

*   **Recommended Golf Courses:**
    *   **Oita Golf Club:** A classic course with a challenging layout and beautiful scenery.
    *   **Beppu Golf Club:** Offers stunning views of the Beppu Bay and a challenging golfing experience.
    *   **Hiji Golf Club:** A well-maintained course with a relaxed atmosphere and a variety of challenges.

*   **Accommodation Options:**
    *   **Suginoi Hotel:** A large resort in Beppu with multiple onsen, restaurants, and activities.
    *   **Yufuin Tamanoyu:** A luxurious ryokan (traditional Japanese inn) in Yufuin with private onsen and exquisite cuisine.
    *   **Hotel Nikko Oita Oasis Tower:** A modern hotel with a convenient location in Oita city.

*   **Things to Do Beyond Golf:**
    *   Visit Beppu and explore the "Hells of Beppu," a series of geothermal hot springs.
    *   Relax in one of Beppu's many onsen (hot springs).
    *   Explore the charming town of Yufuin, known for its traditional crafts and art galleries.
    *   Visit the Usa Jingu Shrine, one of Japan's most important Shinto shrines.
    *   Sample local delicacies like toriten (tempura chicken) and reimen (cold noodles).

**5. Nagasaki Prefecture: History, Culture, and Coastal Beauty**

Nagasaki Prefecture, located on the northwestern coast of Kyushu, is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning coastal beauty. The region offers a unique blend of Japanese and European influences, making it a fascinating destination for **winter golf**.

*   **Key Features:**
    *   Rich history and cultural heritage, including the Atomic Bomb Museum and Peace Park.
    *   Beautiful coastal scenery with islands and harbors.
    *   Unique blend of Japanese and European influences.
    *   Delicious local cuisine, including Nagasaki champon (a type of ramen) and castella cake.

*   **Recommended Golf Courses:**
    *   **Nagasaki Golf Club:** Offers stunning views of the Nagasaki Bay and a challenging layout.
    *   **Unzen Golf Club:** Located in the Unzen mountains, this course offers a unique golfing experience with beautiful scenery.
    *   **Sasebo Country Club:** A well-maintained course with a relaxed atmosphere and a variety of challenges.

*   **Accommodation Options:**
    *   **Hotel New Nagasaki:** A luxurious hotel with a convenient location near Nagasaki Station.
    *   **Huis Ten Bosch Hotel Europe:** Located in the Huis Ten Bosch theme park, this hotel offers a unique European-style experience.
    *   **Richmond Hotel Nagasaki Shianbashi:** A comfortable and affordable hotel with a convenient location.

*   **Things to Do Beyond Golf:**
    *   Visit the Atomic Bomb Museum and Peace Park to learn about the atomic bombing of Nagasaki.
    *   Explore the Glover Garden, a historic garden with Western-style houses.
    *   Visit the Dejima Wharf, a former Dutch trading post.
    *   Explore the Huis Ten Bosch theme park, a Dutch-themed amusement park.
    *   Sample local delicacies like Nagasaki champon (a type of ramen) and castella cake.

## Tips for Planning Your Kyushu Winter Golf Trip:

*   **Book in Advance:** Popular golf courses and accommodations can fill up quickly, especially during peak season. Book your reservations well in advance to avoid disappointment.
*   **Check the Weather Forecast:** While Kyushu generally enjoys a mild winter, it's always a good idea to check the weather forecast before your trip and pack accordingly.
*   **Pack for Layering:** Dress in layers to adapt to changing temperatures throughout the day.
*   **Consider a Rental Car:** While public transportation is available, a rental car can provide more flexibility and allow you to explore the region at your own pace.
*   **Learn Basic Japanese Phrases:** While English is spoken in some tourist areas, learning a few basic Japanese phrases can enhance your experience and make it easier to communicate with locals.
*   **Embrace the Onsen Culture:** Don't be afraid to try the onsen (hot springs)! It's a quintessential Japanese experience and a great way to relax and rejuvenate after a round of golf.

Kyushu offers a fantastic alternative for **winter golf** enthusiasts seeking warmer temperatures, stunning scenery, and world-class golfing experiences. From the golfing paradise of Miyazaki to the volcanic landscapes of Kagoshima and the onsen resorts of Oita, Kyushu has something to offer every golfer. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of **winter golf** in Kyushu!
