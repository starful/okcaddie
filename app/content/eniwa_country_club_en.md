Eniwa Country Club is a **multi-course Hokkaido destination** in Eniwa City, best known for its championship **Gold Course** (ANA Open pedigree) plus Silver and Bronze layouts. Visitors can often book through major Japanese portals; summer is the prime window.

## Who this course is for

- Golfers chasing a **tournament-feel bentgrass** day in Hokkaido
- Stay-and-play travelers pairing New Chitose (CTS) with a lodge / onsen night
- Mid-to-low handicappers who manage wind and birch-lined corridors

High handicappers can still enjoy Silver/Bronze when available—ask which course your tee sheet assigns.

## Course overview

**Gold** is the headline: longer corridors, strategic bunkering, and true bent greens that reward center-of-green discipline. Birch and pine frame many holes; wind off the Ishikari plain can turn mid-irons into guessing games.

**Silver / Bronze** are typically slightly more resort-friendly but still demand accurate approaches. Do not assume all three play the same—check the day’s pairing when you book.

## Green fees & booking

Hokkaido peak-season packages are not cheap. Planning ranges (per person, package-dependent):

- **Weekday**: often high-¥10,000s to ¥20,000s+
- **Weekend / Gold peak**: can climb well into the ¥20,000s–¥30,000s with lunch

Book via [Rakuten GORA / OKCaddie booking](/booking/eniwa_country_club_en). Filter carefully for **which course** is included. Rental clubs and caddie carts vary by plan—add them at checkout if you travel light.

Live quotes beat any static “green fee” number on a blog. Tax and meal inclusion differ by product.

## Access

- **Airport**: New Chitose (CTS) → roughly **35–50 minutes** by car toward Eniwa
- Rental car is simplest; taxis work for one-off rounds but add cost
- From Sapporo city: about 45–70 minutes depending on traffic

Fly CTS in the morning only if your tee time is afternoon. For 7–8 a.m. starts, stay nearby the night before.

## Dress code & tips

- Standard Japanese club dress: collared shirt, proper golf bottoms, soft spikes
- Summer mosquitoes and sudden showers are normal—pack a shell
- Bent greens: miss short-sided pins; two-putt from the fat side

## Bottom line

Eniwa is a **Hokkaido bucket-list booking** when you want championship conditioning—not a casual municipal loop. Lock the course name on the voucher, fly CTS with buffer, and play the wind before you play the pin.
