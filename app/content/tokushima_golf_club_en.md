## Course Overview

Tokushima Golf Club, a venerable institution in the Shikoku region, traces its origins back to the mid-20th century, offering a golfing experience rooted in tradition. This 18-hole, par-72 course typically spans between 6,400 to 6,800 yards from the regular tees, providing a balanced challenge for a wide range of players. While the original architect's name may not be widely recognized internationally, the design reflects a classic Japanese approach to land utilization within a natural forest setting. The course generally features bentgrass greens, known for their smooth rolling surfaces, complemented by zoysia grass fairways that provide a firm hitting surface. Its overall character is defined by mature tree lines, strategic elevation changes, and a quiet, immersive atmosphere distinct from coastal or resort-style layouts.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating Tokushima Golf Club requires thoughtful shot placement and an understanding of its tree-lined corridors. Here are four holes that exemplify the strategic demands of the course:

*   **Hole 3: Par 4 (360-390 yards)**
    This medium-length par 4 presents a gentle dogleg left, with dense forest guarding the left side and a strategically placed bunker on the right side of the landing zone for longer hitters. The tee shot demands a controlled draw or a precisely aimed hybrid to avoid either penalty area, setting up a clear approach. An iron or utility club is often a prudent choice off the tee to ensure placement over distance. The green itself is moderately undulating, sloping subtly from back to front, making approach shots from above the hole particularly challenging for a two-putt par.

*   **Hole 7: Par 3 (160-190 yards)**
    A picturesque yet intimidating par 3, this hole plays slightly downhill to a green protected by water directly in front and bunkers on either side. Club selection is paramount here, as the downhill trajectory can add distance, but under-clubbing will certainly find the hazard. A mid-iron for most players will be the call, prioritizing a safe landing on the green's ample surface over aggressively attacking the pin. The green has a ridge running through its center, creating distinct putting quadrants that require a careful read, especially if the pin is tucked away behind the ridge.

*   **Hole 12: Par 5 (500-530 yards)**
    The 12th is a strategic par 5 that begins with a tight driving window, framed by tall trees on both sides, leading to a subtle dogleg right. A strong, straight tee shot is essential to have any chance of reaching the green in two, but the second shot is complicated by a stream crossing the fairway approximately 100 yards short of the green, making it a clear three-shot hole for most. For the second shot, consider a lay-up with an iron or fairway wood to a comfortable distance short of the stream, leaving a short iron approach. The green is relatively flat but well-guarded by deep bunkers on the left, demanding precision on the final approach.

*   **Hole 16: Par 4 (410-440 yards)**
    This long, challenging par 4 features a slight uphill climb from tee to green and a narrow fairway that requires accuracy off the tee. Overhanging tree branches can pinch the landing area, making a penetrating driver or a well-struck 3-wood the preferred play. Missing the fairway usually results in a difficult lie in the rough or a punch-out. The approach shot typically involves a mid-to-long iron to a two-tiered green, often playing longer than its stated yardage due to the uphill nature. Pin positions on the upper tier can make for demanding putts if your approach lands on the lower tier.

## Conditions & Seasonality

Tokushima Golf Club generally maintains good conditions, particularly given its value-oriented positioning within the region. The bentgrass greens are typically consistent, rolling at a medium pace that suits everyday play. Fairways, usually zoysia, offer a firm lie, but can become lush during the summer months.

The best months for play are typically spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November), when temperatures are mild, and the foliage provides a scenic backdrop. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid with occasional afternoon thunderstorms, while winter (December-March) sees cooler temperatures, with frost delays possible on colder mornings, though heavy snowfall is rare. Wind can be a factor, particularly in exposed areas, but the forest largely protects many fairways. Pace of play is generally good on weekdays, often around 4 to 4.5 hours for an 18-hole round. Weekends and holidays can see rounds extending to 5 hours, especially during peak season, due to increased traffic. Early morning tee times on weekdays offer the quickest rounds, while mid-day on weekends will be the busiest.

## Green Fees & Booking

Green fees at Tokushima Golf Club offer competitive rates, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious travelers. Weekday green fees typically range from JPY 8,000 to JPY 13,000, inclusive of a shared cart. Weekend and holiday rates are higher, usually falling between JPY 12,000 and JPY 20,000, also including a shared cart. Caddies are often available as an optional add-on for an additional fee, typically JPY 4,000 to JPY 6,000 per player when split among a group, but their availability may vary and should be requested in advance.

As is common in Japan, the club primarily operates on a visitor-friendly policy, with no strict membership requirements for booking tee times. The most efficient way for international visitors to book is through major Japanese online golf booking platforms such as Rakuten GORA or GDO (Golf Digest Online). These sites allow you to search for available tee times, compare packages (e.g., lunch included, cart-only), and make reservations directly. It is advisable to book at least a few weeks in advance for weekend play during peak seasons. While direct booking via phone or email might be possible for groups, the online portals are the most accessible and reliable method for individual and small group bookings.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Adhering to the established dress code and on-course rules is important at Tokushima Golf Club, reflecting common etiquette for Japanese golf courses. For attire, collared shirts are mandatory, and tucked into trousers or shorts. Denim jeans, t-shirts, sleeveless shirts, or tracksuits are not permitted on the course or in the clubhouse. Appropriate golf trousers or tailored shorts are expected, and soft spike golf shoes are required; metal spikes are generally prohibited.

Regarding on-course rules, maintaining pace of play is highly emphasized. Groups are expected to keep up with the group ahead, and slow play may result in a gentle reminder from marshals. Mobile phone use is generally restricted to the clubhouse and designated areas; on the course, phones should be kept on silent and only used for emergencies or quick, discreet communication. Repairing pitch marks on greens and raking bunkers are standard expectations. Additionally, caddies (if taken) are there to assist with club selection, course management, and maintaining pace, so following their guidance is recommended for an efficient round.

## Access

Tokushima Golf Club is situated in Tokushima Prefecture on Shikoku Island, making it a primary golf destination for those exploring the region. The club is easily accessible by car, which is the most practical mode of transportation for golfers carrying equipment.

The nearest major train station is Tokushima Station, located in the heart of Tokushima City. From Tokushima Station, the golf club is approximately a 20 to 30-minute drive by taxi or rental car, depending on traffic. For those traveling from other major cities, drive times are as follows:

*   **From Osaka/Kobe:** Approximately 2.5 to 3 hours via the Akashi Kaikyo Bridge and Naruto Bridge, followed by the Tokushima Expressway. This route involves significant toll roads.
*   **From Hiroshima:** Roughly 3 to 3.5 hours via the Seto-Ohashi Bridge or Kurushima Kaikyo Bridge, traversing through parts of Kagawa or Ehime prefectures. Again, multiple toll roads are involved.
*   **From Tokyo:** Traveling from Tokyo typically involves a domestic flight to Tokushima Awa-Odori Airport (approximately 1 hour 15 minutes flight time). From Tokushima Airport, the club is a further 30 to 40-minute drive. Alternatively, a bullet train (Shinkansen) to Shin-Osaka followed by the aforementioned drive is an option, but the total travel time can be significantly longer.

The club provides ample free parking facilities for private vehicles, accommodating numerous guests, which is standard for Japanese golf courses.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Tokushima Golf Club offers a functional and comfortable environment, providing all the necessary amenities for a round of golf. Upon arrival, golfers will find well-maintained locker rooms for changing and storing personal belongings, complete with private lockers. A highlight of the clubhouse experience is often the traditional Japanese communal bath (daiyokujo), sometimes featuring an onsen (hot spring) if the club has a natural source. These bathing facilities are a staple of Japanese golf culture, providing a relaxing soak after the round, complete with shower stations, amenities, and often an outdoor section.

The club's restaurant typically serves a range of Japanese and Western dishes, catering to diverse palates. Common offerings include local specialties like Tokushima ramen, udon, or grilled fish, alongside staples such as katsu curry, ginger pork (shogayaki), and various set meals (teishoku). Lunch menus are usually comprehensive, providing substantial and satisfying options before or after your round. While not overtly luxurious, the dining facilities are clean, efficient, and offer a pleasant atmosphere for a meal, often with views overlooking parts of the course. A small pro shop is also usually present, stocking essential golf equipment, apparel, and souvenirs.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Tokushima Golf Club with a caddie can significantly enhance your experience, especially for first-time visitors to a Japanese forest course. Common mistakes for visitors include misjudging distances due to the elevation changes and underestimating the subtle breaks on the greens. Caddies provide accurate yardages, account for uphill/downhill lies, and offer precise club recommendations based on the daily conditions. They also possess valuable local knowledge, guiding you away from hidden hazards, such as the numerous tree roots that can impact errant shots in the rough, or the best line to take off the tee on dogleg holes.

They are adept at reading greens, which often appear flatter than they are; trusting their reads on putting lines can save several strokes. Caddies can also help with pace of play, suggesting efficient ways to move between holes and locate stray balls quickly. This course particularly suits players who value strategic shot-making over pure power. Golfers with handicaps ranging from mid-single digits to mid-twenties will find the course engaging and fair, provided they manage their game well within the tree-lined fairways. Players who enjoy a traditional, immersive golf experience, focusing on precision and course management rather than resort-style wide-open layouts, will particularly appreciate Tokushima Golf Club.
