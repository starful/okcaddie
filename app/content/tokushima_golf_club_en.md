## Course Overview

Tokushima Golf Club presents 18 holes of classic Japanese parkland golf, playing to a par of 72. The course measures around 6,700-7,000 yards from the back tees, featuring mature tree-lined fairways characteristic of a traditional forest layout. Opened in the mid-20th century, its design reflects an era focused on integrating golf into the natural terrain rather than extensive earthmoving.

Fairways are typically Zoysia grass, known for its resilience and firm playing surface, while the greens are fast-rolling Bentgrass, maintained to a high standard. The course’s historic nature means well-established trees frequently dictate shot placement, requiring strategic thinking over brute force. It stands out as a strong value-for-money option in the region, without compromising on a genuine golf experience.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating Tokushima Golf Club requires precision, especially on its tighter, tree-lined holes. The course features several memorable challenges that demand thoughtful club selection and accurate ball striking.

The 5th hole, a par-4, is a gentle dogleg left where an aggressive tee shot attempting to cut the corner brings dense trees into play, often resulting in a blocked second shot. A smart play is a 3-wood or hybrid aimed at the center-right of the fairway, leaving a mid-iron approach to a green protected by bunkers short and right. The par-3 12th often plays downhill to a green with subtle undulations, frequently guarded by a water hazard short and left. Club selection here is critical, as a missed shot short or left will find trouble, while an overhit shot may leave a difficult downhill chip. Finally, the par-5 16th is an uphill, narrow hole that plays longer than its yardage suggests. Two precise layups are usually required to avoid dense forestry, setting up a wedge to a relatively flat green that accepts well-struck shots.

## Practical Info

Green fees at Tokushima Golf Club typically range from JPY 8,000-12,000 on weekdays, increasing to JPY 12,000-18,000 on weekends and national holidays. These prices generally include cart fees, reflecting its "Value for Money" tag. Reservations are highly recommended, especially for weekend play, and can be made directly via phone or through major Japanese online golf booking platforms.

The standard mode of play includes a ride-on cart, often equipped with a GPS navigation system to aid course management. While caddies can be requested, they are less common for everyday play and require advance booking. A standard golf dress code applies, meaning collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft spikes are expected.

## Access

Tokushima Golf Club is conveniently situated within easy reach of central Tokushima City, making it highly accessible for visitors staying in the prefectural capital. The course is approximately a 20-minute drive from Tokushima Station.

For those traveling from outside the immediate area, the club is roughly a 90-minute drive (about 70-80 km) from Takamatsu City in Kagawa Prefecture, another major hub in Shikoku. Ample free parking is available directly at the clubhouse, accommodating both rental cars and private vehicles.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse at Tokushima Golf Club provides a comfortable and functional space for golfers before and after their rounds. Locker rooms are well-maintained and offer sufficient space for changing and securing belongings. A common feature in Japanese golf clubs, the facility includes a clean communal bath (ofuro), perfect for relaxing muscles after a round, complete with showering amenities. The on-site restaurant serves a variety of Japanese dishes, often featuring local Tokushima specialties such as Awa-dori chicken or Tokushima ramen, providing a taste of regional cuisine alongside standard golf club fare.

## Tips

For the best experience, consider playing Tokushima Golf Club during spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November), when the weather is mild and the course conditions are typically excellent. Common mistakes often involve underestimating the tightness of the fairways; prioritize accuracy off the tee with hybrids or long irons when facing narrow tree lines, rather than attempting to overpower the course with a driver. Be mindful of summer humidity and occasional winter chills, though Tokushima generally enjoys a milder climate than some other parts of Japan. Peak season pricing applies to all weekends and national holidays, so for those prioritizing value, weekday rounds are recommended.
