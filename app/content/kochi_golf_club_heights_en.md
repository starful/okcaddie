## Course Overview
Kochi Golf Club Heights, nestled in the scenic hills of Kochi, presents an 18-hole, par 72 challenge, typically playing between 6,500 to 6,800 yards from the regular tees. The course is known for its distinctive mountain-style characteristics, featuring significant elevation changes and panoramic views of the surrounding Shikoku landscape. Fairways are generally zoysia grass, providing a firm surface, while the greens are typically bentgrass, offering consistent roll. Its elevated position offers golfers a refreshing escape and a unique blend of strategic play amidst natural beauty.

## Layout & Strategy
The course demands precision rather than sheer power, with many holes requiring thoughtful club selection. Consider the par-4 6th hole, a downhill dogleg left, where the tee shot unveils a generous landing area but hides a pond just beyond a fairway bunker on the inside corner; a 220-240 yard drive with a hybrid or long iron will set up an approach to a green that slopes subtly back to front. The par-3 12th hole, often playing into the prevailing wind, demands a precise iron shot over a ravine to a two-tiered green, where short is better than long. Finally, the par-5 17th presents a genuine risk-reward opportunity: a drive favoring the left side opens up the chance to reach in two for longer hitters, but a narrow landing zone for the second shot is guarded by dense forest on both sides, making a layup with an iron or hybrid a safer play to secure a birdie.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Kochi Golf Club Heights represent excellent value, typically ranging from 6,000-10,000 JPY on weekdays and 10,000-15,000 JPY on weekends, usually inclusive of a shared cart. The course is publicly accessible, though advance reservations, particularly for weekend play, are strongly recommended and can often be made online. Golfers typically utilize motorized carts, which often feature GPS navigation, making course management straightforward. Standard golf attire is expected: collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft-spiked golf shoes are customary. Caddies are usually available upon request for an additional fee, offering local insights into the course's nuances.

## Access
Kochi Golf Club Heights is conveniently situated within the Kochi Prefecture. The nearest major transport hub is JR Kochi Station, from which the course is an approximate 20-30 minute drive by car. For those driving, the course offers ample free parking facilities right at the clubhouse, making self-drive a practical option. The scenic drive from Kochi City up into the heights provides a pleasant preamble to your round.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse at Kochi Golf Club Heights is functional and welcoming, providing all necessary amenities for golfers. Spacious locker rooms are available for changing, and a highlight for many is the large communal bath (onsen), perfect for unwinding after a challenging round. The restaurant, overlooking parts of the course, serves a variety of local specialties; expect fresh seafood options and regional chicken dishes that showcase Kochi's culinary heritage, providing a satisfying end to your golf day.

## Tips
For the most enjoyable experience, consider visiting Kochi Golf Club Heights during the spring (March-May) or autumn (October-November) months, when the weather is typically mild and the scenery vibrant. A common mistake here is misjudging distances due to significant elevation changes; trust your rangefinder and consider a club extra on uphill shots, or one less on downhill plays. While winters are generally mild, mountain winds can make it feel cooler, so layering is advisable. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid with potential for heavy rain, though earlier tee times can mitigate some of the heat. Peak season pricing applies to weekends and national holidays, especially during the temperate months.
