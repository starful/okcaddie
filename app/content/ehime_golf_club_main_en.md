## Course Overview
Ehime Golf Club Main provides a challenging 18-hole, par-72 experience set amidst the mountainous terrain of Uchiko. While the original designer is not widely publicized, the course, likely established in the 1970s or 1980s, embodies the classic Japanese mountain-style layout, demanding precision over raw power. From the back tees, the course typically plays between 6,800 and 7,100 yards, with fairways predominantly Zoysia grass and greens generally Bentgrass, ensuring consistent playing surfaces throughout the year. Its signature characteristics include significant elevation changes, tight, tree-lined fairways that punish errant shots, and strategic bunkering that protects greens and dictates tee shot placement.

## Layout & Strategy
The course demands thoughtful play, starting with holes like the 365-yard, par-4 5th, which presents an uphill, dogleg-right challenge off the tee. A precise drive avoiding the hidden bunker on the right corner is crucial, setting up a mid-iron approach to a two-tiered green where the putting line often breaks more than it appears due to subtle slopes. Later, the 180-yard, par-3 12th features an elevated tee shot over a gully to a well-guarded green, where a slight miss short or right can find trouble; trust your club choice for the yardage, factoring in the downhill trajectory. Finally, the uphill 520-yard, par-5 15th requires two well-placed shots to set up a short iron into a green that slants back-to-front, demanding an approach that finishes below the hole for the best birdie opportunity.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Ehime Golf Club Main reflect its value-for-money designation, typically ranging from JPY 8,000 to 12,000 on weekdays and JPY 12,000 to 18,000 on weekends and holidays, often including cart. Reservations are generally open to the public and can be made through online portals, including OKCaddie. While walking is not a standard option, electric carts are mandatory and frequently equipped with GPS for yardage assistance. Standard golf attire is expected, meaning collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft-spiked golf shoes are required. Caddies are available upon request for an additional fee, though many golfers opt for self-play.

## Access
Ehime Golf Club Main is situated in Uchiko, Ehime Prefecture. The nearest train station is Uchiko Station on the JR Yosan Line, approximately a 20-minute drive from the clubhouse. For travelers arriving from larger urban centers, the course is accessible by car from Matsuyama City, Ehime's capital, taking approximately 60-75 minutes depending on traffic, covering a distance of about 45-50 kilometers. Ample free parking is available on-site for those driving.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse at Ehime Golf Club Main offers functional and clean facilities, including well-maintained locker rooms suitable for pre-round preparation and post-game refreshment. A traditional Japanese communal bath (daiyokujo) provides a relaxing way to unwind after your round, a common feature at many Japanese courses. The restaurant serves a variety of local Ehime specialties alongside classic Japanese golf course dishes such as katsudon, curry rice, and udon, providing hearty options to refuel.

## Tips
To make the most of your round, consider visiting during the spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November) when the weather is mild, and the course scenery is at its most appealing. Common mistakes include underestimating the elevation changes and not adjusting club selection for uphill or downhill shots, leading to greens missed long or short. Given the tight, tree-lined nature of the fairways, bring extra golf balls, as errant shots are often unrecoverable in the dense forest. Peak season weekends and holidays will see higher green fees and more crowded tee sheets, so book well in advance if playing during these times.
