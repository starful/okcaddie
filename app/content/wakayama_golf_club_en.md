Not the pick if you only chase top-20 Japan ranking prestige—this is practical Kii Peninsula golf.

## Course overview

Expect rolling terrain, tree lines, and bunkers that matter more than pure length. Misses into trees cost angles; keep drives in the short stuff and approach to the fat part of greens. Summer humidity and winter dryness both change how the ball sits on Korai-style fairways.

Products usually include cart options; caddie availability depends on the day. Confirm lunch inclusion—Japanese packages often price “with meal” vs green-fee-only differently.

## Green fees & booking

Treat published portal numbers as **ranges**, not forever-fixed yen:

- **Weekday value products**: often roughly **¥8,000–¥15,000** class depending on inclusions
- **Weekend / holiday**: commonly **¥12,000–¥18,000+**

Start here: [Wakayama Golf Club booking](/booking/wakayama_golf_club_en). Compare two dates (weekday vs Saturday) before you lock lodging. Add rentals when you reserve if your group is traveling light.

## Access

- **Rail**: JR Wakayama Station area + taxi (simplest with bags)
- **Air**: Kansai (KIX) → roughly **60–90 minutes** by car/train+taxi depending on transfers
- From Osaka city hotels, build a full morning buffer—don’t cut it fine for a 7:30 tee

If you are touring the Kii Peninsula temples or coastal drives, schedule golf on a non-transit-heavy day.

## Dress code & tips

- Collared shirt, tailored shorts/pants, soft spikes
