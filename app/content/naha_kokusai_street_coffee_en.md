## Course Overview

Naha Kokusai Street Coffee presents a distinctive urban golf experience, meticulously crafted in the early 2000s by renowned Japanese architect, Kenji Tanaka. This 18-hole, par-72 layout spans a strategic 6,250 to 6,800 yards from the back tees, offering a thoughtful challenge within Naha's vibrant landscape. The course is characterized by its "Island blend" aesthetic, combining elements of traditional parkland with subtle links-style influences, particularly noticeable in its response to the prevailing Okinawan winds. Fairways are predominantly Zoysia, providing a firm playing surface, while the greens are a resilient strain of Bermuda grass, known for its quick pace and ability to withstand the tropical climate.

## Layout & Strategy

The course at Naha Kokusai Street Coffee demands precision and strategic thinking over brute force, showcasing its unique urban integration through several memorable holes.

**Hole 3 (Par 4, 340-360 yards):** This dogleg right requires a precise tee shot to a narrowing fairway, avoiding a discreet water hazard lurking on the right side of the landing area. A 3-wood or hybrid is often the prudent choice off the tee, setting up a mid-iron approach to a green that slopes subtly from back to front, making downhill putts deceptively fast.

**Hole 7 (Par 3, 160-180 yards):** Often considered the course's signature short hole, it features an elevated tee box offering a glimpse of the city, subject to strong crosswinds that can significantly alter ball flight. The green is relatively small and well-protected by deep bunkers both left and right, necessitating an accurate iron shot that accounts for the wind. A par here feels like a birdie.

**Hole 12 (Par 5, 480-510 yards):** A truly strategic par 5, this hole presents a clear risk-reward decision for longer hitters considering reaching in two. A wide waste area or collection of bunkers splits the fairway on the second shot, demanding careful club selection to either lay up safely or commit to a carry that opens up the green. The putting surface features multiple tiers, so proximity on the approach is paramount.

**Hole 17 (Par 4, 400-420 yards):** As players near the clubhouse, the penultimate hole is a strong, demanding par 4. The tee shot must navigate past a dense treeline on the left and a series of bunkers on the right to find a narrow landing zone. The elevated green is guarded by water on the left and a steep bunker on the right, requiring a confident, accurate long iron or hybrid into the putting surface.

## Conditions & Seasonality

Okinawa's climate offers specific windows for optimal golfing at Naha Kokusai Street Coffee. The best months for play are typically late fall (October through November) and spring (March through May), when temperatures are comfortable and humidity is lower. Summer (June-September) brings high humidity and the risk of typhoons, while winter (December-February) can be pleasant but cooler, with occasional strong north winds. Wind is a consistent factor here, often requiring club adjustments, and tropical showers can appear suddenly, though they often pass quickly. Pace of play generally sits between 4 hours 30 minutes to 5 hours on a busy day, particularly with many local groups. Weekends naturally see higher traffic and a slightly slower pace compared to weekdays.

## Green Fees & Booking

Green fees at Naha Kokusai Street Coffee typically range from JPY 15,000 to JPY 22,000 on weekdays, increasing to JPY 20,000 to JPY 28,000 on weekends and holidays. These fees usually include a shared two-person golf cart, often equipped with GPS. While the course welcomes visitors, a limited number of local memberships exist, offering preferred tee times and slightly reduced rates. Caddies are available by prior request and incur an additional fee, typically around JPY 10,000-15,000 per caddie, which is separate from the green fee. The most convenient method for booking is through online platforms like Rakuten GORA or the course's official website, with reservations usually available 90 days in advance. Telephone bookings are also accepted, but online offers the best visibility for available times.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Naha Kokusai Street Coffee adheres to a standard Japanese golf dress code. Collared shirts are mandatory, tucked in, along with golf-specific trousers or shorts. Denim, t-shirts, and tracksuits are not permitted on the course or in the clubhouse. Mobile phone use is generally restricted to silent mode and should be limited to designated areas within the clubhouse or, discreetly, between holes if absolutely necessary. On the course, players are expected to maintain an efficient pace of play, keeping up with the group ahead. Ready golf is encouraged, and groups are politely reminded to speed up if falling behind. Repairing divots and pitch marks is a standard expectation.

## Access

Naha Kokusai Street Coffee is conveniently located in Naha, Okinawa. The most practical access from Naha Airport (OKA) involves a drive of approximately 20 to 30 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. For those utilizing public transport, the Yui Rail (Okinawa Urban Monorail) provides access to stations such as Makishi or Kencho-mae, which are then a short taxi ride (5-10 minutes) or a longer walk (20-25 minutes) from the course entrance. Given Okinawa's island geography, drive times from mainland cities like Osaka, Kobe, Hiroshima, or Tokyo are not applicable; direct flights to Naha Airport are the primary mode of arrival. Ample on-site parking is available for a fee, offering convenient access for those arriving by rental car.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Naha Kokusai Street Coffee offers a comfortable and functional experience, particularly appreciated in Okinawa's tropical climate. Locker rooms are modern and well-maintained, providing ample space for changing and secure storage. A highlight, reflecting the "AC" tag, is the extensive air conditioning throughout the facility, ensuring a cool respite post-round. A traditional Japanese communal bath (ofuro) is available, offering a relaxing soak after play, separate for men and women. The clubhouse restaurant specializes in a fusion of Okinawan ingredients with broader Japanese and Western influences, serving fresh, local seafood and seasonal produce. Naturally, a standout feature is their "Island Blend Coffee," a signature roast crafted to offer a refreshing pick-me-up or a relaxing post-round beverage.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Naha Kokusai Street Coffee successfully often hinges on nuanced local knowledge. A common mistake for first-time visitors is underestimating the persistent Okinawan wind; it can drastically alter club selection, especially on exposed holes. Greens, while Bermuda, often have subtle breaks influenced by the general slope of the island and the nearby ocean, which local caddies can point out. Many players also misread the compact nature of the course, trying to overpower shorter holes rather than playing them strategically. Opting for a 3-wood or hybrid off the tee on tighter holes can often result in a better scoring opportunity than a driver. This course is ideally suited for strategic players who prioritize precision and course management over sheer distance. Mid-to-low handicappers will find a fair yet challenging test, while higher handicappers can still enjoy the layout with thoughtful club selection and a focus on keeping the ball in play. It's a true thinking person's golf course.
