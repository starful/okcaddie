**Introduction:**

Ryukyu Golf Club, located in Nanjo City, Okinawa, is more than just a golf course; it's an experience. It embodies the spirit of Okinawan hospitality and offers a unique blend of challenging golf and breathtaking natural beauty. While not the most opulent course in Japan, it delivers exceptional value for money, particularly considering its stunning coastal setting and impeccable course maintenance. This public-access course is a favorite among locals and tourists alike, known for its welcoming atmosphere and regular tournament play. Ryukyu Golf Club provides a golfing adventure steeped in the island's serene charm.

**Price & Booking:**

Green fees at Ryukyu Golf Club vary depending on the season and day of the week.

*   **Weekdays:** Expect to pay between ¥10,000 and ¥15,000 per person, including cart fee and consumption tax. Prices are typically lower during off-peak seasons (June-July, excluding Golden Week and Obon).
*   **Weekends & Holidays:** Green fees range from ¥15,000 to ¥20,000 per person, similar inclusions.
*   **Seasonal Tips:** Booking in advance, especially during the high season (March-May, September-November), is crucial. Look out for special package deals that may include lunch or onsen access. Playing during twilight hours (afternoon tee times) often comes with discounted rates. Consider visiting during the shoulder seasons for a balance of pleasant weather and lower prices.

Booking can be easily done online through Rakuten GORA, Golf Digest Online, or directly via the Ryukyu Golf Club website (Japanese language only, use a translator). Many hotels in the area also offer golf packages that include tee times at Ryukyu Golf Club.

**Course Accessibility:**

Ryukyu Golf Club is a **public** golf course, welcoming both members and non-members. This accessibility makes it a popular choice for visitors to Okinawa who want to experience golf without the constraints of private club memberships. While priority booking might be given to members during peak times, generally, securing a tee time is relatively straightforward, especially with advance planning. The course also hosts several public tournaments throughout the year, providing opportunities for golfers to test their skills in a competitive environment.

**Course Highlights:**

The course at Ryukyu Golf Club is a testament to strategic design, balancing playability for amateurs with challenges for seasoned golfers. While the original architect information is hard to pinpoint definitively, the current layout reflects a thoughtful integration with the natural terrain.

*   **Strategic Layout:** The 18-hole course is a par 72, measuring approximately 6,800 yards from the back tees. The layout features a combination of gently rolling fairways, strategically placed bunkers, and water hazards that come into play on several holes. The course rewards accuracy and thoughtful club selection, demanding players to consider the wind and elevation changes.

*   **Signature Holes:**
    *   **Hole 8 (Par 3):** This iconic hole is a signature experience. Players tee off from an elevated tee box, overlooking a stunning panoramic view of the Pacific Ocean. The green is guarded by bunkers and the ever-present sea breeze, requiring precise distance control.
    *   **Hole 18 (Par 4):** The closing hole offers a challenging finish. A well-placed drive is essential to avoid the fairway bunkers, leaving a demanding approach shot to a green protected by water on the left. Successfully navigating this hole requires nerve and skill.
    *   **Hole 5 (Par 5):** A long par 5 that doglegs to the left. Big hitters can go for it in two, but risk a watery grave if they go too far left. A well-placed layup will be the best strategy for most.

The course is well-maintained, with consistently smooth greens and lush fairways. The bunkers are strategically placed and filled with fine, white sand. Overall, the course provides a fair and enjoyable test of golf for players of all abilities.

**Facilities:**

Ryukyu Golf Club boasts a range of facilities to enhance the golfing experience:

*   **Clubhouse:** The modern clubhouse offers comfortable seating areas, a pro shop, and a restaurant with stunning ocean views.

*   **Restaurant:** The restaurant serves a variety of Japanese and Western dishes, with a focus on local Okinawan cuisine. Be sure to try the *Goya Champuru* (bitter melon stir-fry) or the *Okinawa Soba*. The restaurant also has a selection of local beers and *Awamori* (Okinawan rice liquor).

*   **Pro Shop:** The pro shop stocks a wide range of golf equipment, apparel, and accessories from leading brands. You can also rent clubs and shoes here.

*   **Onsen/Bath:** A highlight of Ryukyu Golf Club is its excellent onsen (hot spring bath) facilities. After a round of golf, relax and rejuvenate your muscles in the natural hot spring waters. The onsen features both indoor and outdoor baths, offering breathtaking views of the ocean. The quality of the onsen is exceptional, providing a truly authentic Japanese experience. Amenities such as shampoo, conditioner, body wash, and towels are provided.

**Scenic Beauty:**

Ryukyu Golf Club is renowned for its stunning natural setting. The course is perched on a cliff overlooking the Pacific Ocean, offering panoramic views from almost every hole.

*   **Best Season to Visit:** The best time to visit Ryukyu Golf Club is during the spring (March-May) or autumn (September-November) months. The weather during these periods is generally mild and sunny, with comfortable temperatures for golf. Avoid visiting during the rainy season (June-July) or during typhoon season (August-September).
*   **Nature:** The course is surrounded by lush vegetation, including native Okinawan trees and plants. Keep an eye out for local wildlife, such as birds and butterflies.
*   **Surrounding Views:** In addition to the ocean views, you can also see the surrounding hills and forests from the course. The sunsets over the ocean are particularly spectacular.

The scenic beauty of Ryukyu Golf Club adds an extra dimension to the golfing experience, making it a truly memorable destination.

**Access:**

Ryukyu Golf Club is located in Nanjo City, Okinawa, approximately 40 minutes by car from Naha Airport (OKA).

*   **From Naha Airport:** Take the Okinawa Expressway and exit at the Haebaru Minami Interchange. Follow Route 329 south towards Nanjo City. The golf club will be signposted.
*   **By Taxi:** A taxi from Naha Airport will cost approximately ¥5,000-¥6,000.
*   **By Rental Car:** Renting a car is the most convenient way to get to Ryukyu Golf Club, especially if you plan to explore other parts of Okinawa. Several rental car companies operate at Naha Airport.
*   **Public Transportation:** While possible, public transportation is less convenient. Take a bus from Naha Bus Terminal to Nanjo City, and then take a taxi to the golf club.

Its public accessibility, reasonable pricing, and excellent facilities make it a top choice for golfers visiting Okinawa. The course is well-maintained, and the staff is friendly and welcoming. The onsen is a definite highlight, providing a perfect way to relax after a round of golf. For travelers seeking a memorable and affordable golf experience in Okinawa, Ryukyu Golf Club is highly recommended. It's not just a game of golf; it's an immersion into the beauty and serenity of the Okinawan landscape.
