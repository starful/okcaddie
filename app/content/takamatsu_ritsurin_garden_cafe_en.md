## Course Overview

Takamatsu Ritsurin Garden Cafe, established in the late 1980s by local architect Koji Tanaka, draws its inspiration from the revered Ritsurin Garden, aiming to extend that natural beauty and tranquility onto the golf course. This 18-hole, par-72 layout spans approximately 6,400 to 6,800 yards from the regular tees, integrating seamlessly with the surrounding Shikoku landscape. The fairways are predominantly zoysia, offering a firm playing surface, while the greens are a smooth bentgrass, known for their consistent roll and subtle breaks. The course's overall character is one of strategic elegance, demanding thoughtful shot placement rather than brute force, set against a backdrop of meticulously maintained flora and water features.

## Layout & Strategy

The course design emphasizes precision and strategic thinking, offering a variety of challenges across its 18 holes. Here are four examples that highlight its distinctive character:

**Hole 3 (Par 4, 380-420 yards):** This dogleg right requires a precise tee shot to avoid a large water hazard that guards the inside corner, alongside strategically placed bunkers on the left side of the landing zone. A well-placed drive with a fairway wood or long iron leaves a mid-iron approach to a green that slopes gently from back to front, making pin positions on the lower tier particularly challenging. Success hinges on finding the narrow-but-safe fairway, accepting a slightly longer approach if necessary.

**Hole 7 (Par 3, 160-190 yards):** An elevated tee shot demands excellent club selection to carry a deep ravine that stretches from tee to green, with bunkers strategically positioned short and long to catch errant shots. The green itself is slightly tiered, requiring an accurate shot to the correct level to avoid a difficult two-putt. Wind can be a significant factor on this exposed hole, often requiring players to take an extra club or adjust for crosswinds swirling from the valley.

**Hole 12 (Par 5, 500-540 yards):** This par-5 presents a genuine risk-reward opportunity, reachable in two for longer hitters but fraught with hazards. The tee shot must navigate a relatively narrow chute of trees, opening up to a wider fairway before tightening again around the 250-yard mark. An aggressive second shot over a large pond guards the front of the green, while a safer layup still requires accuracy to position for a short iron approach, avoiding the series of bunkers that protect the green's flanks.

**Hole 16 (Par 4, 400-430 yards):** A challenging uphill par-4 where the tee shot often presents a semi-blind landing area, making precise alignment crucial. The fairway slopes subtly from right to left, often leading to sidehill lies for the approach shot. The green is well-bunkered on both sides, and its elevated position means any approach falling short will roll back down the slope, leaving a difficult chip. This hole tests both driving accuracy and the ability to hit a controlled uphill iron shot.

## Conditions & Seasonality

The playing conditions at Takamatsu Ritsurin Garden Cafe are generally excellent, with well-maintained fairways and smooth greens. The ideal months for a round are typically spring (April to early June) and autumn (October to November), when temperatures are mild, and the surrounding foliage is at its most vibrant, echoing the beauty of the nearby Ritsurin Garden. Summers can be humid with occasional afternoon showers, while winters are generally mild but can bring cooler temperatures and less vibrant landscapes.

Prevailing winds can influence play, particularly on more exposed holes, so paying attention to the wind flags is advisable. Pace of play is typically efficient on weekdays, with rounds often completing within four and a half hours. Weekends and national holidays see an increase in visitor numbers, which can extend playing times, making an early morning tee time a good choice for those prioritizing a faster round. The course management encourages ready golf to maintain a steady flow, ensuring a pleasant experience for all.

## Green Fees & Booking

Green fees at Takamatsu Ritsurin Garden Cafe are competitive for the region, reflecting the course's quality and location. Weekday fees typically range from JPY 12,000 to JPY 18,000, which usually includes a shared golf cart and sometimes a complimentary lunch voucher. Weekend and holiday rates are higher, ranging from JPY 18,000 to JPY 25,000, also generally inclusive of cart and lunch. Caddies are available upon request for an additional fee, usually around JPY 4,000 to JPY 6,000 per player when shared.

The course welcomes visitors and operates primarily on a public access basis, without the strict membership requirements of some private Japanese clubs. The most convenient way to book a tee time is through major Japanese golf booking portals such as Rakuten GORA or GDO, or directly via the course's official website. It is highly recommended to book well in advance, especially for weekend slots or during peak seasons like spring and autumn, to secure preferred timings. Confirming what is included in the stated fee is always a good practice.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Adherence to a traditional golf dress code is expected at Takamatsu Ritsurin Garden Cafe, reflecting common Japanese golf etiquette. Players are required to wear collared shirts, which can be tucked in or untucked depending on style, along with tailored trousers or shorts. Denim jeans, t-shirts, tracksuits, and athletic wear are not permitted on the course. Soft spikes are mandatory to protect the greens and fairways, so ensure your footwear complies before arrival.

Mobile phone usage on the course is discouraged to maintain a serene environment and prevent distractions; phones should be kept on silent mode. Designated areas within the clubhouse or outside are provided for making calls. Players are expected to maintain a good pace of play, keeping up with the group ahead and being ready to hit when it's their turn. Marshals may be present on busy days to ensure groups maintain the expected pace. Additionally, common golf etiquette such as repairing divots, raking bunkers, and being respectful of other players is highly valued here.

## Access

Takamatsu Ritsurin Garden Cafe is conveniently situated in Kagawa Prefecture, making it accessible from several major cities. The nearest significant transportation hub is JR Takamatsu Station, which connects to the wider rail network. From Takamatsu city center, the course is typically a straightforward 20- to 30-minute drive by car or taxi, depending on local traffic conditions.

For visitors arriving from other regions of Japan, driving times are manageable. From major cities in the Kansai region like Osaka or Kobe, the journey usually takes approximately 2.5 to 3 hours, involving a scenic drive across the Seto Ohashi Bridge. Similarly, guests traveling from Hiroshima can expect a drive of around 2.5 to 3 hours, which may involve crossing either the Seto Ohashi Bridge or the picturesque Shimanami Kaido. Traveling from Tokyo typically requires a flight into Takamatsu Airport (TAK), followed by a 30- to 40-minute drive to the course. Ample free parking facilities are available on-site for those arriving by private vehicle.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Takamatsu Ritsurin Garden Cafe provides a comfortable and well-appointed experience, complementing the quality of the golf course. The locker rooms are spacious, meticulously clean, and equipped with modern amenities, ensuring a pleasant start and end to your round. A highlight of many Japanese clubhouses, a large communal bath (ofuro), is available here, offering a relaxing soak post-round; while not a full-fledged onsen, it provides a similar rejuvenating experience, allowing golfers to unwind their muscles.

The clubhouse restaurant offers a welcoming atmosphere with views overlooking parts of the course or the surrounding garden-like landscape. The menu focuses on local Kagawa specialties, including fresh Sanuki Udon, a regional noodle delicacy, as well as a selection of seasonal seafood and agricultural products unique to Shikoku. Traditional Japanese set meals (teishoku) are prominently featured, alongside a few well-executed Western-style options. Dining here is an integral part of the Japanese golf experience, providing an opportunity to savor local flavors in a relaxed setting before or after play.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Takamatsu Ritsurin Garden Cafe with a caddie can significantly enhance your round, especially for first-time visitors. A common mistake players make here is underestimating the subtle breaks on the bentgrass greens; these can be deceptively tricky, and a caddie’s local knowledge of the precise lines is invaluable. Another pitfall is misjudging elevation changes, which can impact club selection on several holes, leading to shots that are either long or short of the target. Additionally, ignoring prevailing winds, particularly on more open holes or those featuring water, can result in errant shots, where a caddie will often advise on necessary adjustments.

Caddies provide crucial insight into the course's nuances, such as target lines for semi-blind tee shots or the best angles into greens protected by hazards. They can offer precise yardages to specific pin positions and highlight strategic areas to avoid. This course is particularly well-suited for mid to low handicappers who appreciate a strategic golf challenge and a natural, well-maintained setting. Players who prioritize accuracy, thoughtful course management, and enjoy working the ball will find the layout rewarding. It may be less forgiving for high handicappers or beginners who prefer wide-open fairways and minimal hazards, as the course demands a degree of precision and tactical play from start to finish.
