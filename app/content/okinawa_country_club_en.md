## Course Overview
Okinawa Country Club, established in 1968 and designed by K. Kobayashi, presents an 18-hole, par 72 challenge with total yardage typically ranging from 6,400 to 6,800 yards from the back tees. Fairways are generally surfaced with Zoysia grass, providing a firm playing surface, while the greens feature Bentgrass for consistent putting.
The course is known for its rolling terrain, often presenting significant elevation changes and offering glimpses of the East China Sea. Its design incorporates natural Okinawan topography, demanding thoughtful shot placement and rewarding strategic play over brute force.

## Layout & Strategy
The strategic demands of Okinawa Country Club become evident on several holes, particularly where the terrain and wind play significant roles. The 4th hole, a par 4, requires a precise tee shot over a ridge; the landing area is unseen from the tee, with a collection of bunkers guarding the left side. A long iron or hybrid off the tee can set up a manageable approach, avoiding a potential blocked second shot.

The signature 7th, a par 3, plays downhill towards a green framed by the ocean in the distance. Club selection here is critical due to elevation and prevailing coastal winds, often demanding one less club than the yardage suggests, with subtle breaks towards the sea on the putting surface. Finally, the 15th, a risk-reward par 5, tempts longer hitters to go for the green in two. However, a tight fairway bottleneck and a pond short-left of the green necessitate a cautious lay-up with a mid-iron for those prioritizing par, setting up a wedge approach.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Okinawa Country Club generally range from JPY 10,000 to JPY 16,000 on weekdays, increasing to JPY 15,000 to JPY 22,000 on weekends and public holidays, typically including a shared cart. The course is open to the public, though booking well in advance, especially for weekend play or larger groups, is highly recommended through official channels or local golf tour operators.

Play is predominantly conducted with shared electric carts, which navigate the hilly terrain between holes efficiently. A standard golf dress code applies: collared shirts, tailored trousers or shorts, and soft-spike golf shoes are mandatory. Caddies are available upon request for an additional fee, providing valuable local knowledge of wind conditions and green breaks, and enhancing the overall experience.

## Access
Okinawa Country Club is conveniently located in Nishihara, a short drive from Okinawa's main urban center. It is approximately 20-25 kilometers northeast of Naha Airport and Naha City proper, typically taking around 30-45 minutes by car, depending on traffic conditions. As Okinawa lacks a train network, private car, taxi, or pre-arranged shuttle service remains the primary mode of transportation. The club provides ample free parking for guests arriving by personal vehicle.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse at Okinawa Country Club offers practical amenities expected from a well-established Japanese golf facility. Its locker rooms are clean and functional, providing a comfortable space for pre- and post-round preparations. A spacious communal bath (ofuro) is available, offering a relaxing soak after your game, a common and welcome tradition in Japanese golf clubs. The clubhouse restaurant serves a range of local and Japanese cuisine, with the Okinawa Soba being a popular choice for a hearty post-round meal.

## Tips
To best experience Okinawa Country Club, plan your visit during the spring (March to May) or autumn (October to November) months for the most favorable weather, avoiding the summer typhoon season and cooler winter winds. A common mistake for first-time players is underestimating the effect of the coastal winds on shot trajectory, especially on elevated holes; adjust your club selection accordingly. Always stay hydrated, particularly during warmer periods. Be aware that peak season, including major holidays, will see higher green fees and require bookings made several months in advance.
