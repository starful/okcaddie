Green fees at Towada Highland Golf Club represent excellent value, particularly considering the quality of the course and its stunning location. Weekday green fees typically range from ¥8,000 to ¥12,000, while weekend and holiday rates are slightly higher, falling between ¥12,000 and ¥16,000. These prices usually include the use of the clubhouse facilities, but additional fees may apply for caddie services, golf cart rentals, and equipment hire.

For the best deals, consider visiting during the shoulder seasons – spring (April-May) or autumn (September-October). Not only will you avoid the peak season crowds, but you'll also be treated to spectacular scenery as the landscape bursts into vibrant colors. Booking in advance is highly recommended, especially during peak seasons and weekends. Online booking is available through the official website and several Japanese golf reservation portals, making the process convenient for both domestic and international visitors. Look for package deals that combine green fees with accommodation at nearby hotels or ryokans for an even more cost-effective experience.

**Course Highlights:**

The course at Towada Highland is a masterclass in strategic design, seamlessly blending challenge with natural beauty. While the architect's name may not be a household name, their understanding of the terrain is evident in every hole. The layout is characterized by gently rolling fairways, strategically placed bunkers, and undulating greens that demand precision and thoughtful club selection. Water hazards come into play on several holes, adding to the challenge and visual appeal.

One of the standout holes is the par-3 7th. From an elevated tee box, golfers are presented with a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape, with the green nestled below, guarded by a pond and strategically placed bunkers. The wind can often be a factor on this hole, requiring careful consideration of club selection. Another memorable hole is the par-5 12th, a dogleg right that demands a well-placed tee shot to set up a second shot over a ravine. The green is well-protected, making par a rewarding achievement. The course's overall design encourages thoughtful play and rewards accuracy over brute force, making it enjoyable for golfers of all skill levels.
