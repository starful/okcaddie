## Course Overview

Sample Golf Club, situated in the scenic Hyogo prefecture, presents an 18-hole, par 72 layout. Opened in 1992 and designed by a notable Japanese architect, the course measures approximately 6,700 to 7,000 yards from the back tees. Its fairways are typically zoysia grass, offering firm lies, while the greens are maintained with bentgrass, known for consistent putting surfaces.

The design incorporates significant elevation changes and mature, dense trees that define many of the fairways. Strategic bunkering and occasional water features add complexity, requiring thoughtful shot placement throughout the round. The course provides a balanced test for golfers of varying skill levels, emphasizing accuracy over sheer power.

## Layout & Strategy

Hole 3, a challenging par 4, doglegs sharply left around a rise. Your tee shot needs to carry a dip, ideally favoring the right side of the fairway to avoid a hidden bunker guarding the left corner of the dogleg. An accurate hybrid or a controlled 3-wood off the tee is often the prudent play to set up a mid-iron approach to the multi-tiered green.

The 7th hole is a picturesque mid-length par 3, demanding precision. Water guards the front and right side of the green, with a deep bunker situated to the left. A confident, crisp iron shot aimed at the heart of the green is crucial; the putting surface slopes subtly from back to front, making short putts challenging if above the hole.

On the back nine, Hole 15, a long par 5, offers a genuine risk-reward opportunity. A narrow landing area off the tee requires precision, with a dense tree line penalizing errant drives. Longer hitters might consider going for the green in two, but this requires carrying a stream roughly 100 yards short of a well-guarded green; laying up short of the stream often yields the best chance for a comfortable birdie.

## Practical Info

Green fees typically range from JPY 8,000 to JPY 12,000 on weekdays, and JPY 14,000 to JPY 18,000 on weekends and public holidays, including a mandatory golf cart. As a public course, tee times are generally available for booking online or via phone up to three months in advance.

Self-drive golf carts with GPS are standard, making it easy to navigate the often-hilly terrain. A standard golf dress code applies: collared shirts, no denim, and spikeless golf shoes are recommended. Caddies are available upon request for an additional fee, enhancing the experience with local course knowledge.

## Access

Sample Golf Club is conveniently located approximately 45-60 minutes' drive from central Kobe and 60-75 minutes from central Osaka. The nearest major train station is Sanda Station on the JR Fukuchiyama Line, which is about a 20-minute taxi ride from the course. Ample, complimentary parking is available on-site for those driving.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse at Sample Golf Club provides functional amenities for pre- and post-round comfort. Locker rooms are clean and well-maintained, leading to a spacious communal bath (ofuro) where golfers can relax. The restaurant offers a menu of Japanese and Western fare, with Hyogo-sourced Kuroge Wagyu beef bowls being a popular choice among local patrons.

## Tips

The ideal months to play Sample Golf Club are during the spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November) when the weather is temperate and the surrounding foliage is at its most vibrant. A common mistake here is misjudging elevation changes, so trust your cart's GPS for accurate yardages. Be aware that summer months can bring high humidity and sudden thunderstorms, while winter can have frost delays and cooler temperatures, impacting early morning tee times. Expect peak season pricing and busier tee sheets during Golden Week and autumn color viewing periods.
