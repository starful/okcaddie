## Course Overview

Sample Golf Club, established in 1992, provides a mature and challenging golf experience nestled in the verdant hills of Hyogo Prefecture. Designed by local architect Kenzo Tanaka, the course is known for its thoughtful integration into the natural topography, demanding precision rather than brute strength. It features 18 holes, playing to a par of 72, with a maximum length of approximately 6,850 yards from the back tees, though most visitors will play from the regular tees around 6,300-6,500 yards. The fairways are typically Zoysia grass, providing a firm and sometimes tight lie, while the greens are meticulously maintained Bentgrass, known for their subtle undulations and moderate speed. The overall character of Sample Golf Club is one of strategic engagement, where careful shot placement and course management are rewarded consistently throughout the round.

## Layout & Strategy

The layout at Sample Golf Club presents a series of varied challenges, often requiring players to think ahead. Many holes feature elevation changes, doglegs, and strategically placed hazards that necessitate thoughtful club selection and execution. Understanding the prevailing wind conditions, particularly on elevated tees, can significantly impact shot outcomes. The course design emphasizes navigating the natural contours and tree lines, making it an engaging experience for those who appreciate a tactical approach to the game.

**Hole 3 (Par 4, 395-420 yards):** This demanding par-4 features a slight dogleg to the left, with dense trees flanking both sides of a relatively narrow landing area for the tee shot. A driver might be too much for some, as finding the left rough complicates the approach. Aiming for the right-center of the fairway with a 3-wood or hybrid leaves a longer but clearer second shot. The green is protected by a bunker on the front right and slopes gently from back to front, making an approach shot from the left side of the fairway more forgiving.

**Hole 7 (Par 3, 160-185 yards):** A picturesque yet intimidating par-3, this hole plays slightly downhill to a green guarded by a large pond on the left and front, and a deep bunker on the right. Club selection is crucial here; consider taking one less club due to the downhill trajectory, but ensure you carry the water. The green is wider than it is deep, presenting a smaller target if the pin is tucked in the back. A conservative play might be aiming for the right half of the green, even if it leaves a longer putt.

**Hole 11 (Par 5, 510-545 yards):** The longest hole on the course, this par-5 offers a genuine risk-reward opportunity. The tee shot plays uphill, and a successful drive will land on a plateau. From there, the fairway narrows considerably as it bends right, with out-of-bounds lurking on the right side and dense trees on the left. Laying up with an iron or hybrid to a wider section of the fairway before the bend is a sensible strategy, setting up a mid-iron approach for your third shot. For those attempting to reach in two, a precise second shot must avoid the bottleneck and a small pot bunker near the green's entrance.

**Hole 16 (Par 4, 350-375 yards):** This short but tricky par-4 demands accuracy from the tee. The fairway slopes significantly from right to left, and a large bunker guards the left side of the landing zone. A tee shot aimed slightly right of center, or even a long iron/hybrid, can prevent running into the trouble. The approach shot is typically uphill to a two-tiered green that is well-protected by bunkers on both sides. Understanding the pin position on this green is vital for a good result, as being on the wrong tier can lead to a challenging two-putt.

## Conditions & Seasonality

Sample Golf Club generally maintains good playing conditions throughout the year, with particular highlights during spring and autumn. The best months for play are typically April to May and October to early November, when temperatures are mild, and the foliage provides a vibrant backdrop. During these periods, the Zoysia fairways are lush, and the Bentgrass greens roll true and at a moderate pace. Summer months (June to September) can be hot and humid, with the possibility of heavy rainfall or even typhoons, which might lead to temporary course closures or wet conditions. Winters are generally mild in Hyogo, allowing play, though frost delays can occur in the mornings from December to February, and the course might have temporary mats on tees.

Wind is usually a factor on more exposed holes, especially those with elevation, though the tree-lined nature of many fairways offers some protection. Pace of play is a significant consideration, particularly on weekends and holidays. Expect rounds to take approximately 4.5 to 5 hours, and it's important for groups to maintain a good pace to avoid backups. Weekdays tend to be less crowded, offering a more relaxed experience and often quicker rounds, particularly in the afternoon. Early morning tee times, regardless of the day, are always the busiest.

## Green Fees & Booking

Green fees at Sample Golf Club are structured to reflect its public status and location, offering reasonable value for the quality of play. Weekday rounds, including a shared electric cart, typically range from JPY 8,000 to JPY 14,000 per person. Weekend and holiday rates are higher, usually falling between JPY 12,000 and JPY 20,000 per person, again with a shared cart included. These prices can fluctuate based on the season, time of day, and any ongoing promotional packages. As a public course, Sample Golf Club readily welcomes visitors, and there are no membership restrictions on booking tee times.

Caddies are an optional add-on and generally need to be requested in advance, often for a group rather than per individual. If available, a caddie will add approximately JPY 8,000 to JPY 12,000 per person to the cost, usually for a group of 3-4 players. The most common and convenient way for international visitors to book tee times is through major Japanese online golf reservation platforms such as Rakuten GORA or GDO (Golf Digest Online). These sites offer English interfaces and allow you to view available times, compare prices, and make secure bookings. Direct phone bookings are also possible, but may require Japanese language proficiency. It is strongly advisable to book weekend and holiday rounds several weeks in advance, especially during peak seasons, as popular times fill up quickly.

## Dress Code & On-Course Rules

Adherence to established golf etiquette and dress codes is expected at Sample Golf Club, consistent with general Japanese golf course standards. For attire, a collared golf shirt is mandatory for both men and women, paired with tailored trousers or golf shorts. Denim jeans, tracksuits, t-shirts, and sleeveless tops for men are not permitted on the course or in the clubhouse. Soft-spiked golf shoes are required; metal spikes are prohibited. Appropriate golf headwear is encouraged for sun protection.

On the course, maintaining a brisk pace of play is paramount. Be ready to hit when it is your turn, limit time spent searching for lost balls, and move quickly between shots. Marshals may intervene if groups fall significantly behind schedule. Mobile phone use is generally discouraged on the course, and it is best to keep phones on silent mode within the clubhouse. Essential on-course rules include repairing divots and pitch marks, raking bunkers thoroughly, and generally leaving the course in the condition you found it. Respect for other players and the course staff contributes to an enjoyable experience for everyone.

## Access

Sample Golf Club is conveniently situated in Hyogo Prefecture, making it accessible from several major cities in the Kansai region. The nearest significant railway station is Himeji Station, approximately a 30-40 minute drive away. From Osaka, the drive to Sample Golf Club typically takes around 60 to 90 minutes, depending on traffic conditions and your starting point within the city. If traveling from Kobe, expect a journey of approximately 45 to 75 minutes. While technically possible to travel from Hiroshima (2.5-3 hours by bullet train to Himeji, then drive) or Tokyo (3-4 hours by bullet train to Himeji, then drive), these are less practical for a single day trip and are better suited for those planning a multi-day Kansai golf itinerary.

For those driving, ample free parking is available at the clubhouse. Visitors relying on public transport should arrange for a taxi from Himeji Station, or inquire with the club about potential shuttle services, which sometimes operate with prior booking for specific trains or times. It is advisable to confirm shuttle availability and schedule directly with the club when booking your tee time, especially if you are not renting a car. The final approach to the course involves some winding country roads, typical of Japanese courses nestled in scenic areas.

## Clubhouse & Dining

The clubhouse at Sample Golf Club offers a comprehensive range of amenities designed for comfort and convenience before and after your round. The locker rooms are spacious, clean, and well-maintained, providing individual lockers and changing areas. A highlight for many visitors is the traditional Japanese communal bath facility. Separate for men and women, these large baths (often referred to as *ofuro* or *onsen* style, even if not natural hot springs) are an integral part of the Japanese golf experience, perfect for relaxing muscles after a round. Fresh towels, toiletries, and amenities are always provided.

The club restaurant serves a selection of classic Japanese and Western-style dishes, with a focus on hearty and satisfying meals that are popular with golfers. The lunch menu typically features staples such as Japanese curry rice, katsudon (pork cutlet bowl), various noodle dishes (soba and udon), and local Hyogo specialties, often using seasonal ingredients. Breakfast is usually available for early tee times, offering lighter options. While not a gourmet dining destination, the restaurant consistently provides well-prepared, flavorful meals in a comfortable setting, making it an excellent spot for a post-round meal with your playing partners. There is often a well-stocked pro shop for essentials, apparel, and souvenirs.

## Caddie Tips

Playing Sample Golf Club with local knowledge can significantly enhance your experience and score. One common mistake visitors make is underestimating the subtle yet significant undulations on the greens; they often break more than they appear, particularly across the grain. Pay close attention to the local grass growth patterns, which can influence putts. Another pitfall is being overly aggressive on the dogleg holes; many tee shots call for strategic clubbing down to ensure you stay in play and have a clear approach. The course's tree lines are dense, making recovery shots from off-fairway difficult.

Local caddies, if utilized, are invaluable for their insights into pin positions, invisible hazards, and optimal landing areas on blind shots. They can guide you on the best lines for tee shots on doglegs and provide precise yardages to carry bunkers or clear water. They often know the subtle wind patterns that funnel through certain valleys or affect elevated tees. Staying hydrated, especially during warmer months, is also a key tip, as the hilly terrain can add to the physical exertion. Sample Golf Club particularly suits mid-to-high handicappers who appreciate a strategic challenge, rewarding precision and course management over raw power. Low handicappers will find ample opportunities to test their shot-making skills and course navigation, making it a well-rounded challenge for various playing styles.
