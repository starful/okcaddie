Its unique charm lies not only in the golfing itself but also in the holistic experience it offers – a blend of sporting challenge, relaxation, and natural beauty.

**Price & Booking:**

The cost to play at Izu Ohito Country Club reflects its premium status. Weekday green fees typically range from ¥18,000 to ¥25,000 per person, while weekend and holiday rates can climb to ¥28,000 to ¥35,000 or higher. These prices generally include green fees, caddie fees (mandatory), and usage of the clubhouse facilities. Prices fluctuate depending on the season, with peak seasons (spring and autumn) commanding the highest rates. Shoulder seasons (early summer and late winter) may offer slightly lower prices and fewer crowds.

For booking, advance reservations are essential, especially during peak season. The most convenient way to secure a tee time is through the club's official website or through reputable Japanese golf booking platforms such as Rakuten GORA or GDO (Golf Digest Online). These platforms often offer package deals, including accommodation at nearby hotels, making it a seamless "Stay & Play" experience. Booking well in advance (at least 2-3 months) is highly recommended, particularly if you are traveling during a popular time. It's also advisable to inquire about seasonal promotions or weekday discounts when making your reservation. Be aware of any specific dress code requirements prior to arrival, as adherence to golfing etiquette is highly valued.

Izu Ohito Country Club operates as a semi-private club. While membership exists, the course also welcomes non-member guests, making it accessible to visitors. However, access for non-members is typically subject to availability and may require booking through a member or through a "Stay & Play" package offered by partnered hotels. Calling the club directly to inquire about non-member availability is recommended, especially if online booking options are limited. Securing a tee time as a non-member often involves a slightly higher green fee compared to the member rate. The welcoming approach towards visitors, combined with the exclusivity of a semi-private club, creates a unique and appealing atmosphere for golfers seeking a special experience.

**Course Highlights:**

Izu Ohito Country Club boasts a strategically laid out 18-hole championship course that challenges golfers of all skill levels. The architect skillfully incorporated the natural terrain into the design, resulting in a visually stunning and intellectually stimulating round. The course features undulating fairways, strategically placed bunkers, and challenging water hazards that demand careful shot selection and precise execution. Each hole presents a unique set of challenges, requiring golfers to think their way around the course.

One of the signature holes, often the par-3 17th, showcases the course's beauty and difficulty. This hole typically requires a tee shot over a valley to a well-protected green, demanding accuracy and distance control. The elevated tee provides breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, adding to the dramatic experience. Another memorable hole might be a long par-5 with a dogleg, requiring a well-placed drive and strategic lay-up shots to avoid the bunkers guarding the green. The meticulous maintenance of the course, with its perfectly manicured greens and lush fairways, further enhances the overall golfing experience. Izu Ohito is a course where strategic thinking and skillful execution are rewarded.

**Facilities:**

The clubhouse at Izu Ohito Country Club epitomizes luxury and comfort. Upon arrival, guests are greeted by attentive staff who provide exceptional service. The clubhouse features spacious locker rooms, a well-stocked pro shop, and a variety of dining options.

The restaurant offers a diverse menu featuring both Japanese and Western cuisine, with an emphasis on fresh, local ingredients. After a challenging round of golf, guests can relax and rejuvenate their muscles in the soothing mineral-rich waters. The combination of world-class golfing and traditional Japanese relaxation makes Izu Ohito Country Club a truly unique destination.

**Access:**

Reaching Izu Ohito Country Club is relatively straightforward, though it requires some travel from major transportation hubs. The most convenient option is typically to travel to Shuzenji Station, a major station on the Izu Hakone Railway Line. Shuzenji Station is accessible from Tokyo via the Tokaido Shinkansen (bullet train) to Mishima Station, followed by a transfer to the Izu Hakone Railway Line. The total travel time from Tokyo is approximately 2-3 hours.

From Shuzenji Station, the club is approximately a 20-30 minute taxi ride. Alternatively, you can arrange for a shuttle service through the club or your hotel. Renting a car is also a viable option, providing greater flexibility for exploring the surrounding Izu Peninsula. Driving from Tokyo takes approximately 2-3 hours, depending on traffic conditions. The drive itself is scenic, offering glimpses of the coastline and the mountains. Detailed directions are readily available on the club's website or through online mapping services.
