**Price & Booking:**

Expect Miyazaki Country Club to be priced within the higher echelon of Japanese golf courses. Weekday rates for non-members typically range from ¥35,000 to ¥50,000 (approximately $230-$330 USD), while weekend rates can climb to ¥50,000 to ¥70,000 (approximately $330-$470 USD) or even higher, depending on the season and availability. Green fees typically include caddie fees (mandatory at Miyazaki Country Club) and consumption tax.

Seasonal Tip: Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) offer the most pleasant weather for golfing in Miyazaki, but these are also peak seasons, so booking well in advance is crucial. Summer (June-August) can be hot and humid, and typhoons are a possibility. Winter (December-February) is relatively mild compared to northern Japan, but temperatures can still drop, and the wind can be a factor. Prices may fluctuate based on seasonal demand.

Booking is generally required well in advance, especially for weekends and holidays. Direct bookings can be made through the club, but this is often challenging for non-Japanese speakers. A recommended approach is to utilize a reputable luxury travel concierge service specializing in Japanese golf experiences. These services can handle all aspects of your booking, including securing tee times, arranging accommodations, and providing language assistance. Some high-end hotels in Miyazaki may also offer booking services for their guests.

**Course Accessibility:**

Miyazaki Country Club primarily functions as a semi-private club. While membership is highly valued and provides priority access, the club does welcome a limited number of non-member guests, particularly those staying at partnered hotels or arranged through exclusive travel agencies. Tee times for non-members are typically more restricted than for members, so early booking is essential. The level of accessibility can vary depending on the time of year and the overall occupancy of the course. Contacting the club directly or utilizing a concierge service well in advance of your intended visit is strongly advised to ascertain availability and booking procedures.

The overall design encourages players to think their way around the course, rewarding accuracy and strategic decision-making over brute force.

**Access:**

Reaching Miyazaki Country Club requires careful planning, particularly if you are traveling from outside of Japan. The most convenient airport is Miyazaki Airport (KMI), which offers domestic flights from major cities like Tokyo (Haneda and Narita), Osaka, and Fukuoka. From Miyazaki Airport, the club is approximately a 45-minute to 1-hour drive by car or taxi. Rental cars are readily available at the airport.

Alternatively, you can take the JR Limited Express train from Miyazaki Station to a closer station and then take a taxi to the club, but this option is generally less convenient than driving. Detailed directions from the airport or nearest train station can be obtained from the club or your hotel concierge. It's advisable to have directions written in Japanese, as many taxi drivers may not speak English fluently. Pre-arranged airport transfers are often available through luxury hotels or travel agencies.
