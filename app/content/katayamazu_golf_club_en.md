Katayamazu Golf Club in Kaga, Ishikawa is a **Sea of Japan coast classic**—famous among domestic golfers, with visitor products that do appear on major portals when calendars open. Plan for wind, onsen-town logistics, and weekend demand.

## Quick facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Kaga, Ishikawa Prefecture |
| **Style** | Coastal championship / classic Japanese |
| **Turf** | Korai fairways, bent greens (typical) |
| **Visitor access** | Often bookable via portals; peak dates competitive |
| **Best months** | May–June, Sep–Oct (winter can be snow / closure risk) |
| **Best for** | Serious players combining golf + Kaga onsen |

## Who it's for

- Golfers who want a **name Hokuriku course** with real strategic teeth
- Travelers pairing rounds with **Katayamazu / Yamashiro onsen** stays
- Mid-to-low handicappers comfortable managing coastal wind

Skip if you only want a soft resort scramble—Katayamazu rewards accuracy and patience more than bomb-and-gouge.

## Course overview

The layout uses coastal terrain, tree lines, and bunkering to push you into **smart tee shots**. Sea of Japan weather can turn a calm morning into a half-club longer afternoon. Play center-green on exposed approaches; short-siding bent greens is expensive.

Expect full Japanese day structure: practice area if open, cart play per product, clubhouse meal, baths. Tournament history means conditioning is usually a point of pride—still verify live green speeds and wet-weather cart rules when you check in.

## Green fees & booking

Hokuriku premier rates move with season and package. **Planning ranges** for visitors:

- **Weekday**: often roughly high-¥10,000s to mid-¥20,000s
- **Weekend / holiday**: commonly ¥20,000s+ when lunch and extras stack

**Booking steps**

1. [Katayamazu booking](/booking/katayamazu_golf_club_en)
2. Compare weekday vs weekend inclusions
3. Reserve rentals and confirm English-friendly confirmation emails if you need them

Winter closures and snow can wipe plans—build a backup date if traveling December–February.

## Access

- **Airport**: Komatsu (KMQ) → about **30–50 minutes** by car to Kaga / Katayamazu area
- **Rail**: Kaga Onsen Station + taxi (bags are easier by taxi than local buses)
- From Kanazawa: roughly **45–70 minutes** by car depending on traffic

Stay in Kaga onsen towns to cut morning stress. Related stay-and-play mindset: [Onsen after golf](/guide/onsen-after-golf).

## Dress code & tips

- Standard Japanese dress: collared shirt, tailored bottoms, soft spikes
- Windproof layer; winter gloves if shoulder season

## Bottom line

Katayamazu is a **serious Kaga coast day**: book early for weekends, plan fees in the ¥20,000s band for busy dates, fly into KMQ, and let the wind—not ego—pick your clubs.
