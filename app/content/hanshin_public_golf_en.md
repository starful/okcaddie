## Course Overview

Hanshin Public Golf is an 18-hole, par-72 course stretching approximately 5,900 to 6,200 yards, designed for enjoyable, accessible play. Its primary characteristic is its flat, riverside setting, which introduces consistent wind as a natural defense. Fairways are typically Zoysia, offering a firm lie, while greens are usually Bentgrass, maintained for a moderate pace.

## Layout & Strategy

The 4th hole, a par-3 measuring around 160 yards, often plays into a crosswind, requiring precise club selection to avoid the river left and bunkers right. Aim for the center of the green and be prepared for subtle breaks on your putt towards the water. The 7th hole, a par-4 of about 380 yards, demands a strategic tee shot to a landing area protected by trees on both sides. A long iron or utility off the tee can set up a mid-iron approach to a slightly elevated green, where putts tend to run faster from back to front.

The 15th hole, a par-5 stretching over 500 yards, presents a double dogleg that rewards accuracy over raw power. The optimal play involves two well-placed layups to avoid flanking water hazards, leaving a short iron into a reasonably sized green. Don't be tempted to cut corners, as the risks significantly outweigh the potential rewards on this design.

## Practical Info

Green fees at Hanshin Public Golf typically range from JPY 6,000-9,000 on weekdays and JPY 8,000-12,000 on weekends and holidays, offering excellent value. This is a public course, so reservations are open to all golfers, usually booked online or by phone up to two months in advance.

Most rounds here are walking, though pull carts are available, and a limited number of electric carts can sometimes be reserved for an additional fee. The dress code is standard golf attire; collared shirts and soft spikes are required, with no denim permitted on the course. Caddie services are generally not available, reflecting its public, value-focused model.

## Access

Hanshin Public Golf benefits from its "Easy Access" tag, located just a short distance from major transit hubs. The nearest train station is Amagasaki Station, approximately a 15-20 minute taxi ride from the course.

From central Osaka, expect a drive of around 30-45 minutes depending on traffic, covering a distance of about 15-20 kilometers. On-site parking is ample and free for golfers, making it convenient for those arriving by car.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse at Hanshin Public Golf provides essential amenities suitable for a public course, maintaining a clean and functional environment. Locker rooms are well-maintained, and a communal bath (ofuro) offers a chance to relax after your round, a common and welcome feature in Japanese golf clubs. The restaurant serves a straightforward menu of Japanese comfort food, with local specialties like a hearty katsu-don or udon noodles often highlighted as popular choices for a post-round meal.

## Tips

For the best playing conditions, plan your visit during the spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November) when temperatures are mild and the course is at its peak. A common mistake here is underestimating the persistent river wind, which can significantly alter club selection, especially on approach shots. Always confirm wind direction and strength before committing to a shot.

Summer months bring heat and humidity, while winter can be chilly and damp, though the course remains open year-round. While Hanshin offers good value, expect slightly higher green fees during peak holiday periods and weekends, so consider weekday play for optimal pricing.
