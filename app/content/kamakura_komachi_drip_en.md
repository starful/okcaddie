## Course Overview

Kamakura Komachi Drip presents an 18-hole, par 72 layout, spanning approximately 6,700 to 7,100 yards from the back tees. Opened in the early 1980s and designed by local architect Kenji Tanaka, the course integrates seamlessly with the region's natural undulations. Fairways are typically Zoysia, offering a firm lie, while greens are maintained with Bentgrass for a consistent putting surface. Signature characteristics include elevation changes, views toward the historic Kamakura area, and traditional Japanese garden aesthetics incorporated into the landscaping.

## Layout & Strategy

The course design demands thoughtful shot placement and rewards strategic play over sheer power. Consider the par-4 5th hole, a dogleg right with an elevated tee shot; a hidden bunker guards the right side of the fairway's landing zone, so aim for the left-center to open up the green. The par-3 12th hole often plays into the prevailing wind and features a significant water hazard short of the green, necessitating a precise long iron or hybrid to avoid a penalty stroke. Finally, the par-5 17th presents an uphill tee shot followed by a downhill approach to a narrow green, where a layup short of the final hazard offers a safer wedge approach to finish.

## Practical Info

Weekday green fees typically range from JPY 15,000 to JPY 22,000, while weekend and holiday rates are higher, ranging between JPY 25,000 and JPY 35,000. Public reservations are accepted and highly recommended, especially for weekend play; online booking via the course's official portal is the most efficient method. Play is generally conducted with GPS-equipped carts, though walking with a mandatory caddie is an option for those seeking a more traditional experience. The dress code requires collared shirts, golf-appropriate trousers or shorts, and spikeless golf shoes.

## Access

Kamakura Komachi Drip is conveniently accessible from major urban centers. The nearest train station is Kamakura Station, which is a short taxi ride from the course. For those driving, the journey from Tokyo Station typically takes between 60 and 90 minutes, depending on traffic conditions. Ample on-site parking facilities are provided for visitors arriving by car.

## Clubhouse Notes

The clubhouse provides a well-appointed experience, featuring spacious locker rooms and a large communal bath (ofuro) perfect for relaxation after a round. The restaurant offers a menu focused on regional Japanese cuisine, including local seafood specialties and seasonal produce. A particular highlight is often a matcha-infused dessert, a nod to the area's cultural ties, providing a refreshing end to the meal.

## Tips

The most pleasant golfing conditions at Kamakura Komachi Drip are typically found during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild and the course foliage is vibrant. Common strategic errors include underestimating the subtle elevation changes and misjudging the breaks on the Bentgrass greens. Be aware of the humid conditions during the summer months (July-August) and the potential for typhoons in late summer or early autumn, which can impact play. Expect peak season pricing and limited tee time availability during the cherry blossom season in spring and the peak of autumn foliage.
