## Course Overview
Seto Inland Sea Golf, situated in Kasaoka, Okayama, presents an 18-hole, par 72 championship layout. The course plays around 6,800-7,100 yards from the back tees, offering a substantial test of skill and strategy. Characterized by its seaside links design, the course features bentgrass greens and Zoysia fairways, well-suited to the coastal climate. Elevated tee boxes provide expansive vistas of the Seto Inland Sea, an iconic feature of the region.

## Layout & Strategy
The course design demands careful shot-making and consideration of prevailing winds. The 3rd hole, a challenging par 4, requires a precise tee shot to a landing area that doglegs left, with the Seto Sea lurking beyond the left rough; a 230-250 yard shot with a controlled hybrid or fairway wood is often ideal here. The par 3 7th hole, typically playing around 180-200 yards, is fully exposed to the elements and often plays to a semi-island green, necessitating a confident long iron or hybrid to avoid the surrounding hazards. On the par 5 15th, longer hitters might contemplate reaching the green in two, but a strategically placed pond guards the front left approach, making a lay-up to the right fairway for a precise wedge shot a safer option for most.

## Practical Info
Green fees at Seto Inland Sea Golf generally range from ¥15,000 to ¥25,000 on weekdays, increasing to ¥25,000 to ¥40,000 on weekends and holidays. Reservations are typically required well in advance, especially for weekend play, though the course is publicly accessible. Play is primarily by riding cart, often equipped with GPS, and caddies are available as an optional extra for an additional fee. A smart casual dress code is enforced, including collared shirts and trousers or golf shorts, with soft spikes mandatory.

## Access
Seto Inland Sea Golf is conveniently located from major transport hubs. The nearest train station is Kasaoka Station on the JR Sanyo Line, approximately a 15-minute drive from the course. From Okayama City, expect a drive of about 45-60 minutes via the Sanyo Expressway. Ample on-site parking is available at the clubhouse for those traveling by car.

## Clubhouse Notes
The clubhouse provides comprehensive amenities for golfers. Spacious locker rooms offer individual changing facilities, leading to well-appointed communal baths (ofuro) where players can relax after their round, often with views of the surrounding landscape. The clubhouse restaurant specializes in local Setouchi seafood, with options such as fresh sashimi platters and the regional specialty, a Kasaoka-style ramen, providing satisfying post-game meals.

## Tips
For the most favorable conditions, plan your visit during spring (April-May) or autumn (October-November) when temperatures are mild and the skies are often clear, offering prime views. A common mistake is misjudging the wind direction and strength, which can vary significantly across holes; consult with your caddie if opting for one. Be prepared for potential fog, particularly in the mornings, and the summer months can bring high humidity and typhoon risks. Peak season pricing applies during these popular periods, so booking early is advisable.
