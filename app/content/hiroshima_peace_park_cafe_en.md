## Course Overview

The Hiroshima Peace Park Cafe offers a truly unique and contemplative short-game experience, rather than a traditional 18-hole golf course. It features a series of informal, self-guided putting and delicate chip shot challenges seamlessly integrated into the serene landscape of the Peace Memorial Park. There is no official par or set number of "holes," allowing for a flexible and reflective approach to play.

This distinctive "course" has evolved organically since the cafe's establishment, reflecting the peaceful surroundings rather than a specific golf architect's vision. Its informal origins trace back to the mid-20th century, with recent enhancements focused on providing a respectful recreational activity. The playing surfaces consist of well-maintained natural turf, blending effortlessly with the park's manicured lawns and garden areas. Greens are meticulously kept grass patches, offering subtle breaks and rewarding a gentle touch.

## Layout & Strategy

Navigating the informal layout requires precision, respect for the environment, and a focus on subtle shot-making. Players will find themselves presented with various short-game scenarios, emphasizing touch around the park's natural undulations.

Consider the "Memorial Approach," a delicate approximately 50-70 yard shot from a slightly elevated position towards a designated putting area, with the A-Bomb Dome subtly visible in the distance. The strategy here demands a soft, high-lofted wedge to drop the ball gently onto the green, avoiding any monuments or sensitive areas. Putting lines on this segment can be deceptively tricky, often breaking away from the slight central rise of the park.

Another engaging challenge is the "Children's Monument Putt," a short, intricate putting scenario around the Children's Peace Monument. This "hole" emphasizes reading very subtle ground undulations and the pace required for a slow, true roll, all while maintaining a quiet reverence. A perfectly weighted putt is paramount here, as the surface can influence the ball more than expected.

The "River Bend Chip" presents a short chip shot across a natural depression or towards the Motoyasu River bank, perhaps a 20-30 yard challenge. Success hinges on a high, soft landing with a 58-60 degree wedge to stop the ball quickly. Putting lines often require accounting for a gentle slope towards the river, making pace control crucial for avoiding an over-run.

## Practical Info

A nominal green fee, typically a few hundred JPY, is usually requested at the Hiroshima Peace Park Cafe. This contribution supports park maintenance and is sometimes bundled with a refreshment purchase from the cafe. Reservations are not required for individual play; the experience operates on a flexible, first-come, first-served basis, welcoming spontaneous engagement.

The entire "course" is walking-only, encouraging a leisurely pace suitable for the park setting and fostering a sense of peaceful contemplation. A relaxed yet respectful dress code is appropriate, reflecting the somber and significant nature of the park. While golf attire is welcome, comfortable casual wear that allows for freedom of movement is generally expected.

Caddies are not available for this informal experience, as players typically manage their own equipment. Simple putting and chipping sets are often available for rent directly from the cafe for those without their own.

## Access

The Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park, home to the cafe and its unique "course," is exceptionally well-connected within Hiroshima city. It is easily accessible from Hiroshima Station, with multiple tram lines offering convenient direct routes. Tram lines 2 and 6 are particularly useful, disembarking directly at the Genbaku Dome-mae or Heiwa Kinen Koen stops, a short walk from the cafe.

From central Hiroshima, a drive to the park typically takes 10-15 minutes depending on traffic conditions. However, public transportation is highly recommended due to the historical area's popularity and limited public parking availability. Should you choose to drive, public parking facilities are located nearby, though spaces can be scarce during peak tourist hours and holidays.

## Clubhouse Notes

The Hiroshima Peace Park Cafe itself serves as the informal "clubhouse," providing a quiet, reflective space before or after engaging with the park's challenges. While not offering traditional locker rooms or an onsen, basic, clean restroom facilities are available for visitors. The cafe is renowned for its selection of artisanal coffees, local teas, and traditional Japanese pastries, making it a perfect spot for light refreshments.

## Tips

The best months to experience the Hiroshima Peace Park Cafe's unique challenges are during spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November). These seasons offer pleasant temperatures, vibrant foliage, and comfortable walking conditions. A common mistake is underestimating the subtle undulations and reading pace on the natural turf, so practice a gentle, consistent stroke. While peak season pricing is not applicable, as fees remain consistent, be aware that park crowds can be larger during major Japanese holidays and school breaks.
